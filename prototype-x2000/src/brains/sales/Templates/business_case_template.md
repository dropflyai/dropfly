# Business Case Template — Quantified Justification for Investment

## Purpose

This template provides a structured framework for building a buyer-ready business
case that quantifies the economic impact of your solution. A business case is not
a product brochure — it is a financial argument that enables the economic buyer
to justify the investment to their stakeholders, board, or CFO.

The business case should be co-developed with the buyer. Assumptions should be
theirs (or validated by them), not yours. A business case built by the seller
alone is marketing. A business case validated by the buyer is a decision tool.

### ROI Calculation

```
Year 1:
  Total Benefits:     $[amount]
  Total Investment:   $[amount]
  Net Benefit:        $[amount]
  ROI:                ([Benefits - Investment] / Investment) x 100 = [X]%

3-Year:
  Total Benefits:     $[amount]
  Total Investment:   $[amount]
  Net Benefit:        $[amount]
  ROI:                [X]%

Payback Period:
  Monthly Benefit:    $[amount] / 12 = $[monthly]
  Payback:            $[Year 1 Investment] / $[monthly benefit] = [X] months
```

### NPV Calculation (for Finance Teams)

```
Discount Rate: [8-12%, per buyer's WACC or hurdle rate]

Year 0 (Investment):  -$[amount]
Year 1 (Net Benefit):  $[amount] / (1 + r)^1 = $[PV]
Year 2 (Net Benefit):  $[amount] / (1 + r)^2 = $[PV]
Year 3 (Net Benefit):  $[amount] / (1 + r)^3 = $[PV]

NPV = Sum of PVs = $[amount]
```
