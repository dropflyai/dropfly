## Call Execution Framework

### Opening (5 Minutes)

**Up-Front Contract (Sandler):**

"[Name], thank you for taking the time today. Before we jump in, let me suggest
an agenda to make sure we use your time well:

1. I would love to learn about [their company/team] and understand what is
   happening that made this conversation worth having.
2. If it seems relevant, I will share how we have helped similar companies
   address these challenges.
3. At the end, we will decide together whether it makes sense to continue
   the conversation — and if so, what the logical next step would be.

Does that work for you? Is there anything you would like to add to the agenda?"

**Rapport (2 minutes, maximum):**
_Keep this brief and genuine. Reference something specific from research:_

"I noticed [specific detail from LinkedIn/news]. How has that been going?"

---

### Situation Context (10 Minutes)

**Goal:** Establish baseline understanding of their environment. Do NOT over-invest
here — questions should fill gaps your research could not cover.

**Questions:**

"Help me understand your current setup:
- How does your team currently handle [relevant process]?"
- "What tools or systems are you using today for [function]?"
- "How is your team structured? Who is involved in [process]?"
- "What does the workflow look like from [start] to [end]?"

### Problem Discovery (15-20 Minutes)

**Goal:** Surface explicit pain, quantify business impact, and uncover emotional
dimension. This is the most valuable segment of the call.

**Problem Questions (SPIN):**

"Where does this process break down or create friction?"
"What is the biggest challenge your team faces with [process]?"
"How reliable is your current approach? Where does it fall short?"
"When things go wrong, what does that look like?"

**Notes:**

**Implication Questions (SPIN — Follow Every Pain Statement):**

"When that happens, what is the downstream impact?"
"How does this affect [their team's ability to hit goals]?"
"What does this cost your organization — in time, money, or risk?"
"How long has this been going on? Has it gotten worse?"
"What happens if nothing changes in the next 12 months?"

**Goal:** Co-create the desired future state. Let the buyer describe what success
looks like in their own words.

"What would it mean for your team if [pain] were eliminated?"
"If you had [capability], how would that change your approach to [goal]?"
"What would success look like in 6 months if we solved this?"

### Solution Bridge (5 Minutes — Brief, Not a Full Demo)

**Goal:** Connect your capabilities to their stated pain. This is NOT a demo. It is
a 2-3 minute bridge between their problem and your solution.

"Based on what you have shared, here is where I see a fit:

[Pain 1] → We address this through [capability], which has helped [similar company]
achieve [specific result].

[Pain 2] → Our approach to this is [brief explanation], and customers typically
see [metric improvement].

I do not want to go into a full product overview today — I would rather do that
with the right people in the room and tailored to your specific situation."

### Next Steps (5 Minutes — Critical)

**Goal:** Secure a specific, date-bound, attendee-confirmed next step.

"Based on our conversation, it sounds like there is a real fit worth exploring.
Here is what I would suggest as a next step:

[Proposed next step — demo, technical deep dive, stakeholder meeting, POC scoping]

Who else should be in that meeting? Specifically:
- Who on the technical side should evaluate this?
- Who would need to approve the budget?
- Anyone else who would be affected by this decision?

Can we find a time in the next [timeframe]? I will send a calendar invite with
a tailored agenda."
