# Investor Update Template — Monthly Communication

## Instructions

Send this update to all investors on the same day each month (e.g., 15th of
the following month, after close). Keep to one page / 5-minute read maximum.
BCC all investors to prevent reply-all. Send even in bad months -- silence
creates more anxiety than bad news. Customize the template with your actual
metrics and narrative. Bold the single most important thing in the update.

---

## Subject Line

```
[Company Name] — [Month Year] Investor Update
```

## Formatting Guidelines

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|----------|
| Length | 1 page maximum (5-minute read) |
| Metrics table | Include every month for consistency |
| Wins | 2-3 bullets, quantified |
| Challenges | 2-3 bullets, with action plans |
| Asks | 1-3 specific, actionable requests |
| Tone | Professional, honest, concise |
| Sending | BCC all investors, same day each month |
| Subject | Consistent format: "[Company] -- [Month] Investor Update" |

## What to Include in Bad Months

```
When metrics are down:
  - Lead with the bad news (do not bury it)
  - Explain root cause (what happened)
  - Share your plan (what you are doing about it)
  - Quantify the impact (how bad is it, really?)
  - Maintain perspective (one bad month is not a trend)

Example:
  "MRR declined 2% this month due to 3 enterprise churns ($45K MRR total).
  Two were acquired (out of our control); one cited competitive alternative.
  We have implemented quarterly business reviews for all $10K+ accounts
  and are increasing CSM coverage in enterprise. Current pipeline for
  next month shows strong recovery."

What NOT to do:
  - Skip the update (investors assume the worst)
  - Blame external factors without an action plan
  - Hide the numbers (trust is destroyed, not built)
  - Over-explain (keep it concise)
```

## Tracking Investor Engagement

```
Track after each update:
  Investor       | Responded | Quality    | Score
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
  [Lead VC]      | Yes       | Actionable | +3
  [Angel 1]      | Yes       | Encouraging| +1
  [Angel 2]      | No        |            | 0
  [VC Partner 2] | Yes       | Offered intro | +3

Engagement scoring:
  +3: Provided warm intro or actionable help
  +2: Responded with specific feedback
  +1: Responded with encouragement
   0: No response
  -1: Unresponsive to direct request

Use scores to:
  - Identify your most engaged investors (ask more of them)
  - Identify disengaged investors (reduce detail)
  - Plan follow-on round participation
```

## Annual Summary Version

```
In December/January, send an annual summary:

Subject: [Company] — [Year] Annual Review & [Next Year] Plan

Additional sections:
  - Year in review (key milestones, metrics progression)
  - Annual metrics comparison (this year vs last year)
  - Key learnings from the year
  - Plan for next year (strategic priorities, financial targets)
  - Team growth and key hires
  - Thank you to investors for specific help they provided
```

## Pre-Send Checklist

- [ ] All metrics verified and reconciled
- [ ] Wins are specific and quantified
- [ ] Challenges include action plans
- [ ] Asks are specific (name, company, reason)
- [ ] Tone reviewed (honest but confident)
- [ ] Length is under 1 page
- [ ] Subject line follows standard format
- [ ] BCC list is current (add new investors, remove departed)
- [ ] Sent on the regular cadence date
