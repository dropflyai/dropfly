# Finance Brain -- Patterns Index

## Overview

Patterns are proven, repeatable approaches to common financial operations and
strategic finance challenges. They encode institutional knowledge from production
finance work. Every pattern follows the same structure: context, problem, solution,
implementation, trade-offs, and references. Consult patterns before starting
any major financial workstream.

---

## Available Patterns

### Fundraising Round Pattern
**File**: `fundraising_round_pattern.md`
**Context**: Executing a venture fundraising round from preparation to close.
**When to use**: Any fundraising process (seed through growth). Covers preparation,
investor targeting, process management, negotiation, and closing.

### Financial Close Pattern
**File**: `financial_close_pattern.md`
**Context**: Executing the monthly financial close process.
**When to use**: Every month-end. Covers the close calendar, reconciliation
procedures, variance analysis, and board reporting.

### Budget Planning Pattern
**File**: `budget_planning_pattern.md`
**Context**: Building the annual operating budget.
**When to use**: Annual planning cycle (typically Q4 for next fiscal year).
Covers top-down targets, bottom-up departmental builds, scenario modeling,
and board approval.

## How to Use Patterns

1. **Before starting work**: check if a pattern exists for your workstream
2. **During work**: follow the pattern's phases and checklists
3. **After work**: if the pattern was insufficient, update it with learnings
4. **New patterns**: propose via the Finance Brain update process

## Pattern Structure

Every pattern document must include:
- **Context**: when and why to use this pattern
- **Problem Statement**: what specific problem it solves
- **Solution Architecture**: the approach with process flows
- **Implementation Steps**: phased, actionable steps
- **Trade-offs**: what you gain and what you sacrifice
- **Anti-patterns**: what to avoid
- **Checklist**: verification steps
- **References**: sources and further reading
