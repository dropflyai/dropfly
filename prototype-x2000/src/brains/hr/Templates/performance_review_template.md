# Performance Review Template

## Purpose

This template provides the standard format for documenting annual (or periodic) performance reviews. It captures goal attainment, competency demonstration, development progress, and the overall performance rating. Every performance review must be documented using this format to ensure consistency, fairness, legal defensibility, and actionable development feedback.

---

## Template Usage Instructions

1. Employee completes the self-assessment section first
2. Manager completes the manager assessment section independently (before reviewing self-assessment in detail)
3. Manager reviews self-assessment and adjusts own assessment as appropriate
4. Manager submits provisional rating for calibration
5. After calibration, manager conducts the review conversation with the employee
6. Both parties sign to acknowledge the review took place (signature does not indicate agreement)
7. File in performance management system

## Review Quality Checklist

Before submitting, the manager must verify:

- [ ] All goals from the beginning of the period are addressed
- [ ] Ratings are supported by specific behavioral evidence (not general impressions)
- [ ] Feedback covers the full review period (not just the last 1-2 months)
- [ ] Both strengths AND development areas are addressed
- [ ] Narrative is factual, specific, and professional (no emotional or subjective language)
- [ ] Development plan is actionable and forward-looking
- [ ] Rating is consistent with the evidence presented
- [ ] No surprises: Any significant feedback in this review has been previously shared with the employee during the review period
- [ ] Review has been calibrated with peer managers
- [ ] DEIB consideration: Would I provide this same feedback and rating to any employee with this performance, regardless of demographic characteristics?

## Documentation Standards

**What to include:**
- Specific examples with dates ("In the Q3 product launch on September 15, you...")
- Measurable outcomes ("Reduced deployment time from 4 hours to 45 minutes")
- Behavioral observations ("In team meetings, you consistently...")
- Impact statements ("This resulted in a 15% improvement in...")

**What NOT to include:**
- Vague statements ("Great job this year" — too vague to be useful)
- Personal characteristics ("You have a great personality" — not job-related)
- Protected characteristic references ("For someone your age, you're very adaptable" — discriminatory)
- Hearsay without investigation ("I've heard that you..." — verify before including)
- Predictions without evidence ("I don't think you'll succeed at the next level" — must be based on demonstrated behavior)
- Comparisons to specific other employees ("Unlike [Name], you..." — creates interpersonal conflict)

---

## Legal Considerations

- Performance reviews are discoverable in litigation. Write every review as if a jury will read it.
- Inconsistency between positive reviews and subsequent termination creates strong inference of pretext.
- Ratings that skew by demographic group may evidence discrimination.
- Employee comments on the review become part of the legal record.
- Retain performance reviews per the organization's record retention schedule (typically duration of employment + 7 years).
