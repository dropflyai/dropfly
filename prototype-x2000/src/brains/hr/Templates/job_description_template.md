# Job Description Template

## Purpose

This template provides the standard format for all job descriptions. Every job posting must follow this structure to ensure consistency, legal compliance, inclusivity, and effectiveness. Job descriptions serve multiple purposes: they attract candidates, define the role for internal stakeholders, establish essential functions for ADA compliance, and provide the foundation for performance evaluation.

---

## Template Usage Instructions

1. Complete all sections marked with `[brackets]`
2. Review for inclusive language (use Textio or Gender Decoder if available)
3. Verify FLSA classification with compensation team
4. Confirm salary range with compensation team
5. Have HRBP review before posting
6. Post internally for minimum 5 business days before (or concurrent with) external posting

## Template Quality Checklist

Before posting, verify:

- [ ] **Title**: Standard title from job architecture (not inflated or creative)
- [ ] **Essential functions**: 5-8 specific, measurable responsibilities
- [ ] **Requirements**: Limited to genuine must-haves (no degree requirement unless truly necessary)
- [ ] **Inclusive language**: Reviewed for gendered, ableist, or exclusionary language
- [ ] **Salary range**: Included (legally required in many jurisdictions; recommended for all)
- [ ] **FLSA classification**: Confirmed with compensation team
- [ ] **EEO statement**: Included
- [ ] **Accommodation statement**: Included
- [ ] **Process transparency**: Interview process and timeline described
- [ ] **No excessive requirements**: Total required qualifications < 10 items
- [ ] **Years of experience**: Not used as sole or primary qualification (age discrimination risk)
- [ ] **Degree requirement**: Removed if skills-based alternative exists
- [ ] **Spelling and grammar**: Reviewed and error-free

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Is a Problem | Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| "10+ years of experience required" | Correlates with age; after 5 years, experience has near-zero correlation with performance | "Significant experience with [specific skill]" |
| "Bachelor's degree required" | Screens out qualified candidates from non-traditional backgrounds; may have disparate impact | "Bachelor's degree or equivalent experience" or remove if not genuinely required |
| "Ninja," "Rockstar," "Guru" | Gendered language; research shows these terms discourage women and non-binary applicants | Use standard professional titles |
| "Fast-paced environment" | Often code for "we expect you to work unsustainable hours" | "Dynamic environment where you will have significant impact" |
| "Culture fit" | Often means "similar to current employees" — perpetuates homogeneity | "Culture add" — what unique perspectives and skills will you bring? |
| 20+ requirement bullet points | Research shows women apply when meeting 100% of requirements; men apply at 60%. Long lists disproportionately discourage diverse applicants | Keep requirements to 5-7 genuine must-haves |
| No salary range | Candidates self-select out or waste time in misaligned processes; legal requirement in many jurisdictions | Always include salary range |
| "Other duties as assigned" (without essential functions) | Does not meet ADA essential function documentation requirements | List essential functions clearly; "other duties" is supplementary only |

## Accessibility Requirements for Job Postings

- Posted on ADA-accessible career site (screen reader compatible, keyboard navigable)
- Available in plain text format upon request
- Font size readable; sufficient color contrast
- Application form accessible (no CAPTCHA that blocks assistive technology)
- Alternative application method available for candidates who cannot complete the online form

---

## Job Description Maintenance

- Review all active job descriptions annually for accuracy and compliance
- Update when: role responsibilities change significantly, compensation bands are adjusted, legal requirements change, or organizational structure changes
- Archive (do not delete) outdated job descriptions for compliance and historical reference

---

## Legal References

- ADA: Essential functions must be documented prior to advertising the position
- Title VII: Job requirements must be job-related and consistent with business necessity
- ADEA: Age-proxy requirements (years of experience, graduation dates) create litigation risk
- EEOC Uniform Guidelines: Selection criteria must be validated if they produce adverse impact
- State pay transparency laws: Salary range disclosure requirements vary by jurisdiction
