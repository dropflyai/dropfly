# Offer Letter Template

## Purpose

This template provides the standard format for employment offer letters. The offer letter is both a legal document and a candidate experience touchpoint. It must be precise, complete, and welcoming. Every offer letter must be reviewed by the compensation team (for range compliance) and HR (for legal compliance) before delivery.

---

## Template Usage Instructions

1. Complete all fields marked with `[brackets]`
2. Verify compensation within approved range
3. Confirm benefits eligibility date
4. Have compensation team and HRBP review
5. Send digitally via e-signature platform (DocuSign, HelloSign, etc.)
6. Set expiration date (typically 5-7 business days)
7. Retain signed copy in employee file

---

## Offer Letter Quality Checklist

Before sending, verify:

- [ ] **Title and level** match approved requisition
- [ ] **Base salary** within approved compensation range
- [ ] **Bonus target** matches level/role standard
- [ ] **Equity grant** within approved range for level
- [ ] **Start date** confirmed with hiring manager and candidate
- [ ] **Work location** accurately reflects arrangement (remote, hybrid, office)
- [ ] **Benefits eligibility date** accurate per plan terms
- [ ] **At-will statement** included (for applicable jurisdictions)
- [ ] **Contingencies** appropriate (background check, I-9, drug screen if applicable)
- [ ] **Signing bonus clawback** terms clear (if applicable)
- [ ] **CIIAA reference** included
- [ ] **Non-compete/non-solicit** reviewed by counsel for enforceability in employee's state
- [ ] **Expiration date** set (5-7 business days)
- [ ] **Spelling and accuracy** of candidate name, title, compensation amounts
- [ ] **Compensation team** approved
- [ ] **HRBP** reviewed
- [ ] **E-signature** platform ready

## Common Offer Letter Mistakes

| Mistake | Risk | Prevention |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Promising specific employment duration | Creates implied contract; undermines at-will | Always include at-will statement; never promise "permanent" employment |
| Including salary history reference | May violate salary history ban laws | Never reference prior salary in the offer letter |
| Incorrect FLSA classification | Overtime liability if misclassified | Compensation team verifies before offer |
| Missing contingency language | Cannot withdraw offer if background check fails | Always include contingency section |
| Verbal promises not in writing | Creates implied contract risk | Everything material must be in the written offer |
| Offering benefits not yet available | Employee expectation mismatch; potential breach | Verify benefit eligibility with benefits team before including |
| Non-compete in restricted state | Unenforceable; creates litigation risk; bad candidate experience | Legal review for employee's specific jurisdiction |

## Post-Offer Process

1. **Verbal offer call**: Recruiter or hiring manager delivers verbal offer before sending letter
2. **Offer letter sent**: Via e-signature platform within 24 hours of verbal acceptance
3. **Negotiation**: If candidate negotiates, follow negotiation framework in `02_talent_acquisition/hiring_process.md`
4. **Signed letter received**: File in HRIS / employee record
5. **Contingencies initiated**: Background check, drug screen (if applicable)
6. **Pre-boarding begins**: Welcome email, equipment, system access

---

## Legal References

- At-will employment: Applicable in all states except Montana (see `01_foundations/employment_law_foundations.md`)
- FCRA: Background check disclosure and authorization requirements
- IRCA: I-9 verification requirements and timeline
- State-specific: Pay transparency, salary history bans, non-compete restrictions vary by jurisdiction
- OWBPA: If offer includes release of claims for 40+ candidates (rare in new hire offers; common in separation)
