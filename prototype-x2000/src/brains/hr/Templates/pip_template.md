# Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Template

## Purpose

This template provides the standard format for Performance Improvement Plans. A PIP is a formal, structured process for addressing specific, documented performance deficiencies. It provides the employee with clear expectations, measurable goals, defined support, and a specific timeframe for improvement. A PIP is NOT a pre-termination formality -- it is a genuine effort to help the employee succeed. If the decision to terminate has already been made, do not use a PIP; proceed with a respectful separation per the progressive discipline process.

---

## Template Usage Instructions

1. Before initiating a PIP, the manager must consult with the HRBP
2. HRBP reviews for consistency (are similarly-situated employees treated the same?)
3. HRBP reviews for legal risk (protected activity, protected class, recent complaints)
4. Legal counsel review recommended if employee is in a protected class, has recently engaged in protected activity, or if the situation has elevated litigation risk
5. Manager and HRBP present the PIP to the employee together
6. Employee receives a copy; original filed in the employee's personnel record
7. All meetings and check-ins during the PIP are documented in writing

### Section 6: Consequences

**Successful completion:**
If you meet or exceed all of the expectations outlined in this plan by the end of the PIP period, the PIP will be closed and you will return to the standard performance management process. Your performance will continue to be monitored, and sustained improvement is expected.

**Partial improvement:**
If you demonstrate meaningful but incomplete improvement, the PIP may be extended for one additional period (maximum one extension). An extension is not guaranteed and is at the company's discretion.

**Insufficient improvement:**
If you do not meet the expectations outlined in this plan within the specified timeframe, additional action will be taken, up to and including termination of your employment.

## PIP Quality Checklist

Before presenting to the employee:

- [ ] All deficiencies are specific, documented with dates and examples
- [ ] Prior feedback has been given before PIP initiation (no surprises)
- [ ] Expectations are measurable and objectively evaluable
- [ ] Support provided is genuine and adequate
- [ ] Timeline is reasonable for the type and severity of the issues
- [ ] Similarly-situated employees have been treated consistently
- [ ] HRBP has reviewed and approved
- [ ] Legal counsel has reviewed (if elevated risk)
- [ ] No protected activity or protected class concerns that suggest retaliation or discrimination
- [ ] The manager genuinely believes improvement is possible (if not, this should be a separation, not a PIP)

---

## Legal Considerations

- PIPs are discoverable in litigation. Every word must be defensible.
- A PIP followed by termination will be scrutinized for whether the PIP was genuine or pretextual.
- Inconsistent use of PIPs (some employees PIPed for behavior that others are not PIPed for) creates disparate treatment claims.
- If the employee recently filed a complaint, requested accommodation, or took protected leave, consult counsel before initiating the PIP.
- Document the PIP conversation itself: who was present, what was said, employee's reaction.
- Retain PIP documents per record retention schedule (duration of employment + 7 years minimum).
