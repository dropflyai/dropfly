# Performance Review Pattern -- Annual Review Cycle Playbook

## Context

This pattern applies when executing the annual (or semi-annual) performance review cycle. It covers the full cycle from goal-setting through calibration, rating finalization, compensation linkage, and post-review follow-up. This pattern assumes a performance enablement model with continuous feedback augmented by formal review checkpoints.

---

## Problem

Performance reviews are the most dreaded process in HR -- by managers, employees, and HR alike. Yet when executed well, they are the primary mechanism for ensuring accountability, fairness, and development. The core challenges are: rating inflation (most employees rated above average), manager inconsistency (ratings vary more by manager than by employee performance), recency bias (evaluating only the last month), and disconnection from development (reviews feel like judgment, not growth).

---

## Forces

- **Fairness**: Employees expect equitable, consistent evaluation
- **Development**: Reviews should drive growth, not just judge past performance
- **Compensation**: Ratings inform merit increases and bonus; creates high-stakes pressure
- **Manager capability**: Most managers are poorly trained as evaluators
- **Legal defensibility**: Documented performance evaluations are the primary defense in termination claims
- **Employee experience**: The process should not be demoralizing or performative
- **Time investment**: Reviews are time-intensive; the investment must produce proportional value

---

## Solution: The Performance Review Cycle

### Phase 1: Goal Setting (Q1, January-February)

**Step 1.1: Company and team OKR cascade**
- Leadership sets company-level OKRs for the year
- Department and team leaders cascade to team-level OKRs
- Individual goals are set in alignment with team OKRs

**Step 1.2: Individual goal setting**
- Employee drafts 3-5 goals (OKR or SMART format per `04_performance/performance_management.md`)
- Goals cover:
  - Results/output goals (60-70% weight): What will you deliver?
  - Development goals (30-40% weight): How will you grow?
- Manager reviews, refines, and approves goals
- Goals documented in performance management system
- Deadline: February 28

**Step 1.3: Development plan**
- Employee creates or updates IDP per `04_performance/career_development.md`
- Manager provides input on development priorities
- Both agree on development actions for the year (70-20-10 model)
- IDP documented alongside goals

**DEIB checkpoint**: Are goals set equitably? Are stretch goals distributed fairly across the team, not disproportionately assigned to underrepresented employees?

### Phase 2: Mid-Year Check-In (Q3, July-August)

**Step 2.1: Self-reflection**
- Employee completes mid-year self-assessment:
  - Progress against each goal (on track, at risk, completed, deprioritized)
  - Key accomplishments since last review
  - Challenges encountered and support needed
  - IDP progress
- Written self-assessment submitted to manager

**Step 2.2: Manager assessment**
- Manager reviews self-assessment and adds perspective
- Manager completes mid-year feedback (not a formal rating; directional assessment)
- Manager identifies any goals that need recalibration due to business changes

**Step 2.3: Mid-year conversation**
- 45-60 minute meeting between manager and employee
- Review goal progress and adjust as needed
- Discuss development plan progress
- Identify support needed for H2
- Address any emerging performance concerns early
- Document outcomes and action items

**Critical rule**: If there are performance concerns, they MUST be raised at mid-year. Surprising an employee with a negative year-end review that was not foreshadowed at mid-year is a management failure.

### Phase 3: Year-End Review (Q4, October-December)

**Step 3.1: Self-assessment**
- Employee completes comprehensive self-assessment:
  - Results against each goal (with evidence and metrics)
  - Key accomplishments and impact
  - Competency demonstration (behaviors, not just results)
  - Development progress and learning
  - Areas for improvement acknowledged
- Submission deadline: Early November

**Step 3.2: Peer feedback collection (optional but recommended)**
- Manager solicits feedback from 3-5 peers, cross-functional partners, or stakeholders
- Structured questions aligned to competencies
- Anonymous aggregation (minimum 3 respondents for anonymity)
- Input informs manager assessment; not directly shared with employee as raw data

**Step 3.3: Manager assessment**
- Manager drafts assessment addressing:
  - Goal attainment (evidence-based evaluation of each goal)
  - Competency demonstration (behavioral evidence at appropriate level)
  - Development progress
  - Overall impact and contribution
  - Provisional rating (1-5 scale per `04_performance/performance_management.md`)
- Manager references full year of 1:1 notes, project outcomes, and peer feedback
- Assessment draft completed before calibration

**Step 3.4: Calibration**
- Cross-manager calibration sessions per `04_performance/performance_management.md`
- HRBP facilitates; department head has final authority
- Rating distribution reviewed for consistency and bias
- Demographic analysis conducted (gender, race, age by rating)
- Ratings finalized post-calibration

**Step 3.5: Manager-employee review conversation**
- 60-minute meeting (minimum) in a private setting
- Manager shares the calibrated rating and assessment
- Focus on evidence and impact, not labels
- Employee shares perspective and any disagreements
- Forward-looking: Discuss development priorities for next year
- Employee can submit written response if they disagree with the rating

**DEIB checkpoint**: Run post-calibration analysis of rating distribution by demographic group. If any group's average rating differs by >0.3 points from the overall average, investigate for potential bias.

### Phase 4: Compensation Linkage (Q1, January-February)

**Step 4.1: Merit increase allocation**
- Compensation team translates ratings into merit increase guidelines
- Managers allocate merit increases within budget and guidelines
- HR reviews for equity: Are similarly-rated employees receiving comparable increases?
- Pay equity check: Does the merit allocation widen or narrow existing pay gaps?

**Step 4.2: Bonus calculation**
- Company performance modifier determined
- Individual bonus calculated: Base x target % x company modifier x individual modifier
- Bonus amounts approved by department head and compensation team

**Step 4.3: Promotion decisions**
- Promotion candidates identified through calibration
- Promotion cases prepared per `04_performance/career_development.md`
- Promotion increases calculated (10-15%, to appropriate position in new band)
- Promotions approved by skip-level and compensation team

**Step 4.4: Compensation communication**
- Compensation letters prepared (base increase, bonus, equity refresh, total comp)
- Manager delivers in private 1:1 (separate from performance conversation)
- Manager prepared with talking points and comparison data
- Total compensation statements distributed per `03_compensation/benefits_design.md`

**Critical rule**: The performance conversation (focused on development) and the compensation conversation (focused on pay) should be separated by at least 2-4 weeks. Combining them causes employees to focus only on the number, not the feedback.

### Phase 5: Follow-Up and Cycle Reset (Q1, February-March)

**Step 5.1: New goal setting**
- The cycle resets with new goal setting for the coming year
- Goals informed by prior year review, development plan, and business priorities

**Step 5.2: Process evaluation**
- HR evaluates the review cycle effectiveness:
  - Completion rates (target: 100% within deadline)
  - Rating distribution analysis
  - Demographic equity analysis
  - Employee and manager satisfaction surveys
  - Process improvement feedback

**Step 5.3: System and process updates**
- Incorporate feedback into next cycle design
- Update competency models if roles have evolved
- Refresh training materials for managers
- Update performance management system configuration if needed

---

## Consequences

**Expected positive outcomes:**
- Consistent, calibrated performance evaluation across the organization
- Clear documentation supporting compensation, promotion, and development decisions
- Equitable treatment verified through demographic analysis
- Development-oriented conversations that drive growth
- Legal defensibility for all performance-related decisions

**Risks and mitigations:**
- Manager procrastination: Automated reminders; manager accountability metrics; leadership visibility
- Rating inflation: Calibration sessions with distribution guidelines; data on historical patterns
- Recency bias: Mid-year check-in provides half-year anchor; year-round documentation in 1:1 notes
- Employee disengagement with process: Communicate the "why"; share how feedback leads to action; simplify where possible

## Success Criteria

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| On-time completion (reviews finalized by deadline) | 95%+ |
| Rating distribution within guidelines | <10% deviation from guideline |
| Demographic rating equity (avg rating gap between groups) | <0.3 points |
| Employee satisfaction with review process | >3.5/5.0 |
| Manager satisfaction with review process | >3.5/5.0 |
| Goal completion rate (goals rated "achieved" or "exceeded") | 60-80% |
| Merit increase equity (comparable increase for comparable rating) | <1% gap |
