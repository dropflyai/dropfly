# HR Brain Patterns -- Reusable Playbooks

## Purpose

Patterns are documented, reusable playbooks for recurring HR processes and scenarios. They encode institutional learning so that the HR Brain applies proven approaches consistently rather than reinventing solutions for known problems. Every pattern has been validated through experience, research, or both.

---

## 1. What Is a Pattern?

A pattern is a structured solution to a recurring HR problem. It includes:

- **Context**: When does this pattern apply? What triggers it?
- **Problem**: What challenge or decision must be addressed?
- **Forces**: What competing concerns create tension?
- **Solution**: The proven approach, with step-by-step guidance
- **Consequences**: Expected outcomes, risks, and trade-offs
- **Related modules**: Which HR Brain modules govern this pattern?

Patterns are not rigid scripts. They are frameworks that must be adapted to specific organizational context while preserving the core principles.

## 3. Pattern Quality Standards

Every pattern must meet these quality standards:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| **Evidence-based** | Grounded in research from HR Brain modules or documented organizational learning |
| **Complete** | Covers the full process from trigger to completion, including edge cases |
| **Actionable** | Provides specific, executable steps (not vague guidance) |
| **Measurable** | Includes success criteria and metrics to evaluate effectiveness |
| **Compliant** | Accounts for legal requirements and references relevant compliance modules |
| **DEIB-aware** | Includes equity checks and bias mitigation at every decision point |
| **Documented** | All steps produce documentation sufficient for legal defense and organizational learning |

## 4. Creating New Patterns

When a recurring HR scenario is identified that does not have an existing pattern:

1. **Document the scenario**: What triggered it? What decisions were made? What was the outcome?
2. **Extract the generalizable approach**: Remove situation-specific details; identify the reusable process
3. **Validate against HR Brain modules**: Ensure the pattern complies with all relevant module guidance
4. **Peer review**: Have another HR professional review the pattern for completeness and accuracy
5. **Add to this directory**: Create a new `.md` file following the standard pattern structure
6. **Update this README**: Add the new pattern to the Available Patterns section
7. **Log in Memory**: Record the learning in the HR Brain Memory directory

---

## 5. Pattern Maintenance

- **Annual review**: All patterns reviewed annually for accuracy, compliance updates, and improvement opportunities
- **Post-incident update**: After any significant deviation from a pattern (successful or not), update the pattern with lessons learned
- **Compliance check**: After any employment law change that affects a pattern, update immediately
- **Version tracking**: Document the date and nature of each pattern update

---

## 6. Pattern Usage Protocol

When the HR Brain encounters a situation covered by a pattern:

1. **Identify the applicable pattern** from this directory
2. **Read the full pattern** before beginning execution
3. **Adapt to context** (organization size, industry, jurisdiction, specific circumstances)
4. **Execute the steps** in order, documenting each step as specified
5. **Flag deviations** from the pattern and document the rationale for deviation
6. **Evaluate outcomes** against the pattern's success criteria
7. **Feedback loop**: If the pattern was insufficient or could be improved, update it

## 7. Anti-Patterns

Anti-patterns are approaches that seem reasonable but consistently produce poor outcomes. When an anti-pattern is identified, document it in the relevant pattern file as a "What NOT to do" section. Common HR anti-patterns include:

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Skipping structured interviews because "we know a good candidate when we see one" | Validity drops from .51 to .20; bias increases | Always use structured interviews with rubrics |
| Promoting top individual contributors to management without development | 60% of new managers fail in first 24 months (CEB) | Management readiness assessment + development before promotion |
| Conducting engagement surveys without action plans | Decreases future participation by 10-15% per cycle; increases cynicism | Never survey without a committed action planning process |
| Using PIPs as pre-termination paperwork | Employees and managers view PIPs as performative; legal risk if PIP is not genuine | Only PIP when genuine improvement is the goal; if decision is made, proceed with respectful separation |
| Matching external offers to retain employees | Teaches employees to threaten departure; does not address underlying issues | Proactive retention through career development, engagement, and fair compensation |

## 8. Cross-Brain Pattern Integration

Some patterns require collaboration with other specialist brains:

| Pattern | Engineering Brain | Design Brain | MBA Brain |
|---------|:---:|:---:|:---:|

| Performance Review | Performance system configuration | Review UX design | Performance-comp linkage |

When a pattern requires cross-brain collaboration, the HR Brain initiates the request and coordinates the workflow.
