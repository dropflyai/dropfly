# Hiring Pattern -- End-to-End Hiring Playbook

## Context

This pattern applies whenever the organization needs to fill a role, whether backfill, new headcount, or restructured position. It covers the complete process from business need identification through the new hire's first day handoff to the onboarding process.

---

## Problem

Hiring is the highest-leverage people decision. A great hire compounds value over years; a bad hire costs 50-200% of annual salary to correct (SHRM). Yet most organizations execute hiring inconsistently, with fragmented ownership, unclear criteria, and unstructured evaluation. This pattern ensures every hire follows a rigorous, evidence-based, legally defensible process.

---

## Forces

- **Speed**: Hiring managers want positions filled immediately; candidates lose interest if the process drags
- **Quality**: Thorough evaluation takes time; rushing degrades selection accuracy
- **Fairness**: Every candidate deserves equitable treatment; bias enters through unstructured processes
- **Compliance**: Employment law governs every aspect of hiring; violations create liability
- **Candidate experience**: Top candidates evaluate the employer during the process; poor experience loses talent
- **Cost**: Recruiting is expensive; inefficient processes waste resources

---

## Solution: The Hiring Process

### Phase 1: Requisition and Planning (Days 1-5)

**Step 1.1: Business case validation**
- Why does this role exist? What business problem does it solve?
- What is the cost of not filling this role? (Revenue impact, productivity loss, team burden)
- Has the role been approved in the headcount plan? If not, submit business case.
- Is this truly a new hire, or could the work be absorbed, automated, or contracted?

**Step 1.2: Job definition**
- Review or create job description per `02_talent_acquisition/hiring_process.md` standards
- Confirm job level using job architecture and level descriptors
- Identify 4-6 core competencies from the competency model
- Define must-have vs. nice-to-have qualifications (challenge inflated requirements)
- Determine FLSA classification (exempt/non-exempt) per `01_foundations/employment_law_foundations.md`

**Step 1.3: Compensation approval**
- Pull market data for the role per `03_compensation/compensation_strategy.md`
- Set salary range (minimum-midpoint-maximum)
- Determine equity grant range (if applicable) per `03_compensation/equity_compensation.md`
- Obtain compensation approval from finance/comp team
- Include salary range in job posting (per applicable pay transparency laws)

**Step 1.4: Interview process design**
- Assign competencies to interview stages
- Select interviewers (diverse panel; trained; no competency gaps in coverage)
- Create or select behavioral interview questions per `02_talent_acquisition/interview_design.md`
- Prepare scorecards with anchored rating scales
- Confirm interview format: in-person, virtual, or hybrid

**Step 1.5: Requisition intake meeting**
- Recruiter + hiring manager meet (30-60 minutes)
- Align on role, candidate profile, process, timeline, and sourcing strategy
- Document agreements in ATS requisition notes
- Set weekly sync cadence for duration of search

**DEIB checkpoint**: Is the job description inclusive? Are requirements truly necessary? Is the interview panel diverse? Is the sourcing strategy designed to reach underrepresented talent?

### Phase 2: Sourcing and Screening (Days 5-20)

**Step 2.1: Post and source**
- Post internally (5-day minimum internal posting period)
- Post externally on job boards, LinkedIn, and diversity-focused channels
- Activate employee referral network
- Begin direct sourcing for passive candidates (if senior/specialized role)
- Track source attribution for every candidate

**Step 2.2: Application screening**
- Screen applications against must-have criteria only (not nice-to-have)
- Apply screening criteria consistently to every application
- Blind resume review if feasible (remove name, photo, university)
- Disposition non-qualified candidates with appropriate reason code
- SLA: Screen within 5 business days of application

**Step 2.3: Phone screen**
- Recruiter conducts 30-minute phone screen
- Standard screening questions (role interest, compensation alignment, logistics, 3-4 targeted questions)
- Document responses in ATS
- Advance or decline within 3 business days

**Step 2.4: Hiring manager screen**
- 45-minute conversation focused on role depth and 2-3 competencies
- Hiring manager completes scorecard within 24 hours
- Decision to advance or decline within 2 business days

**DEIB checkpoint**: Review pipeline diversity at this stage. If pipeline does not reflect market availability for underrepresented groups, extend sourcing before proceeding.

### Phase 3: Assessment and Interview (Days 20-35)

**Step 3.1: Skills assessment (role-dependent)**
- Technical roles: Take-home or live assessment (maximum 2-3 hours of candidate time)
- Non-technical roles: Case study, writing sample, or portfolio review
- Evaluate against standardized rubric
- Compensate candidates for take-home assessments exceeding 2 hours ($100-500)

**Step 3.2: On-site/panel interview**
- 3-4 interviewers, each assigned specific competencies
- Each interviewer uses prepared behavioral questions and anchored scoring rubric
- Include cross-functional interviewer and culture/values interviewer
- Interviewers submit scorecards independently within 24 hours

**Step 3.3: Debrief**
- All scorecards submitted BEFORE debrief meeting
- Recruiter facilitates structured discussion (60 minutes)
- Review aggregate scores; discuss evidence (not impressions)
- If evidence is insufficient, consider additional interview (not gut feeling)
- Hiring decision documented with rationale
- If declining, document specific, non-discriminatory reasons

**DEIB checkpoint**: Are evaluation criteria applied consistently across all candidates? Are there any patterns in who advances and who does not that correlate with demographic characteristics?

### Phase 4: Offer and Close (Days 35-45)

**Step 4.1: Reference checks**
- 2-3 references (at least 1 former manager)
- Structured reference questions aligned to competencies
- Verify specific claims from interviews
- Document findings

**Step 4.2: Offer construction**
- Position within salary band per `03_compensation/compensation_strategy.md`
- Include base, bonus, equity, benefits summary
- Offer approval workflow (recruiter -> comp team -> hiring manager -> department head if above midpoint)
- Prepare offer letter per `Templates/offer_letter_template.md`

**Step 4.3: Offer delivery**
- Verbal offer call (recruiter or hiring manager -- align in advance)
- Walk through total compensation, not just base salary
- Allow 5-7 business days for decision
- Be prepared for negotiation per `02_talent_acquisition/hiring_process.md` negotiation framework

**Step 4.4: Offer acceptance**
- Signed offer letter collected
- Initiate background check per `02_talent_acquisition/hiring_process.md`
- Initiate pre-boarding process
- Notify declined candidates with personalized communication (for finalists)

### Phase 5: Handoff to Onboarding (Days 45+)

**Step 5.1: Pre-boarding activation**
- Welcome email from hiring manager
- Equipment order initiated
- System access provisioned
- Buddy assigned
- 30-60-90 plan drafted by hiring manager

**Step 5.2: Day 1 preparation**
- All systems confirmed ready
- Onboarding schedule set
- Team notified
- HR orientation scheduled

**Step 5.3: Handoff to onboarding process**
- Recruiter hands off to HRBP / HR Ops for employee lifecycle management
- Hiring manager owns the 30-60-90 plan and ongoing development
- Onboarding process per `02_talent_acquisition/hiring_process.md` onboarding section

## Consequences

**Expected positive outcomes:**
- Higher quality of hire (structured process predicts performance)
- Better candidate experience (clear, timely, respectful)
- Legal defensibility (documented, consistent, non-discriminatory)
- Diverse talent pipeline (intentional sourcing and bias mitigation)
- Efficient process (SLAs prevent delays; stakeholders aligned)

**Risks and mitigations:**
- Process too slow for competitive market: Compress timelines; pre-build pipelines for critical roles
- Hiring manager resists structure: Educate on validity research; executive mandate
- Assessment burden on candidates: Limit total candidate time to 4-6 hours; compensate for extended assessments

## Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Time to fill (requisition to acceptance) | <45 days | ATS data |
| Quality of hire (12-month performance) | Top 2 quartiles | Performance system |
| Offer acceptance rate | >85% | ATS data |
| Candidate experience NPS | >40 | Post-process survey |
| Pipeline diversity | Meets market availability | ATS demographic data |
| First-year retention | >85% | HRIS data |
| Hiring manager satisfaction | >4.0/5.0 | Post-hire survey |
| Process compliance (all steps completed) | 100% | ATS audit |
