## 4. Discussion Guide / Protocol

### For Interviews

```
INTRODUCTION (5 minutes)
- Thank participant for their time
- Introduce yourself and the purpose: "We are learning about how people [topic area]"
- Set expectations: "There are no right or wrong answers — we want to learn from your experience"
- Ask permission to record: "May I record this session for note-taking purposes?"
- Remind: "You can stop at any time or skip any question"

WARM-UP / CONTEXT (5-10 minutes)
- "Tell me about your role and what you do day to day"
- "How long have you been in this role?"
- "What tools do you use regularly for [relevant activity]?"

TOPIC 1: [CURRENT EXPERIENCE] (10-15 minutes)
- "Walk me through the last time you [relevant activity]"
- "What happened next?"
- "What was the most frustrating part of that experience?"
- "How did you handle [specific challenge]?"
- Follow-up: "Can you show me?" / "Tell me more about that"

TOPIC 2: [PAIN POINTS AND NEEDS] (10-15 minutes)
- "What is the biggest challenge you face with [topic area]?"
- "How often does this come up?"
- "What happens when it goes wrong?"
- "What have you tried to solve this?"
- "What would your ideal solution look like?"

TOPIC 3: [SOLUTIONS AND ALTERNATIVES] (10-15 minutes)
- "How are you solving this today?"
- "What do you like about your current approach?"
- "What do you wish were different?"
- [If testing a concept]: "Let me show you something — I'd love your reaction"
  - "What is your first impression?"
  - "How would you use this in your work?"
  - "What questions do you have?"
  - "What concerns you?"

WRAP-UP (5 minutes)
- "Is there anything I should have asked but did not?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"
- "Can I follow up if I have additional questions?"
- Thank the participant; provide compensation
```
