## Section 1: Customer Email -- Price Increase Announcement

### Email A: Standard Price Increase

**Subject line options (test 2-3):**
1. "Important update to your [Product] plan"
2. "Changes to [Product] pricing effective [Date]"
3. "An update on your [Product] subscription"

**From:** [CEO Name or Customer Success Name]

---

**Email body:**

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for being a valued [Product] customer. Over the past [time period], we have [invested heavily in / shipped X new features / expanded to serve Y customers] -- all to help you [core value proposition].

**What is changing:**

Starting [Date], [Product] pricing will be updated. Your plan, [Current Plan Name], will change from [Current Price] to [New Price] per [billing unit].

**What you are getting:**

Since you joined, we have added [brief list of 3-4 significant improvements]:
- [Feature/improvement 1]
- [Feature/improvement 2]
- [Feature/improvement 3]
- [Feature/improvement 4]

And we have even more planned for [upcoming quarter/year], including [1-2 upcoming features].

**Your options:**

- **Stay on your current plan** at the new price -- no action needed.
- **Switch to a different plan** that better fits your needs -- [link to pricing page].
- **Lock in annual pricing** and save [X%] -- [link to annual billing].
- **Talk to us** if you have questions -- [link to support / reply to this email].

This change takes effect on your next billing cycle after [Date]. You will see the updated amount on your next invoice.

We appreciate your trust in [Product] and are committed to continuing to deliver value that exceeds the investment.

Best,
[Name]
[Title]

## Section 3: FAQ Document

### Customer-Facing FAQ

**Q: Why are prices changing?**
A: We have [invested significantly in the product / added X features / expanded our infrastructure] over the past [period]. This update allows us to continue investing in improvements that help you [core outcome]. We are committed to ensuring the value you receive significantly exceeds the price.

**Q: How much is my price changing?**
A: Your [Plan Name] is changing from [Old Price] to [New Price] per [unit], effective [Date]. This is a [X%] change.

**Q: When does the change take effect?**
A: The new pricing takes effect on your next billing cycle after [Date]. You will see the updated amount on your next invoice.

**Q: Can I keep my current price?**
A: [If grandfathering:] Existing customers on annual plans will keep their current rate through the end of their current billing period. [If no grandfathering:] The new pricing applies to all customers starting [Date]. We are giving [X days] notice so you can plan accordingly.

**Q: Can I switch to a different plan?**
A: Yes. You can switch plans at any time from your account settings. [Link to plan comparison].

**Q: Can I lock in a lower rate?**
A: Annual billing offers a [X%] discount compared to monthly billing. You can switch to annual billing from your account settings. [Link].

**Q: What if I want to cancel?**
A: We understand price changes can be a consideration. If [Product] is not the right fit at the new price, you can cancel from your account settings. We would appreciate the opportunity to discuss your needs before you go -- [contact link].

**Q: Who can I talk to about this?**
A: You can reach our team at [support email] or [phone number]. Enterprise customers can contact their account manager directly.

## Section 4: Internal Talk Track

### For Sales Team

**When a prospect asks about the price change:**

"We recently updated our pricing to reflect the significant investment we have made in the product. In the past [period], we have shipped [specific improvements]. Our customers tell us the value they get from [Product] far exceeds the investment. Let me show you how [Product] delivers ROI for companies like yours..."

### For Customer Success Team

**When an existing customer expresses concern:**

1. **Acknowledge:** "I completely understand -- I want to make sure we address any concerns."
2. **Reframe to value:** "Let me pull up your account. Since you started using [Product], you have [specific usage/outcome data]. That is [value statement]."
3. **Explore needs:** "Is the concern about the price itself, or has something changed about how you are using [Product]?"
4. **Offer options:** Depending on the conversation, offer:
   - Annual billing discount (self-service approval)
   - Plan adjustment to better fit needs (self-service)
   - Extended grandfather period (manager approval)
   - Custom arrangement for high-value accounts (VP approval)

### For Support Team

**Standard response template:**

"Thank you for reaching out about the pricing update. I understand this is important to you. Here is what is changing for your account: [specific change]. This update reflects our continued investment in [Product], including [1-2 recent improvements]. You have several options: [list relevant options]. Would you like me to help you explore any of these?"

**Escalation criteria:**
- Customer revenue > $[threshold]: Escalate to account manager.
- Customer threatens cancellation: Offer to connect with CS manager.
- Customer requests exception: Escalate to CS manager with account context.

## Section 5: Retention Offers

### Pre-Approved Retention Offers

| Offer | Customer Eligibility | Discount | Duration | Approval |
|-------|---------------------|----------|----------|----------|
| Annual billing switch | Any customer on monthly | [X]% off monthly rate | Ongoing | Automatic |
| Loyalty lock | Customer 12+ months, at-risk | Current price locked | 6 months | CS Manager |
| Downgrade + discount | Mid-market, considering churn | [X]% off lower tier | 12 months | CS Manager |
| Custom retention | Enterprise, high LTV | Negotiated | Negotiated | VP CS / CRO |
| Let go | Low LTV, high effort | None | N/A | CS Manager |

### Retention Conversation Framework

```
1. LISTEN -- Let customer express concern fully
2. EMPATHIZE -- "I understand this is a significant consideration"
3. EXPLORE -- "Help me understand what is most important to you"
4. VALUE -- Share specific value data from their account
5. OPTIONS -- Present relevant options (not all options, just relevant ones)
6. AGREE -- Confirm the path forward
7. FOLLOW UP -- Check in 30 days after resolution
```
