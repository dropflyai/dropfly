# Brand Launch Pattern — New Brand Creation from Zero to Market

## Context

This pattern applies when creating a brand for a new entity — a new company, a new product, or a new business unit — where no prior brand equity exists. It covers the complete journey from strategic foundation through market launch and initial measurement.

---

## Prerequisites

Before beginning a brand launch, the following must be established:

- [ ] Business strategy is defined (target market, value proposition, competitive positioning)
- [ ] Funding or budget is allocated for brand development
- [ ] Executive sponsor is identified with decision authority
- [ ] Timeline expectations are set (minimum 12-16 weeks for a complete brand launch)
- [ ] Key stakeholders are identified and committed to participate
- [ ] Brand Brain preflight checklist completed (see CLAUDE.md)

---

## Phase 1: Strategic Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

### Objectives
Define the brand's strategic platform: purpose, positioning, values, personality, and architecture.

### Activities

**1.1 Discovery Research**
- Stakeholder interviews (founders, executives, board)
- Target audience research (qualitative interviews + quantitative survey)
- Competitive landscape audit (visual, verbal, positional)
- Category conventions analysis (what do all brands in this space look like/sound like?)
- Cultural context scan (macro trends relevant to the brand's space)

**1.2 Strategic Platform Development**
- Brand purpose: Why does this brand exist beyond profit?
- Brand vision: What future does this brand seek to create?
- Brand mission: How does this brand create value today?
- Brand values: 3-5 authentic, behavioral principles
- Brand positioning: Target + frame of reference + point of difference + reasons to believe
- Brand personality: 3-5 human traits that define how the brand behaves
- Brand architecture: How this brand relates to any parent or sibling brands

**1.3 Naming (If Required)**
- Naming brief development
- Name generation (aim for 100+ candidates across naming types)
- Screening: linguistic check, trademark preliminary search, domain availability
- Shortlist: 5-8 candidates for qualitative testing
- Final selection: 2-3 candidates for trademark clearance
- Legal clearance and registration initiation

### Quality Gate 1
- [ ] Strategic platform document approved by executive sponsor
- [ ] Positioning tested with target audience (minimum n=30 qual or n=200 quant)
- [ ] Name cleared by trademark counsel (if new name)
- [ ] All stakeholders aligned on strategic direction

### Deliverables
- Brand Strategy Brief (see Templates/)
- Brand Positioning Statement
- Brand Values and Personality Framework
- Name (if applicable) with trademark filing

---

## Phase 2: Identity Development (Weeks 5-10)

### Objectives
Create the brand's visual and verbal identity system.

### Activities

**2.1 Visual Identity**
- Creative brief from strategy (reference brand_strategy_brief_template.md)
- Minimum 3 creative territories explored
- Mood boards and design direction presentations
- Logo design: primary mark, secondary marks, favicon, social avatar
- Color palette: primary, secondary, extended, semantic
- Typography: display and body typefaces, scale, hierarchy
- Photography direction: style guide with example imagery
- Illustration style (if applicable)
- Iconography system
- Motion principles (if applicable)
- Design system foundations: spacing, grid, radius, shadow

**2.2 Verbal Identity**
- Brand voice definition: personality traits expressed in language
- Tone spectrum: how voice adapts across contexts (formal-casual, serious-playful)
- Messaging framework: key messages by audience and context
- Tagline development (if applicable)
- Boilerplate: 25-word, 50-word, 100-word company descriptions
- Naming conventions: how products, features, and initiatives are named

**2.3 Brand Guidelines**
- Comprehensive brand guidelines document (reference brand_guidelines_template.md)
- Digital-first format (web-based preferred over PDF)
- Do/don't examples for every element
- Quick-reference guide for non-designers

### Quality Gate 2
- [ ] Visual identity tested with target audience (recognition, preference, category fit)
- [ ] Accessibility audit passed (color contrast, readability)
- [ ] Legal review of all trademark-relevant elements
- [ ] Brand guidelines reviewed by cross-functional stakeholders
- [ ] Design system foundations documented and tokenized

### Deliverables
- Complete visual identity system
- Verbal identity guide
- Brand guidelines document
- Design system foundations

---

## Phase 3: Asset Production (Weeks 11-14)

### Objectives
Produce all launch-critical brand assets and templates.

### Activities

**3.1 Digital Assets**
- Website design (home, about, product, careers at minimum)
- Social media profiles and templates (all active platforms)
- Email templates (marketing, transactional)
- Presentation templates (keynote/PowerPoint/Google Slides)
- Document templates (letterhead, business cards, proposals)

**3.2 Product Integration**
- In-product brand implementation (if software/digital product)
- App icon and app store assets
- Loading screens, empty states, error pages
- Onboarding flows with brand narrative

**3.3 Physical Assets (If Applicable)**
- Signage and environmental design
- Packaging
- Merchandise and swag
- Trade show and event materials

### Quality Gate 3
- [ ] All assets reviewed against brand guidelines (BrandScore evaluation)
- [ ] Digital assets tested across browsers and devices
- [ ] Print assets proofed and color-matched
- [ ] Asset library organized in DAM with metadata

### Deliverables
- Complete asset library
- Template system
- Product brand integration

---

## Phase 4: Launch Execution (Weeks 15-16+)

### Activities

**4.1 Internal Launch (Before External)**
- All-hands presentation: brand story, rationale, guidelines
- Brand training for customer-facing teams
- Internal brand ambassador identification
- Employee feedback collection

**4.2 External Launch**
- Launch communications plan (PR, social, email, content)
- Website go-live
- Social media profile launch
- Press outreach (if warranted)
- Partner and vendor notification

**4.3 Post-Launch Monitoring**
- Social listening for brand reception
- Web analytics for brand content performance
- Customer feedback collection
- Brand tracking study baseline (within 30 days)

### Quality Gate 4
- [ ] Internal teams trained and equipped
- [ ] All digital properties live and functioning
- [ ] Launch communications executed
- [ ] Post-launch monitoring active

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | Prevention |
|-------------|-----------|
| Strategy rushed or skipped | Phase 1 quality gate enforced; no design without strategy |
| Design by committee | Executive sponsor has final decision authority |
| Testing skipped | Minimum sample sizes enforced at each quality gate |
| Internal launch neglected | Employees hear about brand before or simultaneously with market |
| No measurement baseline | Brand tracking study commissioned before or at launch |
| Guidelines not adopted | Digital-first, easy-access guidelines; training program |
| Budget exhausted before launch | Phase-gated budgeting with contingency reserve |

## Stakeholder Map

| Phase | RACI |
|-------|------|
| Phase 1: Strategy | R: Brand lead. A: Executive sponsor. C: Marketing, Product, Sales. I: All-hands. |
| Phase 2: Identity | R: Brand lead + Design. A: Executive sponsor. C: Marketing, Product. I: Engineering. |
| Phase 3: Assets | R: Design + Marketing. A: Brand lead. C: Engineering, Product. I: All functions. |
| Phase 4: Launch | R: Marketing + Brand. A: Executive sponsor. C: All functions. I: External partners. |

## Timeline Summary

| Phase | Duration | Key Milestone |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| Strategic Foundation | 4 weeks | Strategy approved |
| Identity Development | 6 weeks | Identity system complete |
| Asset Production | 4 weeks | All assets ready |
| Launch Execution | 2+ weeks | Brand live in market |
| **Total** | **16+ weeks** | |

## Post-Pattern Actions

After completing this pattern:
1. Log execution in `Memory/README.md` with lessons learned
2. Conduct brand launch retrospective with all stakeholders
3. Establish ongoing brand governance cadence
4. Schedule first brand health check at 90 days post-launch
5. Begin brand tracking study cadence (quarterly or semi-annual)
