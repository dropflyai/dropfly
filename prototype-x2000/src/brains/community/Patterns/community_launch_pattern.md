# Pattern: Community Launch

Launching a community from zero is the highest-stakes community operation.
The first 100 members set the culture forever. The first 30 days determine
whether the community thrives or stalls. This pattern codifies the proven
approach to launching a community that survives its first year.

---

## CONTEXT

Use this pattern when:
- Starting a brand new community from scratch
- No existing member base or audience to convert
- The community purpose, audience, and platform have been decided
- Resources (time, budget, personnel) have been allocated

Do NOT use when:
- Migrating an existing community (use Platform Migration Pattern)
- Reviving a dormant community (use Engagement Revival Pattern)
- Adding a community channel to an existing platform

---

## FORCES

The tensions inherent in community launch:

| Force | Tension |
|-------|---------|
| Speed vs. Quality | Pressure to launch fast vs. need for solid foundation |
| Growth vs. Culture | Desire for members vs. need for cultural establishment |
| Content vs. Empty | Need for content vs. no members to create it |
| Structure vs. Freedom | Need for organization vs. organic community feel |
| Investment vs. ROI | Upfront cost vs. delayed returns |

---

## SOLUTION

### Phase 0: Strategic Foundation (Week 1–4)

Complete before any member recruitment:

1. **Define the 5P Framework** (see 02_strategy/community_strategy.md)
   - Purpose: Why does this community exist?
   - People: Who is it for (and who is it NOT for)?
   - Place: Which platform?
   - Participation: How do members engage?
   - Policy: What are the rules?

2. **Create foundational documents:**
   - Community guidelines / code of conduct
   - Welcome message template
   - Channel descriptions and purposes
   - FAQ document
   - Moderator guidelines

3. **Configure platform:**
   - Set up channels/spaces (start with fewer than 10)
   - Configure roles and permissions
   - Set up moderation tools
   - Create welcome/onboarding automation
   - Test the entire member journey

4. **Prepare seed content:**
   - Write 10–20 seed posts/discussions
   - Prepare a content calendar for first 4 weeks
   - Create resource documents (getting started guide)
   - Draft first 4 weekly discussion prompts

### Phase 1: Founding Circle (Week 5–8)

Recruit and onboard the first 20–50 members:

1. **Identify founding members:**
   - Hand-pick 20–50 people who embody ideal community culture
   - Prioritize diversity (perspective, background, expertise)
   - Ensure geographic/timezone diversity
   - Choose people willing to invest extra effort

2. **Personal invitation:**
   - Individual, personalized outreach to each person
   - Explain the vision and their role as a founder
   - Be transparent about the stage (early, needs their help)
   - Give them specific first actions

3. **Founding circle onboarding:**
   - Private onboarding session (live call)
   - Explain community vision, values, expectations
   - Collect feedback on initial setup
   - Ask them to introduce themselves
   - Assign a "founding member" role/badge

4. **Culture establishment:**
   - Founding members set behavioral norms through example
   - Community manager models expected interaction patterns
   - First conflicts/edge cases define enforcement
   - Document cultural norms as they emerge

### Phase 2: Controlled Growth (Week 9–16)

Open to broader audience while maintaining quality:

1. **Soft launch:**
   - Expand invitation to wider network
   - Enable self-registration (with guidelines acceptance)
   - Announce via existing channels (email, social, product)
   - Target 50–200 members by end of period

2. **Programming launch:**
   - Start first recurring event (weekly meetup or office hours)
   - Begin content calendar execution
   - Launch first discussion prompt series
   - Highlight member contributions publicly

3. **Feedback loop:**
   - Collect feedback from founding members weekly
   - Survey new members at Day 7 and Day 30
   - Adjust platform configuration based on usage
   - Iterate on content strategy based on engagement

4. **Measurement:**
   - Track activation rate (% of joiners who participate)
   - Track engagement rate (DAU/MAU)
   - Track retention (7-day and 30-day return rate)
   - Track member satisfaction (qualitative feedback)

### Phase 3: Establishing (Month 5–12)

Transition from launch mode to sustainable operation:

1. **Program formalization:**
   - Launch second recurring program
   - Recruit first volunteer contributors (3–5)
   - Create member recognition system
   - Establish operational cadence

2. **Growth channels:**
   - Begin content-led growth (SEO, social sharing)
   - Enable organic referral mechanisms
   - Consider first cross-promotion partnership
   - Track growth source attribution

3. **Community maturation:**
   - Document institutional knowledge
   - Create self-service resources
   - Reduce community manager content creation (members take over)
   - Establish moderator pipeline

- [ ] Cultural norms documented

### Controlled Growth
- [ ] Self-registration enabled

- [ ] First recurring event held
- [ ] Content calendar executing on schedule

- [ ] Second program launched
- [ ] First volunteer contributors recruited
- [ ] Recognition system active

- [ ] Operational cadence established

## TIMELINE

| Week | Phase | Key Activities |
|------|-------|---------------|
| 1–4 | Foundation | Strategy, platform, content preparation |
| 5–6 | Founding Circle | Recruit and onboard founding members |
| 7–8 | Founding Circle | Establish culture, collect feedback |
| 9–12 | Controlled Growth | Soft launch, first events, iteration |
| 13–16 | Controlled Growth | Growth push, measurement, optimization |
| 17–24 | Establishing | Programs, volunteers, growth channels |
| 25–52 | Establishing | Operational cadence, sustainability |

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS

| Anti-Pattern | What Happens | Prevention |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Big bang launch | Open to thousands on day 1, culture never forms | Graduated launch with founding circle |
| Build and wait | Build platform, expect people to show up | Active recruitment and seeding |
| Perfectionism | Delay launch waiting for perfect setup | Launch with minimum viable community |
| Growth fixation | Focus on member count, ignore engagement | Health metrics from day 1 |
| Founder absence | Community manager not active daily | Daily presence for first 90 days |
| Over-engineering | Too many channels, too many rules, too many bots | Start simple, add as needed |

## EXAMPLES

### Developer Community Launch (Product Company)

**Week 1–4:** Strategy session, Discord server setup, 15 seed discussions
about common developer pain points, documentation of getting-started guides.

**Week 5–8:** Invited 30 developers from existing user base who had been
active in support channels. Held virtual launch event. Established weekly
office hours.

**Week 9–16:** Opened registration via in-product announcement. Grew to
150 members. Launched weekly "Show Your Build" showcase. Started monthly AMA
with engineering team.

**Month 5–12:** First 5 community moderators recruited. Discord reached
500 members. Community-generated documentation exceeded staff documentation.
Monthly community call established.

### B2B Customer Community Launch (SaaS)

**Week 1–4:** Discourse forum configured, branded. Created 8 categories.
Wrote 20 FAQ entries from support ticket history.

**Week 5–8:** Invited 25 customers from top accounts. VIP onboarding call.
Seeded discussions around use cases and best practices.

**Week 9–16:** Soft launch via email to all customers. Grew to 200 members.
Bi-weekly customer spotlight program. Feature request voting.

**Month 5–12:** Support tickets deflected by 15%. Customer retention for
community members 12% higher than non-members. Ambassador program launched
with 10 founding ambassadors.

---

**The community launch pattern is a proven framework, but it requires
discipline. The temptation to skip the founding circle phase and jump to
growth is strong. Resist it. The 30 hours invested in hand-recruiting 30
founding members will determine the trajectory of the next 3 years. There
is no shortcut to culture.**
