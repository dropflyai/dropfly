## Section 2: Interview Guide

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Welcome and rapport:**

"Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. My name is [Name] and I am working on [brief context]. This conversation will take about [duration] minutes.

I want to emphasize that there are no right or wrong answers. I am here to learn from your experience and perspective. Your honest feedback -- even if it is critical -- is incredibly valuable.

[If recording:] With your permission, I would like to record this conversation so I can focus on our discussion rather than taking notes. The recording will only be used by our research team and your responses will be anonymized. Is that okay?"

**Consent:** [ ] Verbal consent obtained [ ] Written consent obtained

**Warm-up question:**
"Before we dive in, could you tell me a little about yourself and your role? What does a typical day look like for you?"

---

### Section A: Context and Background (10 minutes)

*Understand the participant's world before discussing the specific topic.*

**Questions:**

1. "Tell me about your role and responsibilities. What are you primarily responsible for?"

2. "What are the biggest challenges you face in your work right now?"
   - **Probe:** "Can you give me a specific example?"
   - **Probe:** "How often does this come up?"
   - **Probe:** "What have you tried to solve this?"

3. "Walk me through how you currently handle [the task/workflow relevant to your product]."
   - **Probe:** "What tools or methods do you use?"
   - **Probe:** "What works well about your current approach?"
   - **Probe:** "What is frustrating or time-consuming?"

4. "How do you measure success in this area? What does 'good' look like?"

---

### Section B: Problem Exploration (15 minutes)

*Deep dive into the specific problems your product addresses.*

**Questions:**

5. "Tell me about the last time you experienced [specific problem]. Walk me through what happened."
   - **Probe:** "What triggered the situation?"
   - **Probe:** "What did you do first? Then what?"
   - **Probe:** "How long did it take to resolve?"
   - **Probe:** "How did it make you feel?"

6. "How important is solving [this problem] relative to your other priorities?"
   - **Probe:** "On a scale of 1-10, how painful is this?"
   - **Probe:** "What would change if this problem were solved?"

7. "What have you tried to address this? (Tools, workarounds, processes)"
   - **Probe:** "What worked about those solutions?"
   - **Probe:** "What fell short?"
   - **Probe:** "Why did you stop using [previous solution]?"

8. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [this area], what would it be?"

---

### Section C: Product / Solution Exploration (15 minutes)

*If participant uses your product or evaluating a concept/prototype.*

**For current users:**

9. "How did you first discover [product]? What made you decide to try it?"

10. "Walk me through how you use [product] in a typical week."
    - **Probe:** "Which features do you use most? Least?"
    - **Probe:** "Is there anything you wish it did that it does not?"
    - **Probe:** "Is there anything confusing or frustrating?"

11. "Think about the last time [product] really helped you. What happened?"

12. "Think about the last time [product] let you down. What happened?"

13. "If [product] disappeared tomorrow, what would you do instead?"
    - **Probe:** "How hard would that transition be?"

**For concept / prototype testing:**

9. "I would like to show you something we are working on. This is [early / not finished]. I am looking for your honest reaction."

10. [Show prototype/concept] "What is your first impression?"
    - **Probe:** "What do you think this does?"
    - **Probe:** "Who do you think this is for?"

11. "How well does this address the problem we discussed earlier?"
    - **Probe:** "What is most appealing about this?"
    - **Probe:** "What concerns you?"
    - **Probe:** "What is missing?"

12. "Would you use this? Why or why not?"
    - **Probe:** "What would need to be true for you to use this regularly?"

---

### Section D: Decision-Making and Purchase (5 minutes)

*Understand how they evaluate and buy solutions.*

14. "When you are evaluating a new tool or solution, what matters most to you?"
    - **Probe:** "Who else is involved in the decision?"
    - **Probe:** "What would make you say no?"

15. "How much would you expect to pay for a solution like this?"
    - **Probe:** "What would feel like a great deal?"
    - **Probe:** "At what price would you say 'too expensive'?"

---

### Closing (5 minutes)

16. "Is there anything else about [topic] that I should have asked but did not?"

17. "Is there anyone else you think I should talk to about this?"

**Wrap up:**
"Thank you so much for your time and insights. This has been incredibly helpful. [Explain incentive delivery]. If I have any follow-up questions, would it be okay to reach out?"

## Section 3: Probing Techniques Reference

| Technique | When to Use | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Clarification** | Answer is vague or unclear | "What do you mean by [term]?" |
| **Elaboration** | Want more depth | "Can you tell me more about that?" |
| **Example** | Answer is abstract | "Can you give me a specific example?" |
| **Frequency** | Understand how common | "How often does that happen?" |
| **Emotion** | Understand feelings | "How did that make you feel?" |
| **Comparison** | Understand preferences | "How does that compare to [alternative]?" |
| **Silence** | Participant is thinking | [Wait 5-7 seconds before prompting] |
| **Redirect** | Participant is off-topic | "That is interesting. Going back to..." |
| **Summary** | Confirm understanding | "So if I understand correctly..." |

## Section 5: Interview Quality Checklist

After each interview, self-assess:
- [ ] I asked open-ended questions (not yes/no)
- [ ] I listened more than I talked (80/20 rule)
- [ ] I probed on interesting responses
- [ ] I did not lead the participant toward desired answers
- [ ] I asked about behavior, not just opinions
- [ ] I got specific examples, not just generalities
- [ ] I noted emotional responses and body language
- [ ] I avoided introducing product features too early
- [ ] I completed post-interview notes within 1 hour
