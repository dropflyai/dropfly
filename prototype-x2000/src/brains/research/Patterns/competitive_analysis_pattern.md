# Pattern: Competitive Analysis

A structured approach to conducting comprehensive competitive landscape assessments that inform strategic positioning and go-to-market decisions.

---

## Pattern Overview

**When to use:** Annual strategic planning, entering a new market segment, responding to a competitive threat, preparing for fundraising, or establishing competitive positioning.

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks for a full assessment. 1-2 weeks for a focused update.

**Team:** 1 researcher/analyst, with input from product, sales, and marketing.

**Investment:** $5K-$15K (primarily for tools, reports, and any primary research).

**Deliverables:** Competitive landscape report, positioning map, competitor profiles, battlecards, and strategic recommendations.

---

## Phase 1: Scoping and Planning (Week 1)

### 1.1 Define Competitive Scope

Not all competitors deserve equal attention. Begin by defining the competitive universe and tiering.

**Competitor identification workshop (60 minutes):**

Attendees: Product lead, sales lead, marketing lead, researcher.

Activities:
1. Brainstorm all known competitors (direct, indirect, potential, substitutes).
2. Add competitors from review sites (G2, Capterra), analyst reports, and customer mentions.
3. Tier competitors: Tier 1 (3-5 primary threats), Tier 2 (5-10 secondary), Tier 3 (monitoring only).
4. Define the tiering criteria (market share, deal frequency, strategic threat level).

### 1.2 Define Key Intelligence Questions

What specific competitive questions must this analysis answer?

**Examples:**
- "How does our feature set compare to Competitor X's latest release?"
- "What pricing strategy is Competitor Y using for the enterprise segment?"
- "Which competitors are winning in the [specific segment] and why?"
- "Where are the positioning gaps we can exploit?"
- "What are the competitive threats to our renewal rates?"

### 1.3 Source Plan

Map the sources to be used for each tier:

| Source | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Product evaluation (sign up, use) | Yes | Select | No |
| Website and pricing page analysis | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Review site analysis (G2, Capterra) | Yes | Yes | Scan |
| Job posting analysis | Yes | Optional | No |
| Financial/funding analysis | Yes | Yes | Scan |
| Social media analysis | Yes | Optional | No |
| Win/loss data review | Yes | Yes | No |
| Content and messaging analysis | Yes | Optional | No |
| Patent/technology analysis | Optional | No | No |

## Phase 2: Data Collection (Weeks 1-3)

### 2.1 Product Evaluation

For each Tier 1 competitor, experience their product firsthand.

**Activities:**
- Sign up for free trial or freemium tier.
- Complete their onboarding flow. Note the experience.
- Explore core features relevant to your competitive overlap.
- Test advanced features and integrations.
- Document the user experience: what is good, what is frustrating, what is missing.
- Screenshot key screens, especially dashboard, pricing, settings, and differentiating features.

**Output:** Product evaluation notes per competitor with annotated screenshots.

### 2.2 Digital Presence Analysis

**Website analysis:**
- Home page messaging: headline, subheadline, value proposition, CTA.
- Target audience signals: language, imagery, customer logos, case studies.
- Feature pages: how they describe and categorize their capabilities.
- Pricing page: model, tiers, prices, packaging, discounting signals.
- Blog/content: publishing frequency, topics, thought leadership positioning.

**Social media analysis:**
- Platform presence and activity level.
- Content themes and messaging consistency.
- Engagement rates and community size.
- Advertising activity (Facebook Ad Library, LinkedIn Ad tracking).

**Review site analysis:**
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Gartner Peer Insights.
- Overall ratings and rating trends.
- Most praised features (from positive reviews).
- Most criticized weaknesses (from negative reviews).
- Competitive comparison reviews (reviews that mention you or other competitors).

**Reference:** `06_competitive/competitive_intelligence.md`

### 2.3 Internal Data Collection

**Win/loss data:** Pull CRM data on competitive deals. Analyze win rate by competitor, deal size, segment, and time period. If win/loss interviews exist, review themes.

**Sales team interviews:** Conduct 4-6 structured interviews with sales reps who regularly compete against Tier 1 competitors. Ask: What is their sales pitch? What do they claim? What objections do buyers raise about us? What objections do buyers raise about them? When do we win and when do we lose?

**Customer feedback:** Review support tickets, NPS verbatims, and customer interviews that mention competitors.

### 2.4 Financial and Strategic Analysis

**For public companies:** Revenue, growth rate, gross margin, R&D spending, sales and marketing spending, geographic mix, segment breakdown, management commentary from earnings calls.

**For private companies:** Funding raised, investors, valuation (if known), headcount growth (LinkedIn data), job posting volume and roles, executive hires, M&A activity.

**For all:** Recent press releases, partnership announcements, product launches, customer wins.

## Phase 3: Analysis (Weeks 3-5)

### 3.1 Feature Comparison Matrix

Build a weighted feature comparison for Tier 1 competitors.

**Process:**
1. Define feature categories based on customer decision criteria (from win/loss data and customer research).
2. List specific capabilities within each category.
3. Rate each competitor (Full, Partial, None, or 1-5 scale).
4. Weight categories by customer importance (from surveys, interviews, or sales data).
5. Calculate weighted scores.

**Reference:** `06_competitive/competitive_analysis.md`

### 3.2 Positioning Map

**Process:**
1. Select two axes that represent the most strategically meaningful dimensions for your market.
2. Place each competitor on the map based on product evaluation, messaging analysis, and customer perception data.
3. Identify white space (unoccupied but potentially valuable positions).
4. Identify crowding (positions with many competitors, high competition intensity).
5. Mark your current position and desired position.

### 3.3 Competitor Profiles

For each Tier 1 competitor, create a structured profile:

```
COMPETITOR PROFILE: [Name]

OVERVIEW
- Founded: [Year]
- Headquarters: [Location]
- Funding: [Total raised, last round, investors]
- Headcount: [Estimated, from LinkedIn]
- Revenue: [Estimated, from public data or sources]

PRODUCT
- Core offering: [Description]
- Key differentiators: [What they do uniquely well]
- Weaknesses: [Where they fall short]
- Technology stack: [Known tech choices]
- Recent releases: [Last 6 months]

MARKET POSITION
- Target segment: [Who they sell to]
- Pricing: [Model, approximate price points]
- Market share: [Estimated, with source]
- Growth trajectory: [Accelerating, stable, declining]

STRATEGY
- Strategic direction: [Where they are heading]
- Recent moves: [Launches, hires, partnerships, M&A]
- Likely next moves: [Predictions based on evidence]

COMPETITIVE ASSESSMENT
- Strengths: [3-5 evidence-based strengths]
- Weaknesses: [3-5 evidence-based weaknesses]
- Win rate against us: [From CRM data]
- Top reasons we win against them: [From win/loss data]
- Top reasons we lose against them: [From win/loss data]
```

### 3.4 SWOT Analysis

Conduct SWOT for each Tier 1 competitor and for your own position relative to the competitive landscape. Cross-reference competitor strengths with your weaknesses and vice versa to identify strategic imperatives.

### 3.5 Strategic Implications

Translate analysis into strategic recommendations:

**Positioning recommendations:** Based on white space analysis, customer needs, and competitive gaps, where should we position?

**Product recommendations:** Based on feature comparison and customer priorities, what should we build, improve, or deprioritize?

**Sales enablement recommendations:** Based on win/loss patterns, how should we sell against each competitor?

**Marketing recommendations:** Based on messaging analysis, how should we differentiate our communications?

## Phase 4: Deliverables (Weeks 5-6)

### 4.1 Competitive Landscape Report

**Structure:**
1. Executive summary (1 page): Top 5 findings and strategic implications.
2. Market overview: Market structure, key trends, and dynamics.
3. Competitive landscape map: Tiered competitor list and positioning map.
4. Tier 1 competitor profiles (2-3 pages each).
5. Feature comparison matrix with weighted scoring.
6. Win/loss analysis summary.
7. Strategic recommendations: Positioning, product, sales, marketing.
8. Appendix: Tier 2 profiles, data sources, methodology.

### 4.2 Battlecards

Create or update competitive battlecards for the sales team. One battlecard per Tier 1 competitor.

**Battlecard structure:**
- Quick overview (who they are, who they serve).
- Why we win / why we lose.
- Their key claims and our responses.
- Landmines and trap questions.
- Objection handling scripts.
- Proof points and customer quotes.

**Reference:** `06_competitive/competitive_analysis.md`

### 4.3 Presentation and Workshop

Present findings to leadership and cross-functional stakeholders. Follow with a 60-minute workshop to discuss strategic implications and align on competitive response.

**Workshop activities:**
- React to positioning map: Does it match the team's perception? Where are the surprises?
- Prioritize competitive threats: Which competitors require the most attention?
- Align on positioning: Agree on desired position and the investment required to get there.
- Assign follow-up actions: Product roadmap adjustments, messaging updates, sales training.

## Ongoing Maintenance

### Monitoring Cadence

After the initial assessment, maintain competitive intelligence through ongoing monitoring:

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Automated monitoring (Crayon, Google Alerts) | Continuous |
| Review site scan | Monthly |
| Battlecard update | Monthly |
| Win/loss data review | Monthly |
| Competitive digest distribution | Monthly |
| Feature comparison update | Quarterly |
| Full landscape refresh | Annually |

### Trigger-Based Updates

Refresh competitive analysis out-of-cycle when:
- A Tier 1 competitor launches a major product or feature.
- A competitor raises significant funding or is acquired.
- Win rates against a competitor change meaningfully.
- A new entrant gains traction in the market.
- The company is preparing for a strategic decision (fundraising, pricing change, new segment entry).

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Competitive scope covers direct, indirect, potential, and substitutes
- [ ] Tier 1 competitors have product evaluation from direct experience
- [ ] Feature comparison is weighted by customer importance
- [ ] Positioning map uses strategically meaningful dimensions
- [ ] Win/loss data is analyzed and patterns are identified
- [ ] Battlecards are created/updated for all Tier 1 competitors
- [ ] Strategic recommendations are specific and actionable
- [ ] Monitoring cadence is established for ongoing maintenance
- [ ] Findings are presented to stakeholders with workshop follow-up
- [ ] Insights are logged in the research repository

---

**This pattern provides the standard approach for competitive analysis. Adjust scope and depth based on the strategic context and available resources.**
