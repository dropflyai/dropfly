# Pattern: Market Entry Research

A comprehensive research program for evaluating whether to enter a new market, how to position, and what to build first.

---

## Pattern Overview

**When to use:** Considering entering a new market, launching a new product category, expanding to a new geography, or targeting a new customer segment.

**Duration:** 8-12 weeks (can be compressed to 6 weeks for urgent decisions).

**Team:** 1-2 researchers, with input from product, strategy, and sales stakeholders.

**Investment:** $15K-$50K (including incentives, tools, and external data).

**Deliverables:** Market entry recommendation report, market sizing, competitive landscape, persona profiles, positioning recommendation, and go-to-market research foundation.

---

## Phase 1: Market Landscape (Weeks 1-3)

### 1.1 Desk Research

**Objective:** Build foundational understanding of the market using secondary sources.

**Activities:**
- Industry report review (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, Statista, IBISWorld).
- Government data analysis (Census, BLS, public filings).
- Startup and VC activity mapping (Crunchbase, PitchBook, CB Insights).
- Regulatory environment scan.
- Technology landscape assessment.

**Output:** Market landscape brief with initial TAM/SAM estimate, key trends, and regulatory considerations.

**Reference:** `03_market_research/market_sizing.md`, `03_market_research/market_analysis.md`

### 1.2 Competitive Landscape Mapping

**Objective:** Identify and categorize all relevant competitors.

**Activities:**
- Build tiered competitor list (direct, indirect, potential, substitutes).
- Analyze competitor positioning, pricing, and feature sets.
- Map competitive landscape on 2x2 positioning matrix.
- Identify competitive gaps and white space opportunities.

**Output:** Competitive landscape map, competitor profiles (Tier 1), initial positioning hypothesis.

**Reference:** `06_competitive/competitive_intelligence.md`, `06_competitive/competitive_analysis.md`

---

## Phase 2: Customer Discovery (Weeks 3-6)

### 2.1 Expert Interviews

**Objective:** Validate market assumptions and identify non-obvious dynamics.

**Activities:**
- Identify 5-8 industry experts (analysts, advisors, former executives in the space).
- Conduct 45-60 minute semi-structured interviews.
- Focus on: market dynamics, unmet needs, buyer behavior, competitive dynamics, barriers to entry.

**Output:** Expert interview synthesis with validated/invalidated market assumptions.

**Reference:** `05_qualitative/qualitative_methods.md`

### 2.2 Target Customer Interviews

**Objective:** Understand the target customer's needs, pain points, decision process, and willingness to adopt a new solution.

**Activities:**
- Recruit 12-15 potential customers from the target segment.
- Conduct 60-minute semi-structured interviews.
- Explore: current solutions, pain points, Jobs to Be Done, decision criteria, willingness to switch, budget and procurement process.
- Include both current users of competitor solutions and non-consumers.

**Output:** Customer interview synthesis, preliminary persona drafts, Jobs to Be Done map.

**Reference:** `02_user_research/user_research_methods.md`, `05_qualitative/qualitative_analysis.md`

### 2.3 Observation (Optional)

**Objective:** Observe target customers in their natural environment to identify unstated needs and workarounds.

**Activities:**
- Shadow 3-5 target customers for 2-4 hours each.
- Document workflows, tools, pain points, and workarounds.
- Photograph artifacts and environments (with consent).

**Output:** Observation notes, workflow maps, opportunity identification.

---

## Phase 3: Quantitative Validation (Weeks 6-9)

### 3.1 Market Sizing Validation

**Objective:** Triangulate market size using bottom-up analysis in addition to top-down.

**Activities:**
- Build bottom-up market model using customer count and pricing assumptions from interviews.
- Compare with top-down estimates from Phase 1.
- Refine TAM/SAM/SOM with validated assumptions.
- Model growth scenarios (conservative, base, aggressive).

**Output:** Triangulated market sizing with explicit assumptions and confidence ranges.

**Reference:** `03_market_research/market_sizing.md`

### 3.2 Concept Validation Survey

**Objective:** Quantify demand, willingness to pay, and feature priorities among a larger sample.

**Activities:**
- Design a 10-minute survey based on interview findings.
- Include: problem validation (frequency and severity of pain), concept interest (after concept description), feature priorities (MaxDiff or ranking), willingness to pay (Van Westendorp or Gabor-Granger), demographic/firmographic profiling.
- Deploy to 150-300 respondents from the target segment.

**Output:** Quantified demand validation, feature priority ranking, preliminary pricing insights.

**Reference:** `04_survey/survey_design.md`, `04_survey/survey_analysis.md`

---

## Phase 4: Synthesis and Recommendation (Weeks 9-12)

### 4.1 Cross-Method Synthesis

**Activities:**
- Triangulate findings across desk research, interviews, observation, and survey.
- Identify convergence (strong evidence) and divergence (areas of uncertainty).
- Develop final personas based on qualitative and quantitative data.
- Refine positioning hypothesis based on competitive gaps and customer needs.

### 4.2 Market Entry Recommendation

**Deliverable structure:**
1. **Executive Summary:** Go/No-Go recommendation with top 3 supporting reasons.
2. **Market Opportunity:** TAM/SAM/SOM with growth projections.
3. **Customer Profile:** Target personas with needs, behaviors, and decision criteria.
4. **Competitive Landscape:** Positioning map, competitive gaps, differentiation strategy.
5. **Risks and Mitigations:** Key risks to market entry and proposed mitigations.
6. **Go-to-Market Foundation:** Recommended positioning, pricing direction, and initial feature set.
7. **Next Steps:** Research needed post-entry (beta testing, pricing optimization, etc.).

### 4.3 Decision Gate

Present findings to the decision-making body. Provide a clear recommendation (Enter, Do Not Enter, Enter with Conditions) with confidence level and key assumptions that, if wrong, would change the recommendation.

---

## Pattern Adaptation Guide

### Compressed Timeline (6 weeks)

- Overlap Phase 1 and 2 (desk research in parallel with interviews).
- Reduce interview count (8-10 instead of 12-15).
- Use shorter survey (5 minutes, 100-150 respondents).
- Accept lower confidence in quantitative findings.

### Enterprise vs SMB Focus

- Enterprise: Emphasize expert interviews and fewer, deeper customer interviews. Decision process mapping is critical.
- SMB: Emphasize survey validation with larger sample. Shorter interviews. Self-serve purchasing behavior.

### International Market Entry

- Add regulatory deep-dive for target geography.
- Include local expert interviews (cultural, regulatory, competitive nuances).
- Consider localization research (language, cultural adaptation needs).
- Survey must be translated and culturally adapted.

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Market sizing is triangulated (top-down + bottom-up) with explicit assumptions
- [ ] At least 10 customer interviews conducted with target segment
- [ ] Competitive landscape covers Tier 1 competitors with feature analysis
- [ ] Survey validates demand with n >= 100 from target segment
- [ ] Personas are data-grounded (not assumption-based)
- [ ] Positioning hypothesis is tested against customer data and competitive gaps
- [ ] Recommendation includes confidence level and key risks
- [ ] Findings are synthesized across methods (not just appended)

---

**This pattern provides the standard approach for market entry research. Adapt to context but maintain rigor.**
