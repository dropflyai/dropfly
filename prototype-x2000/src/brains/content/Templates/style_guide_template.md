### Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** is constant—it is who we are. It does not change across
contexts.

**Tone** adapts to the situation while maintaining the voice. Think of
voice as personality and tone as mood.

| Context | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| Blog post (educational) | Helpful, clear, encouraging | "Here's how to get started..." |
| Error message | Calm, empathetic, solution-focused | "Something went wrong. Here's what to do..." |
| Product announcement | Excited, confident, benefit-focused | "We're thrilled to introduce..." |
| Support response | Warm, patient, thorough | "I understand the frustration. Let me help..." |
| Social media | Conversational, engaging, concise | "Quick tip: ..." |
| Sales email | Professional, value-oriented, respectful | "Based on your goals, I think..." |
| Legal/compliance | Precise, neutral, complete | "These terms govern your use of..." |
| Crisis communication | Transparent, serious, accountable | "We want to be upfront about..." |

### Sentence and Paragraph Standards

**Average Sentence Length:** 15–20 words
**Maximum Sentence Length:** 30 words (exceptions require justification)
**Maximum Paragraph Length:** 4 sentences (web content), 6 sentences
(long-form print)
**Active Voice Ratio:** 80%+ active voice, 20% or less passive

### Lists

**Bulleted lists for:**
- Items without inherent order
- Features, benefits, examples
- Parallel structure required (all items same grammatical form)

**Numbered lists for:**
- Sequential steps or instructions
- Ranked items
- Items that will be referenced by number

**List formatting:**
- Capitalize first word of each item
- Parallel grammatical structure (all start with verbs, or all are
  noun phrases)
- Period at end: [ ] Yes (full sentences only) [ ] No [ ] Always

### Links

**Link text rules:**
- Descriptive anchor text (describes the destination)
- Never use "click here," "read more," or "learn more" as standalone
  anchor text
- Underlined or colored to indicate clickability
- External links: [ ] Open in new tab [ ] Open in same tab

### Images

**Alt text requirements:**
- All images must have alt text
- Describe the content and function of the image
- Maximum 125 characters
- Do not begin with "Image of" or "Photo of"
- Decorative images: empty alt attribute (alt="")

## Section 6: Inclusive Language

### Principles

- Use gender-neutral language unless referring to a specific person
- Avoid ableist language and metaphors
- Use people-first language for disabilities
- Represent diverse perspectives in examples and imagery
- Avoid culturally specific idioms that may not translate

### Specific Guidelines

| Instead of | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Guys, mankind | Team, everyone, people, humanity |
| Crazy, insane | Unexpected, surprising, remarkable |
| Blind spot | Gap, oversight, unexamined area |
| Grandfathered | Legacy, exempt, pre-existing |
| Master/slave | Primary/replica, leader/follower |
| Whitelist/blacklist | Allowlist/blocklist |
| Sanity check | Confidence check, quick review |
| Crippled | Disabled, impaired, limited |
| Tone deaf | Unaware, insensitive, misguided |
| He/she (generic) | They, the user, the customer |

## Section 7: Legal and Compliance

### Required Disclosures

| Content Type | Disclosure Required | Template |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| Sponsored content | "Sponsored by [Brand]" | [Location: top of content] |
| Affiliate links | "Contains affiliate links" | [Location: before first link] |
| AI-assisted content | [Per company policy] | [Location: footer] |
| Testimonials | "Results may vary" | [Location: near testimonial] |
| Financial claims | [Per regulatory requirement] | [Location: ___] |

### Claims and Substantiation

- All performance claims must be substantiated with data
- Superlatives ("best," "fastest," "#1") require third-party evidence
- Competitor comparisons must be factual and current
- Customer results must be attributed with permission
- Forward-looking statements require appropriate disclaimers

### Privacy and Data

- Never reference specific customer data without written permission
- Use fictional names and companies in examples unless permission granted
- Comply with GDPR, CCPA, and applicable privacy regulations
- Email content must include physical address and unsubscribe link
