# Content Brain — Patterns Library

## Purpose

This directory contains reusable content patterns—proven, repeatable
frameworks for common content production scenarios. Patterns encode
institutional knowledge so that every team member (human or AI) can
produce consistent, high-quality output without reinventing the wheel.

---

## What Is a Content Pattern?

A content pattern is a documented solution to a recurring content
challenge. It includes:
- The context (when to use this pattern)
- The structure (what the output should look like)
- The process (how to produce it)
- The quality criteria (how to evaluate the result)
- The anti-patterns (common mistakes to avoid)

Patterns are not templates. Templates are fill-in-the-blank documents.
Patterns are strategic frameworks that guide decision-making. Use
patterns to think; use templates to produce.

## Available Patterns

### blog_post_pattern.md
**When to use:** Creating any blog post format (how-to, listicle,
opinion, data-driven, case study).
**Covers:** Post type selection, structure by type, hook formulas,
CTA placement, SEO integration, quality gates.

### content_campaign_pattern.md
**When to use:** Planning a multi-asset content campaign tied to a
business objective (product launch, seasonal push, thought leadership
series).
**Covers:** Campaign architecture, asset planning, channel strategy,
production timeline, measurement framework.

### content_repurposing_pattern.md
**When to use:** Maximizing the reach and ROI of an existing content
asset by adapting it for multiple formats and channels.
**Covers:** The 1-to-10 framework, format adaptation rules, quality
preservation, distribution sequencing, tracking.

## Pattern Usage Protocol

1. **Check patterns before creating content.** If a relevant pattern
   exists, follow it. Do not deviate without documenting why.

2. **Patterns are living documents.** When a pattern produces
   consistently poor results or a better approach is discovered,
   update the pattern. Log the change in Memory/README.md.

3. **New patterns require evidence.** A solution must be used
   successfully at least three times before it qualifies as a pattern.
   Until then, document it as an experiment in Memory/.

4. **Patterns do not replace judgment.** Patterns provide structure,
   not answers. Every content piece has unique context that may require
   adaptation. The pattern is the starting point, not the ceiling.

## Pattern Template

When creating a new pattern, use this structure:

```markdown
# [Pattern Name]

## Context
When and why to use this pattern.

## Structure
The framework or architecture of the solution.

## Process
Step-by-step production instructions.

## Quality Criteria
How to evaluate whether the pattern was applied successfully.

## Anti-Patterns
Common mistakes and how to avoid them.

## Examples
Links to published content that exemplifies this pattern.

## References
Sources and research that informed this pattern.
```

## Maintenance

Patterns are reviewed quarterly during the Content Strategy Review.
Outdated patterns are archived with a note explaining why. New patterns
are proposed by any team member and approved by the content lead after
three successful applications.
