# Content Repurposing Pattern — Maximizing ROI Through Format Adaptation

## Context

Use this pattern after publishing any substantial content asset (blog
post of 2,000+ words, webinar, white paper, podcast episode, or
original research). Repurposing transforms one content investment into
multiple touchpoints across formats and channels, dramatically
increasing content ROI. This pattern ensures that repurposed content
maintains quality, respects platform conventions, and tracks back to
the original for attribution.

---

## Pattern Overview

The repurposing workflow follows three stages:

```
EXTRACT → ADAPT → DISTRIBUTE
```

Extract the valuable components from the source content. Adapt them
for target formats and platforms. Distribute them with proper
tracking and cross-linking.

---

## Stage 1: Extract — Mining the Source Content

### Extractable Asset Types

Every substantial content piece contains multiple extractable assets.
Identify all of the following within the source:

**Key Insights (3–8 per piece)**
Self-contained observations, findings, or recommendations that can
stand alone as individual social posts, email sections, or slide
content.

Extraction method: Read the source and highlight every sentence or
paragraph that communicates a complete, valuable idea without requiring
the surrounding context.

**Data Points (1–5 per piece)**
Specific statistics, benchmarks, or quantified claims that can be
visualized and shared independently.

Extraction method: Identify every number, percentage, or quantified
comparison in the source. Each becomes a potential graphic or social
post.

**Frameworks and Models (0–3 per piece)**
Conceptual structures, decision matrices, process diagrams, or mental
models that can be visualized.

Extraction method: Identify every structured framework—whether
explicitly diagrammed or described in text—that can be represented
visually.

**Quotable Statements (3–10 per piece)**
Bold, concise statements that work as standalone quotes for social
media or slide decks.

Extraction method: Identify sentences that express an opinion,
prediction, or principle in 30 words or fewer with enough impact to
stand alone.

**Step-by-Step Processes (0–3 per piece)**
Sequential instructions that can be converted into tutorials, checklists,
or carousel content.

Extraction method: Identify every how-to section that presents steps
in order. Each becomes a potential carousel, video, or checklist.

**Stories and Examples (1–5 per piece)**
Narratives, case studies, or concrete examples that illustrate abstract
concepts.

Extraction method: Identify every anecdote, case study, or example that
brings a concept to life. These become video scripts, podcast talking
points, or social narratives.

### The Adaptation Matrix

| Quote | X | X | | | | |

### Platform-Specific Adaptation Rules

**LinkedIn Post Adaptation**
- Length: 1,200–1,500 characters for maximum engagement
- Open with the most provocative insight as a hook
- Use line breaks between every 1–2 sentences
- End with a question to prompt comments
- Link to source in first comment (not in post body, to avoid
  algorithm suppression of external links)

**Twitter/X Thread Adaptation**
- 6–12 tweets covering the post's key insights
- Tweet 1: Hook with the most compelling finding or claim
- Tweets 2–N: One insight or data point per tweet
- Final tweet: Summary + link to full source + CTA to retweet
- Number the tweets (1/8, 2/8, etc.) for readability

**Instagram/LinkedIn Carousel Adaptation**
- 5–10 slides covering a framework or process
- Slide 1: Hook headline (must stop the scroll)
- Slides 2–N: One concept per slide, visual-first design
- Final slide: CTA (save, share, follow, link in bio)
- Design consistency: Same color palette, font, layout per carousel
- Text per slide: Maximum 30 words

**Short-Form Video Adaptation (60–90 seconds)**
- Script based on one key insight or story from the source
- Hook in first 3 seconds (text overlay + spoken)
- Teaching or storytelling for the body (45–70 seconds)
- CTA in final 10 seconds (follow, comment, link in bio)
- Captions mandatory (85% of social video is watched without sound)

**Email Newsletter Adaptation**
- Feature one key insight as the email's main content
- 150–250 words summarizing the insight with a unique angle
- Link to the full source for readers who want depth
- Pair with 1–2 other content recommendations

**Infographic Adaptation**
- Visualize the source's data points and framework
- 4–8 sections with one data point or concept each
- Include source citations for all data
- Brand elements and CTA at the bottom
- Create both full-size and social-optimized dimensions

### Quality Preservation Rules

Every derivative must pass these checks:

**1. Standalone Value**
Would someone who never sees the original find this derivative
valuable? If the answer is no, the adaptation is too thin.

**2. Accuracy Preservation**
Are all facts, statistics, and claims accurate in their adapted
context? Summarization can inadvertently change meaning. Verify.

**3. Platform Optimization**
Is this derivative formatted for its target platform? A LinkedIn post
that reads like a tweet (too short) or a blog post (too long) fails
the platform test.

**4. Voice Consistency**
Does the derivative maintain brand voice? Platform adaptation changes
format, not personality. Casual is fine for social; off-brand is not.

**5. Attribution**
Does the derivative link back to or reference the source content?
Cross-linking serves both SEO (internal links) and audience
(deeper exploration).

## Stage 3: Distribute — Sequencing and Tracking

### Distribution Timeline

Do not publish all derivatives simultaneously. Stagger distribution
to maximize reach and avoid audience fatigue.

```
Day 0: Publish source content (blog, webinar, etc.)

Day 1: LinkedIn post (key insight #1)
Day 1: Email newsletter featuring the source

Day 2: Twitter thread (comprehensive summary)

Day 3: Instagram/LinkedIn carousel (framework)

Day 5: Short-form video (key insight with visual)

Day 7: LinkedIn post (key insight #2)
Day 7: Second email to non-openers with different subject line

Day 10: Quote graphics (series of 3, one per day)

Day 14: LinkedIn post (data point with commentary)

Day 21: Repurposed guest post pitch to external publication

Day 30: Performance review — double down on top performer format
```

### Tracking and Attribution

Every derivative must be trackable back to the source:

**UTM Parameters**
Use consistent UTM structure for all derivative links:
```
utm_source=[platform]
utm_medium=[format]
utm_campaign=[source-content-slug]
utm_content=[derivative-identifier]
```

Example:
```
https://example.com/blog/content-strategy-guide
?utm_source=linkedin
&utm_medium=carousel
&utm_campaign=content-strategy-guide
&utm_content=framework-carousel-v1
```

**Internal Tracking**
Maintain a repurposing log for each source content piece:

| Derivative | Platform | Publish Date | Link/Post URL | Views | Engagement | Clicks |
|-----------|----------|-------------|---------------|-------|------------|--------|
| LinkedIn insight #1 | LinkedIn | [Date] | [URL] | | | |
| Twitter thread | Twitter | [Date] | [URL] | | | |
| Carousel | Instagram | [Date] | [URL] | | | |

### Performance Analysis

At 30 days post-source-publication, analyze:
- Which derivative format drove the most traffic to the source?
- Which platform generated the most engagement?
- What is the total reach of all derivatives combined vs. the source
  alone?
- What is the incremental cost of repurposing vs. creating from scratch?

Use findings to optimize the repurposing mix for future content.

---

## Quality Criteria

A successful repurposing effort meets these standards:
- Minimum 8 derivatives produced per qualifying source piece
- Every derivative passes the standalone value test
- All derivatives published within 30 days of source publication
- Every derivative has UTM tracking
- Performance data collected and analyzed at 30-day mark
- Findings documented for future optimization

---

## Anti-Patterns

**The Copy-Paste Derivative**
Taking a paragraph from the blog post and posting it on LinkedIn
verbatim. This is not repurposing—it is lazy cross-posting that fails
the platform optimization test.

**The Derivative Flood**
Publishing all derivatives on the same day across all platforms. This
overwhelms followers who are on multiple platforms and wastes the
compounding effect of staggered distribution.

**The Source-Dependent Derivative**
Creating a teaser that only makes sense if you read the source. Every
derivative must deliver value independently, not just drive clicks.

**The Untracked Derivative**
Publishing derivatives without UTM parameters or tracking. Without
attribution, you cannot measure the ROI of repurposing or optimize the
process.

**The Quality Decay**
Each successive derivative is lower quality than the previous. Maintain
the same editorial standards for derivative #10 as for derivative #1.
