# Playtest Report Template

## What This Enables

This template provides a standardized structure for documenting playtest sessions and their findings. A well-written playtest report transforms subjective play observations into actionable design recommendations with clear severity ratings, evidence, and prioritization. The report serves as the bridge between player experience and design iteration.

| Severity | Definition | Action Required |
|----------|-----------|----------------|

| **Major** | Significantly degrades experience, affects 30%+ of players | Should fix before launch |
| **Minor** | Noticeable issue but does not significantly impact experience | Fix if time permits |
| **Suggestion** | Improvement idea, not a current problem | Evaluate for future iteration |

## Usage Notes

- Complete the report within 3 business days of the playtest session while observations are fresh
- Prioritize findings by severity x frequency -- a critical finding seen in 4/5 participants is the top priority
- Include direct player quotes to make findings vivid and compelling for the team
- Positive findings are not filler -- they prevent the team from changing things that work
- The emotional journey map is particularly valuable for narrative and pacing discussions
- Share the report with the entire team, not just designers -- engineers and artists have insights too
- Always compare to the previous playtest to track whether changes are improving the experience
