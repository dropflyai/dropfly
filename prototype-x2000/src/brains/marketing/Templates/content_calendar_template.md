### Content Production Workflow

```
Ideation → Brief → Draft → Edit → Design → Review → Publish → Distribute → Measure
  │          │        │       │       │        │         │          │           │
  Day 1    Day 2    Day 5   Day 7   Day 9   Day 10   Day 12    Day 12-19   Day 30
```

**Stage Definitions:**

| Stage | Description | Owner | SLA |
|-------|-------------|-------|-----|
| Ideation | Topic selection, keyword research, angle development | Content Lead | Weekly planning |
| Brief | Content brief with outline, target keyword, audience, CTA | Content Lead | 1 day |
| Draft | First draft written per brief specifications | Writer | 3-5 days |
| Edit | Copy editing, fact checking, SEO optimization | Editor | 2 days |
| Design | Visual assets, featured image, in-content graphics | Designer | 2 days |
| Review | Stakeholder review and final approval | Approver | 1 day |
| Publish | CMS upload, SEO metadata, internal links, formatting | Content Ops | 1 day |
| Distribute | Social, email, syndication, paid amplification | Distribution | Days 1-7 |
| Measure | Performance tracking, optimization decisions | Analytics | Day 7, 30, 90 |

### Content Request Process

| Step | Description | SLA |
|------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | Request submitted via form or Slack | Immediate |
| 2 | Content lead triages and prioritizes | Within 2 business days |
| 3 | Accepted requests added to backlog | Within 1 week |
| 4 | Backlog prioritized in weekly planning | Weekly |
| 5 | Scheduled content enters production pipeline | Per calendar cadence |
| 6 | Requester notified of publish date | When scheduled |

---

**The content calendar is a living document that balances strategic planning with
tactical flexibility. Plan themes and pillar content quarterly. Plan specific
pieces monthly. Leave 20% capacity for reactive and opportunistic content.
Measure ruthlessly. Double down on what works. Stop what does not.**
