# Marketing Brain — Patterns Directory

## What Are Marketing Patterns?

Patterns are reusable, evidence-based approaches to recurring marketing scenarios.
They encode institutional knowledge — the collective wisdom of what drives results
and what wastes resources in specific marketing contexts. Patterns are not rigid
playbooks; they are strategic frameworks that can be adapted to market conditions,
audience characteristics, and resource constraints while maintaining their core logic.

Marketing patterns differ from sales patterns in a critical way: marketing patterns
must account for lagging indicators, attribution ambiguity, and the interplay between
brand and performance activities. A sales pattern can measure its outcome in a single
deal cycle. A marketing pattern's full impact may take 6-18 months to materialize.

---

## Pattern Architecture

Each pattern follows a consistent structure:

1. **Context** — When does this pattern apply? What market conditions trigger it?
2. **Challenge** — What makes this scenario difficult? What are the common failure modes?
3. **Strategy** — What is the strategic approach? What frameworks govern decisions?
4. **Execution** — Phase-by-phase tactical implementation with timelines
5. **Metrics** — How do you measure success at each phase?
6. **Anti-Patterns** — Common mistakes that undermine the approach
7. **References** — Connections to other Marketing Brain modules

## How to Use Patterns

1. Identify the marketing scenario you are facing
2. Select the matching pattern from the table above
3. Review the full pattern before beginning execution
4. Adapt the execution phases to your specific market context, budget, and timeline
5. Establish measurement baselines before beginning (critical for attribution)
6. Document deviations and outcomes for future pattern refinement
7. If a scenario does not have a pattern and recurs 3+ times, create one

## Pattern Selection Guide

| Scenario | Recommended Pattern | Key Consideration |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| New product entering market | Product Launch Marketing | Requires cross-functional alignment with product, sales, CS |
| Quarterly pipeline target shortfall | Demand Gen Campaign | Lead time matters — campaigns need 4-8 weeks to produce pipeline |
| Market position has shifted | Brand Refresh | Do not confuse tactical messaging changes with strategic brand refresh |
| Entering new market segment | Product Launch Marketing + Demand Gen | Combine patterns: launch positioning then generate demand |
| Competitive displacement threat | Brand Refresh + Demand Gen | Shore up positioning first, then activate demand defense |
| Post-acquisition integration | Brand Refresh | Brand architecture decisions must precede tactical execution |

## Pattern Quality Criteria

A marketing pattern is considered valid when:
- It has been applied successfully in 3+ independent marketing contexts
- It addresses a scenario that recurs with meaningful frequency
- It provides specific, actionable guidance with timelines and resource estimates
- It has measurable success criteria at each phase (not just final outcomes)
- It accounts for attribution complexity and lagging indicators
- It has been reviewed against Marketing Brain methodology and frameworks
- It includes anti-patterns derived from real failure modes

---

## Adding New Patterns

When you identify a repeatable marketing approach that works:

1. Document it using the pattern structure defined above
2. Include at least 2 examples of successful application (anonymized)
3. Include at least 1 example of the anti-pattern (what failure looks like)
4. Specify the minimum budget, team size, and timeline for viable execution
5. Define leading indicators that predict pattern success or failure
6. Validate with at least one peer or marketing leader
7. Submit for inclusion in this directory
8. Update this README with the new entry in the Available Patterns table

## Pattern Interaction Model

Marketing patterns frequently interact and reinforce each other:

```
Product Launch Marketing ──────→ Demand Gen Campaign
        │                              │
        │ (positioning feeds           │ (demand validates
        │  campaign messaging)         │  launch positioning)
        │                              │
        └──────→ Brand Refresh ←───────┘
                 (market feedback
                  triggers refresh)
```

Understanding these interactions prevents isolated execution that misses
compounding effects.

## Relationship to Templates

Patterns define **what** to do strategically and **why**.
Templates define **how** to execute tactically.

Each pattern references specific Templates for execution:
