# Product Launch Marketing Pattern — Bringing New Products to Market

## Context

This pattern applies when:
- A new product, major feature, or significant version is being released to market
- The launch requires coordinated marketing, sales, and product alignment
- External market awareness and pipeline generation are primary objectives
- Launch timing is fixed (tied to product readiness, event, or competitive pressure)
- The launch represents a material change in company positioning or capabilities
- Internal enablement is required before external announcement

Product launches are the highest-stakes, most cross-functional marketing activity.
They are also the most frequently mismanaged because companies confuse "shipping a
feature" with "launching a product." A launch is a go-to-market event, not a
product event. The product team ships; the marketing team launches.

---

## Challenge

Product launches fail for specific, predictable reasons:

1. **No launch tier classification** — Every feature gets the same treatment,
   diluting major launches and over-investing in minor ones
2. **Messaging before positioning** — Teams write copy before defining who the
   product is for and why it matters, resulting in feature-centric messaging
3. **Sales is not enabled** — The market hears about the product before sales can
   articulate value, leading to lost pipeline from early interest
4. **Channel saturation without sequencing** — All channels fire simultaneously with
   no narrative arc, creating a single-day spike instead of sustained awareness
5. **No measurement plan** — Success is declared by press coverage or social engagement
   without connecting to pipeline or revenue impact
6. **Internal launch neglected** — Employees learn about the launch from LinkedIn
   instead of internal briefings, eroding trust and alignment

---

## Strategy

### Strategic Principle 1: Tier Your Launches

Not every release deserves a full launch. Tiering prevents launch fatigue and ensures
resource allocation matches business impact.

| Tier | Definition | Investment | Cadence |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Tier 1: Flagship | New product, new category, or transformational capability | Full cross-functional launch (8-12 weeks, $50-200K+) | 1-2 per year |
| Tier 2: Major | Significant new feature or expansion of existing product | Moderate launch (4-6 weeks, $10-50K) | 3-6 per year |
| Tier 3: Minor | Feature update, improvement, or iterative enhancement | Lightweight launch (1-2 weeks, <$10K) | Monthly |
| Tier 4: Maintenance | Bug fixes, small improvements, housekeeping | Release notes only | Ongoing |

### Strategic Principle 2: Position Before Message

The positioning work must be complete before any messaging is written:
1. Define the target customer (who cares most about this?)
2. Define the competitive alternative (what do they use today?)
3. Define the unique value (what can they do now that they could not before?)
4. Define the category (how should buyers think about this?)
5. THEN write messaging that communicates the positioning

### Strategic Principle 3: Sequence for Narrative Arc

A launch is a story told over weeks, not a single announcement:
- **Pre-launch**: Build anticipation and educate the market about the problem
- **Launch day**: Announce the solution with maximum coordination
- **Post-launch**: Sustain momentum with depth content, customer stories, and demand gen

### Strategic Principle 4: Enable Before You Announce

Sales must be fully enabled before the market hears about the product:
- Battle cards updated, new demo scripts prepared, objection handling trained
- Customer success briefed on onboarding and support implications
- Partner teams briefed on co-sell and co-marketing opportunities

## Execution

### Phase 1: Planning and Positioning (T-8 to T-6 Weeks)

**Objective:** Align cross-functional team, establish positioning, define success.

| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Assign launch tier (1-4) | Product Marketing | Tier classification document |
| Define target audience and use case | Product Marketing | Audience brief |
| Complete positioning exercise (Dunford framework) | Product Marketing | Positioning document |
| Competitive analysis update | Competitive Intel | Updated battle cards |
| Define launch KPIs and measurement plan | Marketing Ops | KPI dashboard |
| Establish launch timeline with all stakeholders | Program Manager | Launch calendar |
| Budget allocation and approval | Marketing Leadership | Approved budget |

**Exit Criteria:** Positioning approved by CMO/VP Marketing. Launch tier confirmed.
Target audience defined and validated. Budget approved. Timeline locked.

### Phase 2: Content and Asset Creation (T-6 to T-3 Weeks)

**Objective:** Create all assets needed for launch across every channel.

**Messaging Development:**
- Core messaging document (positioning statement, value pillars, proof points)
- Audience-specific messaging variants (by persona, by segment)
- Competitive differentiators and objection responses
- Internal FAQ document

**Content Assets (Tier 1 Launch):**

| Asset | Purpose | Channel |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Blog post (announcement) | Primary narrative | Website, social, email |
| Product page (new or updated) | SEO, conversion | Website |
| Demo video (2-3 min) | Visual explanation | Website, YouTube, social |
| Customer testimonial (if available) | Social proof | All channels |
| Press release | Media coverage | Wire, media outreach |
| Analyst briefing materials | Analyst validation | 1:1 briefings |
| Sales deck (updated) | Sales conversations | Internal |
| One-pager / data sheet | Leave-behind | Sales, partner |
| Email sequence (3-5 emails) | Nurture and announcement | Email |
| Social content (10-15 posts) | Awareness and engagement | LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| Webinar (launch event) | Deep-dive education | Registration landing page |
| Paid ad creative (3-5 variants) | Demand generation | LinkedIn, Google, etc. |

**Exit Criteria:** All Tier 1 assets created, reviewed, and approved. Legal review
complete (if required). Assets staged in CMS, email tool, and social scheduler.

### Phase 3: Internal Enablement (T-3 to T-1 Weeks)

**Objective:** Every customer-facing team is equipped to speak about the product.

| Audience | Enablement Activity | Deliverable |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Sales (AEs) | Live training session (45 min) + recording | Battle card, demo script, talk track |
| SDRs/BDRs | Prospecting brief (30 min) + email templates | Outreach sequences, qualifying questions |
| Customer Success | Impact briefing (30 min) + customer FAQ | Customer communication template |
| Support | Technical FAQ (30 min) + knowledge base update | KB articles, ticket routing rules |
| Partners | Partner webinar or briefing (30 min) | Partner collateral kit |
| All Employees | Company all-hands or Slack announcement | Internal FAQ, social sharing kit |

**Exit Criteria:** Sales certification quiz passed (>80%). All customer-facing teams
briefed. Partner kit distributed. Internal announcement sent.

### Phase 4: Pre-Launch (T-1 Week)

**Objective:** Build anticipation and seed the narrative before announcement.

| Action | Timing | Channel |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Media briefings (under embargo) | T-5 to T-3 days | 1:1 journalist calls |
| Analyst briefings | T-5 to T-3 days | 1:1 analyst calls |
| Customer advisory board preview | T-3 days | Email or call |
| Teaser social posts | T-3 to T-1 days | LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| Teaser email to subscribers | T-2 days | Email |
| SDR outbound blitz preparation | T-1 day | CRM sequences staged |
| Final QA on all assets and links | T-1 day | All channels |

**Exit Criteria:** Embargoed coverage confirmed. Analyst feedback incorporated.
All assets QA'd and staged. Social posts scheduled. Email sequences armed.

### Phase 5: Launch Day (T-0)

**Objective:** Maximum coordinated impact across all channels simultaneously.

**Launch Day Sequence:**
1. 6:00 AM — Press release goes live on wire
2. 6:00 AM — Blog post published, product page live
3. 6:30 AM — Email blast to full subscriber list
4. 7:00 AM — Social posts begin (founder, company, executives)
5. 7:00 AM — Paid campaigns go live
6. 8:00 AM — SDR outbound blitz begins (targeted accounts)
7. 9:00 AM — Partner co-announcements go live
8. 10:00 AM — Internal Slack celebration + social sharing reminder
9. 12:00 PM — Executive LinkedIn posts (personal accounts)
10. 2:00 PM — Community post (if applicable)
11. End of Day — War room debrief: initial metrics review

**Exit Criteria:** All channels activated. No broken links or errors. Initial
engagement metrics captured. Media coverage tracking initiated.

### Phase 6: Post-Launch Sustain (T+1 to T+30 Days)

**Objective:** Convert launch awareness into sustained demand and pipeline.

| Week | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Week 1 | Follow-up blog (deeper dive) | SEO, depth content |
| Week 1 | Social proof posts (early reactions) | Credibility |
| Week 2 | Launch webinar (live event) | Mid-funnel conversion |
| Week 2 | Customer spotlight (early adopter) | Social proof |
| Week 3 | Analyst report or review | Third-party validation |
| Week 3 | Paid retargeting ramp-up | Convert launch visitors |
| Week 4 | Performance review and optimization | Data-driven adjustment |
| Week 4 | Launch retrospective | Process improvement |

## Metrics

### Launch Metrics by Phase

| Metric | Pre-Launch | Launch Day | Post-Launch (30 days) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| Media coverage (articles) | Embargoed commitments | Published articles | Total coverage |
| Social engagement | Teaser engagement | Launch day impressions + engagement | Cumulative reach |
| Website traffic | Baseline | Launch day spike | Sustained lift vs. baseline |
| Email engagement | Teaser open/click rates | Blast open/click rates | Sequence completion rate |
| Sign-ups / trials | Baseline | Launch day sign-ups | 30-day total sign-ups |
| Demo requests | Baseline | Launch day requests | 30-day total requests |
| Pipeline created | Pre-launch pipeline | Launch-attributed pipeline | 30-day pipeline total |
| Sales adoption | Enablement completion rate | First week usage | 30-day attachment rate |

### Launch Scorecard (Post-Launch Assessment)

| Dimension | Score (1-5) | Evidence |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Market Awareness Impact | | Coverage, social reach, branded search lift |
| Pipeline Impact | | Pipeline created within 30 days |
| Sales Enablement Effectiveness | | Rep adoption, win rate on launch deals |
| Internal Alignment | | Survey: did teams feel prepared? |
| Execution Quality | | On-time delivery, no critical errors |
| Process Efficiency | | Retrospective feedback, resource utilization |

## Anti-Patterns

### Anti-Pattern 1: The Feature Dump
**Symptom:** Launch messaging lists every feature without connecting to customer value.
**Consequence:** Buyers cannot understand why they should care; message is forgettable.
**Fix:** Lead with the customer problem and outcome, not the feature list.

### Anti-Pattern 2: The One-Day Wonder
**Symptom:** Massive investment in launch day, zero investment in post-launch sustain.
**Consequence:** Traffic spike followed by immediate decay; no sustained pipeline impact.
**Fix:** Allocate 40% of launch budget to post-launch sustain activities.

### Anti-Pattern 3: The Stealth Launch
**Symptom:** Product ships with a blog post and nothing else; no cross-functional effort.
**Consequence:** Market does not notice; sales cannot articulate value; opportunity wasted.
**Fix:** Apply launch tier classification; even Tier 2 launches deserve structured execution.

### Anti-Pattern 4: The Internal Surprise
**Symptom:** Sales and CS learn about the launch from social media or customer questions.
**Consequence:** Loss of trust, missed pipeline, poor customer experience on early inquiries.
**Fix:** Internal enablement must be complete 1+ week before external announcement.

### Anti-Pattern 5: The Vanity Launch
**Symptom:** Success measured by press clips and social likes, never connected to pipeline.
**Consequence:** Marketing team celebrates, revenue team sees no impact, credibility erodes.
**Fix:** Define pipeline and revenue KPIs before launch; track through 90-day attribution window.
