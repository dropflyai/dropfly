# Campaign Launch Pattern

> A structured, repeatable pattern for planning, creating, executing, and measuring marketing campaigns of any type -- from product launches to seasonal promotions to brand awareness plays.

---

## Context

This pattern applies when you need to launch a bounded marketing campaign with a defined start date, end date, budget, and measurable objective. It covers the full lifecycle from strategic planning through post-campaign analysis.

**Use this pattern for:**
- Product or feature launch campaigns
- Seasonal or event-driven promotions
- Brand awareness or repositioning campaigns
- Performance marketing campaigns (paid acquisition)
- Partnership or co-marketing campaigns

**Do not use for:** Always-on programs (SEO, nurture) -- those are engines, not campaigns.

---

## Challenge

Campaign launches fail for predictable reasons: unclear objectives, insufficient lead time, creative misalignment with audience, poor channel selection, inadequate measurement, and missing post-campaign learning capture. This pattern addresses each failure mode with a structured phase gate approach.

---

## Phase 1: Strategic Planning (Week 1-2)

### 1.1 Campaign Brief Development

Define the campaign foundation before any creative work begins.

**Required elements:**
- **Objective:** One primary objective (awareness, leads, revenue, engagement). Secondary objectives acceptable but ranked.
- **Target audience:** Specific segments with demographic, psychographic, and behavioral attributes. Reference existing personas or create campaign-specific ones.
- **Key message:** The single most important takeaway for the audience. Support with 2-3 proof points.
- **Success metrics:** Primary KPI with target number, plus 2-3 secondary KPIs. Include baselines from previous campaigns.
- **Budget:** Total budget with allocation percentages by channel (paid media, creative production, tools, agency fees).
- **Timeline:** Start date, end date, key milestones, dependencies.

### 1.2 Competitive Landscape Scan

Before finalizing the campaign concept:
- Review competitor campaigns from the past 90 days.
- Identify messaging gaps and opportunities for differentiation.
- Note channel strategies competitors are using or ignoring.
- Document creative approaches that are working in the market.

### 1.3 Audience Segmentation

Refine targeting beyond basic demographics:
- **Behavioral segments:** Based on product usage, website activity, or purchase history.
- **Intent signals:** Search behavior, content consumption, competitor engagement.
- **Lifecycle stage:** New prospect, known lead, existing customer, churned customer.
- **Channel preference:** Where this audience spends time, how they prefer to be reached.

## Phase 2: Creative Development (Week 2-4)

### 2.1 Concept Development

Generate 3-5 creative concepts that:
- Align with the campaign brief and key message.
- Are adaptable across multiple channels and formats.
- Have a clear visual and verbal identity distinct from always-on marketing.
- Include a strong call-to-action appropriate to the campaign objective.

**Concept evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Message clarity | 25% |
| Audience relevance | 25% |
| Creative differentiation | 20% |
| Channel adaptability | 15% |
| Production feasibility | 15% |

### 2.2 Asset Production

**Core assets (required for every campaign):**
- Hero visual or video (primary creative asset).
- Headline and body copy variations (minimum 3 each).
- Landing page or dedicated campaign destination.
- Email creative (announcement + follow-up sequences).
- Social media assets (platform-specific dimensions and formats).

**Extended assets (based on channel plan):**
- Paid media creative (display, search, social ad formats).
- Blog post or long-form content.
- Sales enablement materials (one-pager, talk track).
- PR or media materials (press release, media kit).

### 2.3 Creative Review and Approval

**Review process:**
1. Internal creative team review (design + copy alignment).
2. Marketing leadership review (strategy alignment).
3. Legal/compliance review (claims, disclaimers, regulatory).
4. Stakeholder review (product, sales, executive if needed).
5. Final approval with sign-off documented.

Build in 3-5 business days for each review round. Expect 2 rounds minimum.

## Phase 3: Channel Selection and Planning (Week 3-4)

### 3.1 Channel Prioritization Framework

Score each potential channel:

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Audience presence | Is the target audience active on this channel? |
| Message fit | Does the channel support the message format and depth? |
| Cost efficiency | What is the expected CPM/CPC/CPA relative to budget? |
| Measurement capability | Can you track performance end-to-end? |
| Competitive density | How crowded is this channel with competitor messaging? |
| Historical performance | How has this channel performed for similar campaigns? |

### 3.2 Channel-Specific Planning

**Paid media:** Budget allocation per channel, audience targeting parameters, bid strategy, creative rotation plan, negative targeting.

**Owned channels:** Email segmentation and send schedule, website placements, blog publishing schedule, social media organic posting calendar, in-product messaging.

**Earned channels:** PR outreach plan, influencer or partner activation, community seeding strategy, customer advocacy activation.

### 3.3 Attribution Model Selection

Choose the attribution model before launch:
- **First-touch:** Best for awareness campaigns (understanding discovery channels).
- **Last-touch:** Best for conversion campaigns with short sales cycles.
- **Multi-touch linear:** Best for nurture campaigns where multiple touchpoints matter equally.
- **Multi-touch time-decay:** Best for longer sales cycles where recent touches matter more.
- **W-shaped:** Best for complex B2B where first touch, lead creation, and opportunity creation all matter.

## Phase 4: Execution (Campaign Duration)

### 4.1 Launch Sequence

**T-minus 7 days:**
- All creative assets finalized and approved.
- Landing pages live in staging, tested across devices and browsers.
- Tracking pixels, UTM parameters, and conversion events verified.
- Email sequences loaded and tested.
- Paid media campaigns built in platforms, set to draft/paused.

**T-minus 3 days:**
- Final QA on all assets and links.
- Sales team briefed on campaign messaging and expected lead flow.
- Customer success team briefed on customer-facing changes.
- War room or Slack channel set up for real-time monitoring.

**Launch day:**
- Activate paid campaigns in sequence (stagger by 1-2 hours to monitor).
- Send launch emails per segmented schedule.
- Publish organic social posts.
- Push live on website (banners, landing pages, CTAs).
- Monitor first 4 hours closely for technical issues.
- Send end-of-day performance snapshot to stakeholders.

### 4.2 In-Flight Optimization

**Daily checks (first 7 days, then 2-3x weekly):**
- Spend pacing vs. budget plan.
- CTR and conversion rates by channel, audience, and creative.
- Landing page performance (bounce rate, time on page, form completion).
- Email engagement (open rate, click rate, unsubscribe rate).
- Lead quality feedback from sales (if applicable).

**Optimization actions:**
- Pause underperforming creative; increase budget on winners.
- Adjust audience targeting based on early performance data.
- Refresh creative if fatigue sets in (CTR declining over time).
- A/B test landing page elements (headline, CTA, form length).
- Reallocate budget between channels based on CPA efficiency.

### 4.3 Escalation Triggers

Immediately escalate if:
- Spend pacing exceeds budget by more than 15% without corresponding performance.
- Conversion rates drop below 50% of baseline for 48+ hours.
- Negative sentiment or PR issues surface related to campaign messaging.
- Technical failures (broken links, tracking issues, landing page downtime).
- Legal or compliance concerns raised post-launch.

## Phase 5: Measurement and Post-Campaign Review

### 5.1 Performance Reporting

Report within 7 days of campaign end:

| Metric Category | Key Metrics |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Reach | Impressions, unique reach, share of voice |
| Engagement | CTR, social engagement rate, video completion rate |
| Conversion | Leads generated, conversion rate, cost per lead |
| Revenue | Pipeline generated, revenue attributed, ROAS |
| Efficiency | CPC, CPM, CPA, cost per MQL, cost per SQL |
| Brand | Brand lift (if measured), sentiment change |

### 5.2 Post-Campaign Review Meeting

**Agenda (45 minutes):**
1. Results vs. objectives (5 min)
2. Channel performance analysis (10 min)
3. Creative performance analysis (10 min)
4. Audience insights (5 min)
5. Process and execution review (5 min)
6. Learnings and recommendations (5 min)
7. Action items (5 min)

### 5.3 Knowledge Capture

Document and store:
- Final performance data with benchmarks for future campaigns.
- Top-performing creative assets with context on why they worked.
- Audience insights that should inform future targeting.
- Process improvements for next campaign execution.
- Budget allocation recommendations based on channel ROI.

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Launching without clear KPIs | Cannot optimize what you cannot measure | Define primary KPI before creative starts |
| Creative-first planning | Beautiful ads reaching the wrong people | Strategy and audience first, creative follows |
| Set-and-forget execution | Performance degrades without optimization | Build daily monitoring into first week |
| Single-channel dependence | Concentration risk if channel underperforms | Minimum 3 channels for significant campaigns |
| Skipping post-campaign review | Same mistakes repeated, no compounding learning | Non-negotiable review within 7 days |
| Over-optimizing too early | Insufficient data leads to false signals | Wait for statistical significance before major changes |

## Customization by Campaign Type

| Campaign Type | Planning Duration | Key Emphasis |
|--------------|------------------|--------------|
| Product launch | 8-12 weeks | Cross-functional coordination, PR, sales enablement |
| Seasonal promotion | 4-6 weeks | Timing, creative urgency, promotional offer |
| Brand awareness | 6-10 weeks | Reach, frequency, brand lift measurement |
| Performance/lead gen | 4-6 weeks | Targeting, conversion optimization, CPA efficiency |
| Event marketing | 6-8 weeks | Registration funnel, content, follow-up sequence |
