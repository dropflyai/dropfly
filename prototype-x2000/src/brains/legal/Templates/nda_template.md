## NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement")** is entered into as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**[PARTY A LEGAL NAME]**, a [STATE/JURISDICTION] [ENTITY TYPE], with its principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("Party A"),

and

**[PARTY B LEGAL NAME]**, a [STATE/JURISDICTION] [ENTITY TYPE], with its principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("Party B").

> *ANNOTATION: Use exact legal entity names as they appear in formation documents. Verify jurisdiction of incorporation. For individuals, use full legal name and state of residence.*

Party A and Party B are each referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

---

### 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship concerning [DESCRIBE PURPOSE] (the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose to the other certain confidential and proprietary information. The Parties desire to provide for the protection of such information.

> *ANNOTATION: Define the Purpose narrowly enough to limit use but broadly enough to cover all relevant discussions. A vague Purpose makes enforcement harder. A Purpose that is too narrow may require a new NDA for related discussions.*

---

### 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**2.1** "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public, confidential, or proprietary information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to:

(a) Trade secrets, inventions, ideas, processes, formulas, source and object code, data, programs, software, other works of authorship, know-how, improvements, discoveries, developments, designs, and techniques;

(b) Information regarding plans for research, development, new products, marketing and selling, business plans, budgets and unpublished financial statements, licenses, prices and costs, margins, discounts, credit terms, pricing and billing policies, quoting procedures, methods of obtaining business, forecasts, future plans and potential strategies, financial projections and business strategies;

(c) Information regarding the skills and compensation of employees, contractors, suppliers, and agents of the Disclosing Party;

(d) Information regarding customers, including customer lists, customer contacts, and customer data; and

(e) The existence and terms of this Agreement.

**2.2** Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Was publicly known at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly known through no wrongful act or omission of the Receiving Party;

(b) Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as demonstrated by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as demonstrated by written records;

(d) Is received from a third party who is not under any obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates with the Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain a protective order.

> *ANNOTATION: The exclusions in 2.2 are standard and generally non-negotiable. Pay close attention to (e) -- the compelled disclosure exception should always require notice to the disclosing party. Some parties try to add a "residuals" clause allowing use of general ideas retained in memory; resist this in sensitive contexts.*

---

### 3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

**3.1** The Receiving Party shall:

(a) Hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described herein;

(d) Protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that the Receiving Party uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(e) Limit access to the Confidential Information to those employees, contractors, and advisors ("Representatives") who have a need to know in connection with the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained herein.

**3.2** The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

> *ANNOTATION: Clause 3.1(d) is the standard of care provision. "Reasonable care" is the minimum acceptable standard. Some parties negotiate for a higher standard ("best efforts" or "same degree of care as own most valuable information"). The Receiving Party should avoid accepting a standard higher than it actually maintains.*

---

### 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1** This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall remain in effect for a period of [TWO (2) YEARS / THREE (3) YEARS] from the Effective Date, unless terminated earlier by either Party upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party.

**4.2** The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Agreement shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of [THREE (3) YEARS / FIVE (5) YEARS] from the date of disclosure of the Confidential Information.

**4.3** For trade secrets, the obligations of confidentiality shall continue for as long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

> *ANNOTATION: The term (4.1) governs how long new disclosures can be made. The survival period (4.2) governs how long protection lasts after disclosure. For sensitive information, negotiate for a 5-year survival or indefinite protection of trade secrets (4.3). Many companies accept 2-3 year survival for general business information.*

---

### 5. RETURN OF MATERIALS

Upon termination of this Agreement or upon request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

(a) Return to the Disclosing Party all documents, materials, and other tangible manifestations of Confidential Information; or

(b) Destroy all such materials and certify such destruction in writing;

(c) Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information (i) in automated backup systems in the ordinary course of business, provided such copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations herein, and (ii) as required by law or regulation.

> *ANNOTATION: The backup carve-out in (c)(i) is a practical necessity in modern IT environments. Without it, the return obligation would be technically impossible to fulfill completely. Ensure the retained copies remain subject to confidentiality.*

---

### 6. NO RIGHTS GRANTED

**6.1** Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any intellectual property rights in or to the Confidential Information.

**6.2** Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to enter into any further agreement or transaction.

**6.3** Nothing in this Agreement restricts either Party from engaging in business activities that may compete with the other Party, provided that such activities do not involve the use of the other Party's Confidential Information.

> *ANNOTATION: Clause 6.3 is important for parties that operate in overlapping markets. Without it, the NDA could be argued to create an implied non-compete. Technology companies in particular should insist on this clause.*

---

### 7. REMEDIES

The Parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be inadequate to compensate for a breach of this Agreement. Accordingly, each Party agrees that the other Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity. Such remedies shall not be deemed to be the exclusive remedies for a breach of this Agreement but shall be in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

> *ANNOTATION: This clause preserves the right to seek an injunction (court order to stop the breach) without having to prove monetary damages first. This is standard and important -- by the time you can prove monetary damages from a confidentiality breach, the damage is often irreparable.*

---

### 8. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**8.1** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [STATE], without regard to its conflicts of law provisions.

**8.2** Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved in the [state/federal] courts located in [COUNTY, STATE], and each Party consents to the exclusive jurisdiction of such courts.

> *ANNOTATION: Choose a governing law jurisdiction that is favorable or neutral. Delaware is common for corporate matters. The party's home jurisdiction is standard. For international NDAs, consider arbitration (e.g., ICC or AAA rules) instead of litigation.*

---

### 9. MISCELLANEOUS

**9.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous oral or written agreements.

**9.2 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

**9.3 Waiver.** No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the Party granting such waiver.

**9.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**9.5 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed original signatures.

**9.6 Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

## Customization Notes

| Scenario | Customization |
|----------|--------------|
| Unilateral NDA | Remove mutual obligations; define one party as Disclosing, other as Receiving |
| Technology evaluation | Add provision that no reverse engineering is permitted |
| Employee NDA | Add invention assignment provisions; reference employment agreement |
| Pre-acquisition NDA | Add standstill provisions; consider non-solicitation of employees |
| International | Add data protection provisions if personal data may be shared; consider arbitration |
