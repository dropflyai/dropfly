# Legal Brain -- Patterns Index

> Reusable legal analysis workflows for recurring legal scenarios. Each pattern provides a structured approach to common legal challenges with decision frameworks, risk assessment gates, and compliance checkpoints.

> **DISCLAIMER:** All content in this directory is for educational and informational purposes only. It does not constitute legal advice. Always consult qualified legal counsel for specific legal matters.

---

## How to Use Patterns

1. Identify the legal scenario you are facing.
2. Select the matching pattern from the table above.
3. Read the full pattern before beginning work.
4. Identify the applicable jurisdiction(s) before proceeding.
5. Adapt the steps to your specific legal context, industry, and risk profile.
6. Apply the risk classification at every decision point (LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL).
7. Flag items requiring human attorney review.
8. Document deviations and outcomes in `Memory/README.md`.

---

## Pattern Quality Standards

Every legal pattern must:
- Identify applicable jurisdictions and note where law varies.
- Include risk classification gates at key decision points.
- Reference relevant statutes, regulations, or legal principles.
- Include anti-patterns derived from common legal mistakes.
- Flag when human attorney review is mandatory.
- Include the educational-purposes disclaimer.

## When to Create a New Pattern

Create a new legal pattern when:
- A legal analysis scenario repeats across 3+ engagements.
- The workflow involves 5+ distinct decision points.
- Getting the analysis wrong creates material legal risk.
- The pattern would reduce time-to-analysis for future matters.

---

**Legal patterns encode analytical discipline. They ensure no critical step is skipped when the stakes are high. Use them as structured checklists, adapt them to context, and always apply professional judgment.**
