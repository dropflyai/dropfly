# Partner Launch Pattern — From Signed Agreement to First Revenue

## Context

**Use this pattern when:** A partnership agreement has been signed and the partnership needs to be operationalized — moving from a contractual relationship to a functioning, revenue-generating partnership.

**Problem it solves:** The period between signing and first revenue is the highest-risk phase of any partnership. Without a structured launch process, partnerships stall in the onboarding phase, momentum built during negotiations dissipates, and both teams lose confidence in the partnership's viability.

**Typical duration:** 60-120 days from agreement signing to first partner-sourced revenue.

---

## The Pattern

### Phase 1: Internal Alignment (Days 1-10)

**Objective:** Ensure your own organization is ready to support the partnership.

**Step 1.1: Internal announcement**
- Notify all stakeholder teams (sales, marketing, product, engineering, support) of the new partnership
- Share the partnership overview, value proposition, and what each team needs to do
- Identify the internal champion for each functional area

**Step 1.2: System setup**
- Create the partner record in CRM and partner portal
- Configure deal registration workflow for the new partner
- Set up shared communication channels (Slack Connect, shared email alias)
- Grant partner portal access to the partner's designated users

**Step 1.3: Enablement preparation**
- Prepare partner-specific onboarding materials (customized to the partner's market and capabilities)
- Schedule the onboarding cadence (kickoff, training sessions, certification timeline)
- Assign partner manager and technical contact

### Phase 2: Partner Onboarding (Days 10-30)

**Objective:** Equip the partner team with the knowledge and tools to represent your product effectively.

**Step 2.1: Partnership kickoff meeting**
- Joint meeting with executive sponsors, partner managers, and key functional leads from both organizations
- Review the joint business plan and year-one objectives
- Confirm roles, responsibilities, and communication cadences
- Celebrate the partnership launch (this is a relationship moment, not just a project milestone)

**Step 2.2: Sales enablement**
- Product training for the partner's sales team (positioning, value proposition, competitive differentiation)
- Demo environment setup and training
- Sales tool distribution (battlecards, proposal templates, pricing guides)
- Joint ICP definition (which customers to target first)

**Step 2.3: Technical enablement**
- Integration setup and verification (ensure the technical integration works in the partner's environment)
- Technical training for the partner's implementation team
- Support escalation path setup and testing
- Sandbox and demo environment configuration

**Step 2.4: Marketing enablement**
- Co-branded materials creation (solution brief, landing page, email templates)
- Partnership announcement plan (press release, blog post, social media)
- Co-marketing calendar for the first quarter

### Phase 3: Activation (Days 30-60)

**Objective:** Generate the first pipeline and close the first deal.

**Step 3.1: Target account identification**
- Jointly identify 10-20 target accounts for the first co-selling wave
- For each account, define the approach (who leads, what value proposition, timing)
- Register the first batch of deals

**Step 3.2: First co-selling motion**
- Conduct joint discovery calls with target accounts
- Provide real-time deal support (answer questions, join calls, provide materials)
- Weekly pipeline check-ins to monitor progress and remove blockers

**Step 3.3: Partnership announcement**
- Execute the co-marketing announcement (blog, press release, social media)
- Launch the joint solution page
- Notify existing customers of the new partnership and integration

**Step 3.4: First deal close**
- Provide intensive support for the first 3-5 deals
- Document the first customer win as a case study
- Celebrate the first deal with both teams (momentum matters)

### Phase 4: Stabilization (Days 60-120)

**Objective:** Establish repeatable processes and transition from launch mode to steady-state operations.

**Step 4.1: Process validation**
- Review the deal registration, lead routing, and support escalation processes based on real-world experience
- Identify and fix process gaps that surfaced during the first deals
- Document updated processes

**Step 4.2: First QBR**
- Conduct the first quarterly business review at the 90-day mark
- Review performance against the joint business plan
- Adjust targets and activities based on first-quarter learnings
- Confirm the cadence and rhythm for ongoing operations

**Step 4.3: Operational handoff**
- Transition from launch-intensive support to standard operating cadence
- Ensure the partner team can operate independently for routine activities
- Establish the ongoing communication rhythm (weekly syncs, monthly reviews)

## Key Milestones

| Milestone | Target Day | Success Criteria |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Internal systems configured | Day 5 | CRM, portal, communication channels live |
| Kickoff meeting completed | Day 14 | All key stakeholders attended, JBP reviewed |
| Sales enablement complete | Day 25 | Partner sales team trained, tools distributed |
| Technical integration verified | Day 30 | Integration working in partner environment |
| Partnership announced | Day 35 | Public announcement published, solution page live |
| First deal registered | Day 40 | At least 1 deal registered in the partner portal |
| First deal closed | Day 90 | At least 1 partner-sourced deal closed |
| First QBR completed | Day 90 | Full QBR with performance review and next-quarter plan |
| Steady-state operations | Day 120 | Partner operating independently in standard cadence |

## Common Variations

**Variation 1: Technology-first launch**
When the partnership is primarily a technology integration, Phase 2 emphasis shifts to technical enablement. The integration must be live and stable before commercial activities begin. Add 2-4 weeks to the timeline for integration development and testing.

**Variation 2: Channel partner launch**
When launching a channel/reseller partner, Phase 2 emphasizes sales enablement and certification. The partner must demonstrate selling capability before receiving full commercial benefits. Add certification requirements before deal registration is enabled.

**Variation 3: Strategic alliance launch**
When launching a strategic alliance, Phase 1 expands to include executive alignment sessions and joint strategy workshops. Phase 3 may focus on co-development rather than immediate co-selling. The timeline extends to 120-180 days.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- **Launch without internal alignment**: Signing the agreement and immediately going to market without preparing internal teams — producing confusion and dropped balls when the first partner-sourced lead arrives
- **Training without practice**: Enabling the partner team through classroom training without supervised co-selling practice — producing partners who know the product but cannot sell it
- **Delayed announcement**: Waiting months to announce the partnership, losing the momentum window and signaling lack of commitment
- **Premature autonomy**: Withdrawing launch support before the partner has demonstrated the ability to operate independently — assuming competence before verifying it
- **Skipping the first QBR**: Treating the first quarter as a grace period without formal review — allowing early problems to become established habits

---

## Success Criteria

The partner launch is successful when:
- [ ] First partner-sourced deal closed within 90 days
- [ ] Partner team can independently register deals, conduct demos, and handle basic customer questions
- [ ] Communication cadence is established and both teams are engaged
- [ ] Technical integration is live and stable
- [ ] At least one co-marketing activity has been executed
- [ ] The first QBR has been completed with a clear next-quarter plan
- [ ] Both executive sponsors have engaged at least once during the launch period
