# Video Ad Pattern — Creating Performance Video Advertisements

## Context

This pattern governs the creation of video advertisements designed for paid distribution. Video ads operate under fundamentally different constraints than organic content: they must capture attention in hostile contexts (viewers trying to skip), communicate value in compressed timeframes, and drive measurable action. This pattern covers creative strategy, scriptwriting, production, testing, and optimization for video ads across YouTube, Meta (Facebook/Instagram), TikTok, LinkedIn, and CTV platforms.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Campaign brief from Marketing Brain or campaign owner (objective, audience, budget, KPIs)
- [ ] Target audience defined with platform-specific targeting parameters
- [ ] Landing page or conversion destination ready and tested
- [ ] Ad account set up with tracking pixels and conversion events configured
- [ ] Budget allocated for production AND media spend (minimum 3:1 media-to-production ratio)
- [ ] Brand guidelines reviewed for ad-specific constraints
- [ ] Competitive ad research completed (Facebook Ad Library, YouTube Ad Transparency)

## Phase 1: Creative Strategy (Week 1)

### 1.1 Campaign Objective Alignment

| Campaign Objective | Ad Type | Primary Metric | Creative Focus |
|-------------------|---------|---------------|---------------|
| Brand awareness | Pre-roll, bumper, in-feed | CPM, brand lift, VTR | Emotion, story, brand recall |
| Consideration | In-stream, in-feed | CPV, VTR, engagement | Education, demonstration, social proof |
| Conversion | In-feed, retargeting | CPA, ROAS, CTR | Direct response, urgency, clear CTA |
| App install | In-feed, pre-roll | CPI, install rate | Demo, benefit, one-tap install |
| Lead generation | In-feed, sponsored | CPL, lead quality | Value exchange, form simplicity |

### 1.2 Audience-Message Matrix

For each target audience segment, define the core message:

| Audience Segment | Pain Point | Promise | Proof | CTA |
|-----------------|-----------|---------|-------|-----|
| [Segment 1] | [What frustrates them] | [What your product solves] | [Evidence] | [Specific action] |
| [Segment 2] | [Pain point] | [Promise] | [Proof] | [CTA] |

### 1.3 Creative Concept Development

Develop minimum 3 creative concepts per campaign:

| Concept | Approach | Hook Strategy | Duration | Rationale |
|---------|----------|--------------|----------|-----------|
| Concept A | Problem-solution | Lead with pain point | 15-30s | Direct response, clear value prop |
| Concept B | Social proof | Lead with testimonial/result | 15-30s | Trust building, relatable |
| Concept C | Pattern interrupt | Unexpected visual/statement | 6-15s | Attention capture, brand recall |

**Quality Gate 1:**
- [ ] Creative concepts aligned to campaign objective
- [ ] Audience-message matrix completed for all segments
- [ ] Competitive analysis shows differentiation
- [ ] Platform-specific requirements documented

## Phase 2: Script and Storyboard (Week 2)

### 2.1 Ad Script Framework

**The 3-Second Rule:** 65% of viewers who watch the first 3 seconds will watch 10+ seconds. The first 3 seconds must:
- Visually identify the brand (logo, product, distinctive color — NOT verbal)
- Establish relevance to the viewer
- Create a reason to keep watching

**Script Structure by Duration:**

**6-Second Bumper:**
```
[0-2s] Visual hook + brand presence
[2-4s] Single benefit statement (text + visual)
[4-6s] CTA + logo lockup
```

**15-Second Ad:**
```
[0-3s] Hook: Pain point or attention-grabbing statement
[3-10s] Solution: Product/service demonstration or benefit
[10-13s] Proof: Testimonial snippet, stat, or result
[13-15s] CTA: Clear, single action + logo
```

**30-Second Ad:**
```
[0-3s] Hook: Emotional or curiosity-driven opening
[3-10s] Problem: Establish the pain point with empathy
[10-20s] Solution: Demonstrate how the product solves the problem
[20-25s] Proof: Social proof, results, or testimonial
[25-28s] CTA: Specific action with urgency
[28-30s] Brand: Logo lockup + tagline
```

**60-Second+ Ad (YouTube TrueView, CTV):**
```
[0-5s] Hook: Story opening, provocative question, or visual spectacle
[5-15s] Problem: Empathetic exploration of the pain point
[15-35s] Solution: Detailed demonstration, features, benefits
[35-45s] Proof: Customer testimonial, data, case study
[45-55s] CTA: Clear action with value reinforcement
[55-60s] Brand: Full brand moment with tagline
```

### 2.2 Storyboard

Create a visual storyboard for each concept:

| Frame | Timestamp | Visual | Audio/VO | Text Overlay | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|----------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | 0:00-0:03 | [Description] | [Dialogue/VO] | [On-screen text] | Hook frame |
| 2 | 0:03-0:08 | [Description] | [Dialogue/VO] | [On-screen text] | Problem |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

### 2.3 Sound-Off Version Planning

Every ad must work without sound. Plan the visual-only narrative:
- Text overlays carry the key message points
- Visual demonstration replaces verbal explanation
- Music/VO enhances but does not carry meaning
- Subtitles burned in for all spoken content

**Quality Gate 2:**
- [ ] Scripts approved for all concepts and durations
- [ ] Storyboards visualize the viewer experience
- [ ] Sound-off effectiveness validated (cover audio, read the ad — does it work?)
- [ ] Platform-specific format requirements met

## Phase 3: Production (Weeks 3-4)

### 3.1 Production Approach

**Modular Shooting:**
Shoot for maximum flexibility and testability:
- Record 3+ different hooks for the same ad (test which performs best)
- Record 2+ different CTAs
- Record product demonstration from multiple angles
- Capture testimonial content that can be used as standalone or within larger ad
- Shoot in highest resolution for cropping to multiple aspect ratios

**Platform-Specific Production:**

| Requirement | YouTube | Meta | TikTok | LinkedIn | CTV |
|------------|---------|------|--------|----------|-----|
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 (primary), 9:16 (Shorts) | 1:1, 4:5, 9:16 | 9:16 | 1:1, 16:9 | 16:9 |
| Resolution | 1080p minimum | 1080p minimum | 1080p | 1080p | 1080p-4K |
| Max duration | No limit (TrueView), 6s (Bumper), 15-20s (Non-skip) | 240s | 60s (recommended) | 30s (recommended) | 15-30s standard |
| Safe zones | Standard | 14% top/bottom for UI elements | 20% top, 25% bottom | Standard | Standard |
| Text limits | None | 20% text rule relaxed but minimal text preferred | None | None | Minimal |

### 3.2 UGC-Style Production

For platforms where polished ads underperform (TikTok, Instagram Reels):
- Use smartphone-quality footage (or simulate it)
- Natural lighting, real environments
- Direct-to-camera delivery, conversational tone
- Text overlays using native platform fonts
- No logo until final frame

**Quality Gate 3:**
- [ ] All footage captured for all concepts
- [ ] Multiple hooks and CTAs recorded for testing
- [ ] Platform-specific versions edited
- [ ] Sound-off versions verified
- [ ] Brand compliance checked

## Phase 4: Testing and Optimization (Weeks 5+)

### 4.1 Creative Testing Framework

**What to Test (Priority Order):**

| Test Element | Impact on Performance | Test Method |
|-------------|----------------------|------------|
| Hook (first 3s) | Highest — determines whether ad is watched | A/B test different hooks |
| CTA | High — determines conversion | A/B test CTA copy and placement |
| Concept | High — determines resonance | A/B test different creative concepts |
| Length | Medium — affects completion and cost | Test 6s vs. 15s vs. 30s |
| Talent | Medium — affects trust and relatability | Test different presenters |
| Music/sound | Low-Medium | Test with/without, different tracks |

**Testing Protocol:**
- Test one variable at a time for clean results
- Minimum 1,000 impressions per variant before evaluating
- Run tests for minimum 3-7 days to account for daily variation
- Use platform-native A/B testing where available (Meta dynamic creative, YouTube video experiments)
- Statistical significance: 95% confidence before declaring winner

### 4.2 Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Awareness | Consideration | Conversion |
|--------|-----------|--------------|------------|
| VTR (15s+) | >20% | >25% | >30% |
| CTR | >0.5% | >1.0% | >2.0% |
| CPV | <$0.05 | <$0.08 | <$0.12 |
| CPA | N/A | <$50 | <$[target] |
| ROAS | N/A | N/A | >3x |
| Brand lift | >5% awareness lift | >3% consideration lift | N/A |

### 4.3 Optimization Cadence

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Day 1-3 | Monitor delivery, check for policy rejection, verify tracking |
| Day 3-7 | First performance read; pause underperformers, scale top performers |
| Week 2 | Introduce winning hook from A/B test; test new CTAs |
| Week 3-4 | Creative refresh: new concepts to combat fatigue |
| Monthly | Full creative refresh cycle; retire fatigued ads |

### 4.4 Creative Fatigue Management

Signs of creative fatigue:
- CTR declining >15% from peak
- CPV/CPA increasing >20% from peak
- Frequency >3-4 (audience seeing ad too many times)
- Engagement rate declining

Response: Rotate in new creative; do not simply increase budget on fatiguing ads.

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | Prevention |
|-------------|-----------|
| Ad looks like an ad (immediately skipped) | UGC-style production; lead with value, not brand |
| No clear CTA | Every ad must have one specific action; test CTA prominence |
| Tracking not set up | Verify pixels and conversion events BEFORE launching spend |
| Testing too many variables simultaneously | One variable per test; sequential testing |
| Scaling spend on fatigued creative | Monitor creative fatigue signals; maintain creative pipeline |
| Over-investing in production, under-investing in media | Maintain 3:1 minimum media-to-production ratio |
| Ignoring platform differences | Produce platform-native versions; what works on YouTube fails on TikTok |

## Post-Pattern Actions

1. Document winning creative concepts and learnings in `Memory/README.md`
2. Build a "swipe file" of top-performing hooks, CTAs, and concepts
3. Feed learnings into next creative cycle
4. Share performance data with Marketing Brain for campaign optimization
5. Archive ad creative with performance data for future reference
