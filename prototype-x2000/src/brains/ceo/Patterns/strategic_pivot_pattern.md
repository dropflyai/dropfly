# Strategic Pivot Pattern -- Changing Company Direction

## Situation

This pattern applies when the company needs to fundamentally change its
strategic direction -- changing the target customer, the product, the
business model, or the market. A pivot is not a small iteration; it is a
significant change in strategy based on evidence that the current direction
will not succeed.

Pivots are among the most consequential decisions a CEO makes. They are
Type 1 (one-way door) decisions that affect every person, every product,
and every plan in the organization. This pattern ensures pivots are
deliberate, data-driven, and well-executed.

---

## Prerequisites

Before executing this pattern:
- Evidence exists that the current direction is failing (see decision criteria)
- The CEO Brain has classified this as a potential pivot (not just an iteration)
- Sufficient runway exists to execute the pivot (minimum 9-12 months)
- The CEO is emotionally ready to abandon the current direction

---

## Pivot Decision Criteria

### When to Consider a Pivot

```
PIVOT SIGNAL CHECKLIST:
- [ ] Product-market fit metrics are flat or declining after 6+ months
- [ ] Customer retention curves go to zero (no long-term retention)
- [ ] CAC is increasing with no path to improvement
- [ ] Core hypothesis has been disproven by data
- [ ] Market has shifted (regulation, competition, technology)
- [ ] Team morale is declining due to lack of progress
- [ ] Adjacent opportunity shows stronger signals than core

SCORE: If 3+ items are checked, a pivot should be evaluated.
       If 5+ items are checked, a pivot is likely necessary.
```

### Types of Pivots Available

| Pivot Type | What Changes | What Stays | Example |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| Customer | Target customer | Core product | Dating app to video platform |
| Problem | Problem solved | Customer segment | Check-in app to photo sharing |
| Solution | Product/approach | Problem and customer | Software to services |
| Channel | Distribution method | Product and customer | Direct to partnership |
| Revenue model | How you charge | Product and customer | Licensed to SaaS |
| Technology | Tech platform | Solution concept | Desktop to mobile |
| Feature zoom-in | Feature becomes product | Domain expertise | Game chat to Slack |
| Feature zoom-out | Product becomes feature | Core capability | Standalone to platform |

## Approach: Five Phases

### Phase 1: Assessment (Weeks 1-2)

```
STEP 1: Data Collection
  Brain: Analytics Brain
  Action: Compile all relevant metrics, cohort data, trend analysis
  Output: Comprehensive data package showing current trajectory
  Gate: Data is complete, accurate, and unambiguous

STEP 2: Root Cause Analysis
  Brain: CEO Brain + MBA Brain
  Action: Why is the current direction failing? 5 Whys analysis.
  Output: Root cause document identifying the core issue
  Gate: Root cause is identified with evidence

STEP 3: Customer Insight Refresh
  Brain: Research Brain + Product Brain
  Action: 20+ customer conversations focused on "why are we failing?"
  Output: Customer insight report with verbatim quotes
  Gate: Clear understanding of customer perspective

STEP 4: Pivot Options Generation
  Brain: CEO Brain + MBA Brain + Product Brain
  Action: Generate 3-5 pivot options based on evidence
  Output: Pivot options document with pros/cons/evidence for each
  Gate: At least 3 viable options identified
```

**Quality Gate:** Pivot Assessment Review
- Data clearly shows current direction is failing
- Root cause is understood
- Customer perspective is incorporated
- Multiple pivot options are available
- CEO Brain decision: PIVOT (choose direction) / PERSEVERE / KILL

### Phase 2: Validation (Weeks 2-4)

```
STEP 5: Lightweight Testing
  Brain: Product Brain + Design Brain
  Action: Rapid prototypes or MVPs for top 1-2 pivot options
  Output: Testable artifacts for customer validation
  Gate: Artifacts are ready for customer testing

STEP 6: Customer Validation
  Brain: Research Brain + Product Brain
  Action: Test pivot options with 20+ potential customers
  Output: Validation data for each option
  Gate: At least one option shows strong signal

STEP 7: Business Model Validation
  Brain: MBA Brain + Finance Brain
  Action: Financial model for the pivot direction
  Output: Unit economics, runway analysis, growth projections
  Gate: Business model is viable and fundable
```

**Quality Gate:** Validation Review
- At least one pivot option validated with customers
- Business model is viable
- Team can execute this pivot with existing resources
- CEO Brain decision: COMMIT TO PIVOT / ITERATE / KILL

### Phase 3: Planning (Weeks 4-6)

```
STEP 8: Pivot Strategy
  Brain: CEO Brain + MBA Brain
  Action: Full strategic plan for the new direction
  Output: Strategic plan including positioning, differentiation, GTM
  Gate: Strategy is clear, differentiated, and executable

STEP 9: Product Roadmap
  Brain: Product Brain
  Action: New product roadmap aligned with pivot direction
  Output: PRD for pivot MVP, 12-month roadmap
  Gate: Roadmap is achievable with current team

STEP 10: Resource Reallocation
  Brain: CEO Brain + Finance Brain + HR Brain
  Action: Reallocate budget, headcount, and attention
  Output: Updated budget, hiring plan, organizational changes
  Gate: Resources are sufficient for the pivot

STEP 11: Communication Plan
  Brain: CEO Brain + Marketing Brain
  Action: Plan communication to all stakeholders
  Output: Communication plan with messaging for each audience
  Gate: All stakeholder communications drafted
```

**Quality Gate:** Pivot Plan Review
- Strategy is complete and aligned
- Product roadmap is achievable
- Resources are allocated
- Communications are planned
- CEO Brain decision: EXECUTE PIVOT

### Phase 4: Execution (Weeks 6-18)

```
STEP 12: Internal Communication
  Brain: CEO Brain
  Action: Announce the pivot to the team (all-hands)
  Output: Team is aligned and committed
  Template: Why we are pivoting, what is changing, what is not,
            how we support everyone through the transition

STEP 13: External Communication
  Brain: CEO Brain + Marketing Brain
  Action: Communicate to customers, investors, partners as appropriate
  Output: External stakeholders are informed

STEP 14: Product Development
  Brain: Engineering Brain + Design Brain + Product Brain
  Action: Build the pivot MVP using business_building_pattern.md
  Output: Working pivot product

STEP 15: Go-to-Market
  Brain: Marketing Brain + Sales Brain + Content Brain
  Action: Launch the pivoted product
  Output: Pivot product in market with initial customers
```

### Phase 5: Evaluation (Weeks 18-24)

```
STEP 16: Pivot Metrics Review
  Brain: Analytics Brain + CEO Brain
  Action: Assess pivot success against predefined criteria
  Output: Pivot scorecard

  SUCCESS CRITERIA:
  - [ ] Customer engagement is higher than pre-pivot
  - [ ] Retention curves are improving
  - [ ] Unit economics are positive or trending positive
  - [ ] Growth rate is accelerating
  - [ ] Team morale has improved

STEP 17: Decision
  Brain: CEO Brain
  Action: Evaluate results against kill criteria
  Output: CONTINUE / ITERATE / PIVOT AGAIN / KILL
```

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Description | Prevention |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Emotional attachment | Refusing to pivot because of sunk cost | Define kill criteria before starting |
| Pivot without data | Pivoting based on gut, not evidence | Mandate data in Phase 1 |
| Partial pivot | Trying to do both old and new | Commit fully; half-pivots fail |
| No communication | Team does not understand why | All-hands announcement is mandatory |
| Same mistake | Pivoting into another failing direction | Validate before committing |
| Pivot fatigue | Pivoting too often, team loses faith | Maximum 2 pivots; after that, reassess |

## Communication Guide

### To Employees

"We have been working hard on [current direction], and I am proud of what
we have built. But the data is telling us something important: [evidence].
After careful analysis, we have decided to pivot to [new direction]. Here
is why this is the right move, what changes, and how we support everyone
through this transition."

### To Investors

"Based on [evidence], we are pivoting from [current] to [new direction].
This is supported by [validation data]. We believe this direction has
[specific advantages]. Our runway supports this pivot, and we expect to
reach [milestone] by [date]."

### To Customers

Depends on customer impact. If customers are affected, communicate directly
with empathy and support. If the pivot adds value, frame it as an expansion.

---

**The strategic pivot pattern ensures that direction changes are deliberate,
data-driven, well-communicated, and well-executed. The CEO Brain never
pivots on instinct alone -- it pivots on evidence, plans thoroughly, and
communicates relentlessly.**
