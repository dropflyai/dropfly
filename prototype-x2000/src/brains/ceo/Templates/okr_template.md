# OKR Template -- Objectives and Key Results

## Instructions

This template is used by the CEO Brain to define, track, and score OKRs
at the company, brain, and individual levels. Complete this template
quarterly. Score at end of quarter.

## OKR Quality Checklist

Before finalizing OKRs, verify:

### Objectives
- [ ] Are qualitative and inspirational (not metrics)
- [ ] Are time-bound (this quarter)
- [ ] Are achievable but ambitious
- [ ] Connect clearly to strategy
- [ ] Maximum 5 at company level

### Key Results
- [ ] Are quantitative and measurable
- [ ] Have a clear baseline (where we are now)
- [ ] Have a clear target (where we want to be)
- [ ] Are outcome-based (not activity-based)
- [ ] Maximum 5 per objective (3 is ideal)

### Alignment
- [ ] Every brain OKR supports at least one company OKR
- [ ] No orphaned brain OKRs
- [ ] Dependencies between brains are identified
- [ ] No conflicting OKRs between brains

## End-of-Quarter Scoring

### Scoring Guide

```
0.0: No progress
0.1-0.3: Some progress, but well short of target
0.4-0.6: Significant progress, fell short of target
0.7-0.9: Near or at target
1.0: Exceeded target

TARGET AVERAGE: 0.6-0.7 (ambitious goals with strong execution)
```

## Common OKR Mistakes

| Mistake | Example | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Activity as KR | "Launch 3 features" | "Increase feature adoption to 40%" |
| Unmeasurable KR | "Improve customer satisfaction" | "Achieve NPS of 50+" |
| Too many OKRs | 8 objectives, 30 KRs | Max 5 objectives, 3-5 KRs each |
| Not ambitious | Goals easily achieved | Score should average 0.6-0.7 |
| No baseline | Target without starting point | Always record baseline |
| No alignment | Brain OKR disconnected from company | Every brain OKR supports a company OKR |
| No review | Set and forget | Weekly check, monthly review, quarterly scoring |
