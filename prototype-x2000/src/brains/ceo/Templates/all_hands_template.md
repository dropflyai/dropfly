# All-Hands Meeting Template

## Instructions

This template is used by the CEO Brain to design and deliver monthly
all-hands meetings. The all-hands is the CEO's highest-leverage
communication event -- one hour reaches the entire organization. Use it
wisely. The goal is alignment, transparency, and engagement, not a
one-way broadcast.

---

## Pre-Meeting Preparation

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Identify key messages for this month (3 maximum)
- [ ] Select team spotlight (rotate monthly across functions)
- [ ] Brief the spotlight team on expectations (10 min, informal)
- [ ] Collect pre-submitted questions (anonymous channel)

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Draft talking points for each segment
- [ ] Review metrics and prepare honest assessment
- [ ] Prepare answers for anticipated difficult questions
- [ ] Rehearse (especially if delivering bad news)
- [ ] Send calendar invite with agenda to all employees

### Day Of
- [ ] Test technology (audio, video, slides, Q&A tool)
- [ ] Arrive early / log in early
- [ ] Energy check: be genuinely present and engaged

## Agenda

### Opening: Connection (5 minutes)

```
PURPOSE: Set the tone. Create human connection.

OPTIONS (rotate monthly):
- Customer story: "Let me tell you about [customer name] and how
  our product changed their [specific outcome]..."
- Team shoutout: "Before we start, I want to recognize [person/team]
  for [specific achievement and why it matters]..."
- Personal reflection: "Something I have been thinking about this
  month is [genuine reflection relevant to the team]..."
- Fun moment: "Quick poll: [light question that gets people engaged]"

GOAL: Within the first 2 minutes, every person should feel that this
meeting is worth their time and that the CEO is genuinely present.
```

### Segment 1: State of the Company (15 minutes)

```
PURPOSE: Honest, transparent assessment of where we are.

STRUCTURE:
1. KEY METRICS (5 minutes)
   Show 3-5 metrics that matter most:
   +--------------------------------------------------+
   | Metric          | This Month | Last Month | Trend |
   +--------------------------------------------------+
   | Revenue (MRR)   | $[X]       | $[X]       | [^v]  |
   | Active Users    | [X]        | [X]        | [^v]  |
   | NPS             | [X]        | [X]        | [^v]  |
   | Team Size       | [X]        | [X]        | [^v]  |
   | [Custom metric] | [X]        | [X]        | [^v]  |
   +--------------------------------------------------+

   For each metric: one sentence on what it means and why.
   DO NOT cherry-pick only good numbers. Show the full picture.

2. WHAT IS WORKING (5 minutes)
   - [Win 1]: What happened, who made it happen, why it matters
   - [Win 2]: What happened, who made it happen, why it matters
   - [Win 3]: What happened, who made it happen, why it matters

   Be specific. Name people. Connect wins to strategy.

3. WHAT IS NOT WORKING (5 minutes)
   - [Challenge 1]: What is happening, what we are doing about it
   - [Challenge 2]: What is happening, what we are doing about it

   Be honest. Do not sugarcoat. Show that leadership sees the problems
   and is addressing them. This builds trust more than any win.
```

### Segment 2: Strategic Update (10 minutes)

```
PURPOSE: Reinforce where we are going and why it matters.

STRUCTURE:
1. VISION REMINDER (2 minutes)
   - One sentence on our vision
   - Why it matters (connect to something current and real)
   - This section is a ritual. Repeat it every month. Repetition
     creates internalization.

2. STRATEGY UPDATE (5 minutes)
   - Are we on strategy? [Yes/Adjusted]
   - If adjusted: what changed and why
   - If not adjusted: what is NOT changing (stability anchor)
   - What to focus on this month

3. LOOKING AHEAD (3 minutes)
   - What is coming next month / quarter
   - Any changes employees should know about
   - Any events, milestones, or deadlines to be aware of
```

### Segment 3: Team Spotlight (10 minutes)

```
PURPOSE: Build cross-functional understanding and appreciation.

FORMAT: A team presents their recent work to the whole company.

STRUCTURE:
- What the team does (1 minute -- for context)
- What they accomplished recently (3 minutes)
- How it connects to company goals (2 minutes)
- Demo or visual (if applicable) (2 minutes)
- What they need from other teams (1 minute)
- One thing they are proud of (1 minute)

ROTATION: Different team each month
  Month 1: Engineering
  Month 2: Design
  Month 3: Sales/Marketing
  Month 4: Customer Success/Support
  Month 5: Product
  Month 6: Operations/Finance
  (repeat)

RULES:
- Keep it informal and real (not a polished presentation)
- Show actual work (demos > slides)
- Celebrate the team, not just the work
```

### Segment 4: Q&A (20 minutes)

```
PURPOSE: The most important segment. This is where trust is built or
destroyed.

FORMAT:
1. Start with pre-submitted questions (anonymous, collected beforehand)
2. Open to live questions
3. CEO answers directly and honestly

RULES FOR THE CEO:
- Answer honestly. "I do not know" is a valid answer. Follow up within
  48 hours with an answer.
- Do not dodge. If the question is uncomfortable, answer it anyway.
  Say: "That is a hard question. Here is my honest answer..."
- Do not get defensive. Thank people for asking tough questions.
- If a question requires a private conversation: "That is important.
  Let us talk after this meeting."
- Do not let one person dominate. "Great question. Let me take a few
  more to make sure everyone gets heard."

WHAT TO DO IF NO ONE ASKS QUESTIONS:
- Use pre-submitted questions (this is why you collect them)
- Ask yourself a hard question: "A question I wish someone would ask
  me is..."
- Ask the audience: "What are you most worried about right now?"
- Check the anonymous channel: "I see some great questions here..."

COMMON QUESTION TYPES AND HOW TO HANDLE:
+--------------------------------------------------+
| Question Type        | Response Approach          |
+--------------------------------------------------+
| About layoffs/jobs   | Honest. If no plans, say so|
|                      | directly. If yes, see crisis|
|                      | management.                |
| About compensation   | Acknowledge importance.    |
|                      | Share what you can. Offer  |
|                      | to follow up.              |
| About strategy       | Explain the reasoning. Show|
|                      | you have thought about it. |
| About competitors    | Acknowledge them. Focus on |
|                      | our strengths and customer.|
| About leadership     | Be accountable. Own        |
|                      | mistakes. Share learnings. |
| About culture        | Listen. Validate concerns. |
|                      | Commit to specific actions.|
+--------------------------------------------------+
```

### Closing (5 minutes)

```
PURPOSE: Leave the team energized and aligned.

STRUCTURE:
1. Summarize key messages (3 bullet points maximum)
2. Call to action: "This month, I am asking everyone to focus on..."
3. Gratitude: "Thank you for [specific thing the team did well]"
4. Forward-looking: "I am excited about [genuine thing to look
   forward to]"

TONE: Authentic, energizing, grateful. Not corporate. Not scripted.
The close should feel like a conversation, not a speech.
```

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up

### Within 24 Hours
- [ ] Send meeting recording to all employees
- [ ] Send written summary of key points and announcements
- [ ] Follow up on any questions that were not fully answered
- [ ] Thank the spotlight team

### Within 1 Week
- [ ] Track action items from Q&A
- [ ] Begin preparing for next month's all-hands
- [ ] Collect feedback on all-hands format (quarterly)

### Warning Signs
- Attendance dropping below 70% -- meeting is not valuable enough
- No live questions -- psychological safety is low
- Same people asking all questions -- broader engagement needed
- Feedback scores declining -- format needs refreshing

## All-Hands Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Fix |
|-------------|------------|-----|
| Broadcast only | CEO talks for 55 min, 5 min Q&A | Minimum 20 min Q&A (33% of time) |
| Good news only | Only share wins, hide challenges | Always include "what is not working" |
| Too long | Meeting runs 90+ minutes | Hard cap at 60 minutes |
| Too frequent | Weekly all-hands | Monthly is optimal for most companies |
| No follow-up | Questions go unanswered | Written follow-up within 48 hours |
| Scripted | CEO reads from script, no energy | Talking points only, speak naturally |
| No spotlight | Same people present every time | Rotate teams monthly |

## Adapting for Company Size

| Size | Format | Frequency | Q&A Approach |
|------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| 1-15 | Informal, around a table | Weekly standup, monthly deep dive | Open discussion |
| 15-50 | Semi-formal, single room | Monthly | Live Q&A |
| 50-150 | Formal, large room or video | Monthly | Live + pre-submitted |
| 150-500 | Large-format, hybrid | Monthly | Pre-submitted + moderated live |
| 500+ | Broadcast with regional followups | Monthly + regional | Pre-submitted + regional Q&A |
