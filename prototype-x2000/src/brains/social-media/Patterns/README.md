# Social Media Brain Patterns

## Purpose

Patterns are reusable, battle-tested playbooks for common social media operations. Each pattern provides a complete, executable workflow for a specific scenario. Patterns encode institutional knowledge so that recurring situations are handled consistently and effectively, regardless of which team member executes.

---

## Pattern Philosophy

1. **Patterns are prescriptive**: They tell you exactly what to do, in what order, with what tools
2. **Patterns are adaptable**: They provide a framework that can be customized to specific contexts
3. **Patterns are evidence-based**: Every step is grounded in documented best practices and theoretical foundations
4. **Patterns are living documents**: They evolve based on execution experience and performance data

---

## Available Patterns

### 1. Social Launch Pattern

**File**: `social_launch_pattern.md`

**Use When**: Launching a new product, feature, brand, or campaign on social media. Covers the complete lifecycle from pre-launch planning through launch execution to post-launch optimization.

**Key Phases**:
- Pre-launch (4-6 weeks before): Strategy, content production, influencer alignment
- Launch week: Coordinated content deployment, real-time engagement, paid amplification
- Post-launch (2-4 weeks after): Performance analysis, content optimization, sustained momentum

---

### 2. Crisis Response Pattern

**File**: `crisis_response_pattern.md`

**Use When**: A social media crisis is detected or escalating. Provides minute-by-minute protocol for the first 24 hours, followed by multi-day recovery procedures.

**Key Phases**:
- Detection and classification (0-15 minutes)
- Initial response (15-60 minutes)
- Substantive response (1-4 hours)
- Ongoing management (4-48 hours)
- Recovery and post-mortem (48 hours+)

---

### 3. Influencer Campaign Pattern

**File**: `influencer_campaign_pattern.md`

**Use When**: Planning and executing an influencer marketing campaign. Covers the full campaign lifecycle from strategy through influencer selection, execution, and ROI measurement.

**Key Phases**:
- Strategy and planning (4-6 weeks before)
- Influencer identification and outreach (3-4 weeks before)
- Contracting and onboarding (2-3 weeks before)
- Content creation and review (1-2 weeks before)
- Campaign execution and monitoring
- Analysis and relationship management (post-campaign)

---

## How to Use Patterns

1. **Identify the scenario** that matches your current need
2. **Read the entire pattern** before beginning execution
3. **Customize** timeline, platforms, and specifics to your context
4. **Execute** step-by-step, checking off each phase
5. **Document** deviations and outcomes for pattern improvement
6. **Contribute** learnings back to update the pattern

---

## Pattern Contribution Guidelines

When creating or updating patterns:

- Base all recommendations on evidence (cite theoretical foundations or performance data)
- Include specific tools, templates, and resource references
- Provide realistic timelines with buffer for unexpected complications
- Include decision points where customization is needed
- Document anti-patterns (what NOT to do) alongside best practices
- Include metrics for measuring pattern execution success
