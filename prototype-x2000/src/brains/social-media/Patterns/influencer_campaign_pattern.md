# Pattern: Influencer Marketing Campaign

## Pattern ID

`INFLUENCER-CAMPAIGN-001`

## Classification

Campaign execution pattern for planning, executing, and measuring influencer marketing campaigns. Covers the complete lifecycle from strategy through creator selection, content production, campaign activation, and ROI analysis.

---

## When to Use This Pattern

- Launching an influencer marketing campaign for a product, brand, or initiative
- Executing a seasonal or event-based influencer activation
- Building a new influencer program from scratch
- Running a UGC generation campaign through creators
- Activating a product seeding or gifting campaign

---

## Prerequisites

Before initiating this pattern, ensure:

- [ ] Campaign objectives are defined and aligned with business goals
- [ ] Budget is allocated (creator fees, product costs, paid amplification, tools)
- [ ] Target audience is documented with platform-specific behavioral insights
- [ ] Brand guidelines and messaging framework are available
- [ ] Legal/compliance team is available for contract and FTC review
- [ ] Influencer management tool or tracking system is operational
- [ ] Tracking infrastructure (unique links, discount codes, pixels) is ready

## Phase 1: Strategy and Planning (T-6 to T-4 Weeks)

### 1.1 Campaign Strategy Document

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Campaign name | Internal identifier and any public-facing campaign name |
| Business objective | What business outcome will this campaign drive? |
| Campaign KPIs | 3-5 measurable KPIs (EMV, CPA, engagement, reach, conversions) |
| Target audience | Personas, platforms, demographics, psychographics |
| Campaign concept | Creative direction, key messages, brand angle |
| Platform strategy | Which platforms, content formats, posting cadence |
| Creator strategy | Tier mix, number of creators, selection criteria |
| Budget breakdown | Creator fees, product, paid amplification, tools, contingency |
| Timeline | Full campaign timeline with milestones |
| Measurement plan | How each KPI will be tracked and reported |

### 1.2 Budget Allocation Template

| Line Item | Percentage | Amount | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Creator fees | 50-60% | $X | Per-creator negotiated rates |
| Product/Gifting | 10-15% | $X | Product samples, shipping, packaging |
| Paid amplification | 15-25% | $X | Spark Ads, whitelisting, boosting top content |
| Tools/Platform | 5-10% | $X | Influencer platform, tracking tools |
| Contingency | 5-10% | $X | Unexpected costs, additional creators, rush fees |
| **Total** | **100%** | **$X** | |

### 1.3 Creator Selection Criteria

Define weighted criteria specific to this campaign:

| Criterion | Weight | Minimum Threshold |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Audience demographic match | 25% | >60% overlap with target audience |
| Engagement rate | 20% | >3% (micro), >2% (mid-tier), >1% (macro) |
| Content quality | 20% | Consistent quality aligned with brand aesthetic |
| Brand/Category alignment | 15% | Organic interest or existing use of product category |
| Audience authenticity | 10% | <15% suspicious followers (per verification tool) |
| Past brand partnership performance | 10% | Positive track record if available |

## Phase 2: Influencer Identification and Outreach (T-4 to T-3 Weeks)

### 2.1 Discovery and Shortlisting

| Step | Action | Tool | Output |
|------|--------|------|--------|
| 1 | Generate initial list (50-100 potential creators) | CreatorIQ, Modash, manual search | Long list |
| 2 | Score against selection criteria | Spreadsheet + verification tool | Scored list |
| 3 | Review top 30 content and audience deeply | Manual review, HypeAuditor | Qualified list |
| 4 | Check for competitor partnerships | Manual review, ad library | Conflict-free list |
| 5 | Prioritize and rank top 20 | Team review | Final shortlist |
| 6 | Identify 10 backup creators | From qualified list | Backup list |

### 2.2 Outreach Execution

| Day | Action | Details |
|-----|--------|---------|
| Day 1 | Send outreach to top 10 priority creators | Personalized email per outreach framework |
| Day 2 | Follow up with DM to supplement email | Brief, friendly DM referencing email |
| Day 3-4 | Send outreach to next 10 creators | Same process |
| Day 5 | First follow-up to non-respondents (batch 1) | Gentle follow-up email |
| Day 7 | Assess response rates, activate backup list if needed | Fill any gaps from backup creators |
| Day 7-10 | Negotiate terms with interested creators | Per negotiation framework |

| Item | Agreed | Notes |

## Phase 4: Content Creation and Review (T-2 to T-1 Week)

### 4.1 Content Creation Period

| Action | Owner | Timeline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Creators produce content | Creators | T-2 weeks to T-10 days |
| Creators submit drafts for review | Creators | T-10 days (draft deadline) |
| Brand reviews all submissions | Social media manager | T-10 to T-8 days |
| Feedback delivered to creators | Social media coordinator | T-8 days |
| Creators revise (if needed) | Creators | T-8 to T-5 days |
| Final approval on all content | Social media manager | T-5 days |

### 4.2 Content Review Criteria

| Criterion | Check |
|-----------|-------|
| Brand safety | No inappropriate content, competitors, or controversial elements |
| Message accuracy | Key messages communicated accurately |
| Disclosure compliance | FTC-compliant disclosure prominently placed |
| Tracking elements | Discount code/link mentioned, hashtag included |
| Production quality | Video/image quality meets minimum standards |
| Brand alignment | Tone, aesthetic, and messaging align with brand |
| Platform optimization | Content format optimized for intended platform |
| Creator authenticity | Content feels authentic to creator's voice (not overly scripted) |

### 4.3 Content Feedback Best Practices

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| Be specific about required changes | Give vague feedback ("make it better") |
| Distinguish between mandatory and suggested changes | Treat all feedback as mandatory |
| Respect creator's creative voice | Rewrite their caption word-for-word |
| Provide feedback in writing (documented) | Give verbal-only feedback |
| Be timely (within 48 hours) | Let drafts sit without response |
| Explain the "why" behind feedback | Issue directives without context |

## Phase 5: Campaign Execution (Launch Period)

### 5.1 Posting Coordination

| Action | Owner | Details |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Confirm posting schedule with all creators | Social media coordinator | 48 hours before launch |
| Send posting reminders | Social media coordinator | Day before and morning of |
| Monitor posts going live | Social media coordinator | Real-time verification |
| Verify disclosure compliance on live posts | Social media manager | Within 1 hour of posting |
| Engage with creator posts (brand comment, like) | Community manager | Within 30 minutes of posting |
| Share/repost top content to brand channels | Social media coordinator | Within 24 hours |

### 5.2 Real-Time Monitoring

| Metric | Monitor | Action Trigger |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Creator post engagement | Platform analytics, influencer tool | High performer: prepare Spark Ad/boost |
| Comment sentiment | Manual review | Negative sentiment: address per protocol |
| Tracking link clicks | UTM analytics, Bitly | Low clicks: adjust CTA or link placement |
| Discount code usage | E-commerce analytics | High usage: verify inventory, celebrate |
| Brand safety | Manual review + tools | Issue detected: contact creator immediately |

### 5.3 Paid Amplification

| Action | Timing | Criteria |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Identify top organic performers | 24-48 hours after posting | Engagement rate >2x average |
| Request Spark Ad/whitelisting authorization | 24 hours after posting | From top 3-5 performers |
| Launch paid campaigns on creator content | 48-72 hours after posting | Targeting: lookalike, interest, retargeting |
| Optimize paid campaigns | Daily | Reallocate to top performers |
| Scale successful campaigns | T+7 days | ROAS above threshold |

### 6.3 Campaign ROI Calculation

```
Total Investment:
  Creator fees:           $X
  Product costs:          $X
  Paid amplification:     $X
  Tools/platform:         $X
  Management time:        $X (estimated)
  ─────────────────────────
  Total:                  $X

Total Value:
  Direct revenue:         $X (tracked conversions)
  EMV:                    $X (earned media value)
  Content assets:         $X (production cost equivalent)
  New followers:          X (at $X per follower equivalent)
  ─────────────────────────
  Total:                  $X

Campaign ROI = (Total Value - Total Investment) / Total Investment x 100
```

## Phase 7: Relationship Management (Post-Campaign)

### 7.1 Creator Follow-Up

| Action | Timeline | Details |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Thank you message | Within 48 hours of campaign end | Personal message from brand contact |
| Payment processing | Per contract terms | Ensure timely payment (critical for relationship) |
| Share performance results | Within 2 weeks | Creators appreciate seeing their impact |
| Feedback request | Within 2 weeks | Ask creators for their feedback on the partnership |
| Document for future | Within 2 weeks | Log creator details, performance, relationship notes |

### 7.2 Creator Tiering Post-Campaign

| Classification | Criteria | Next Step |
|---------------|----------|-----------|
| Star performer | >2x average performance, great to work with | Move to ambassador consideration, priority for next campaign |
| Solid performer | Meets expectations, reliable | Include in future campaigns, maintain relationship |
| Underperformer | Below average results despite fair opportunity | One more chance with adjusted brief, or part ways |
| Problem creator | Missed deadlines, non-compliant, difficult | Do not re-engage |

### 7.3 Long-Term Relationship Nurturing

| Activity | Frequency | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Engage with creator content (likes, comments) | Ongoing | Maintain relationship visibility |
| Send new product samples | Per product launch | Keep creator connected to brand |
| Include in exclusive events | Quarterly | Deepen relationship |
| Share relevant industry content | Monthly | Provide value outside of campaigns |
| Birthday/milestone acknowledgment | As appropriate | Personal touch |
| Referral request | Post-campaign | Leverage creator network for new partnerships |

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Campaign ROI | >200% (3x return) | Total value / Total investment |
| Average creator engagement rate | >3% | Total engagements / Total reach |
| Content delivered on time | >90% | On-time / Total deliverables |
| Discount code / link conversions | Per campaign target | E-commerce tracking |
| EMV generated | >3x creator fees | EMV calculation |
| New followers gained | Per campaign target | Net new followers during campaign |
| Brand sentiment | Positive or neutral shift | Social listening sentiment analysis |
| Creator satisfaction | >8/10 average | Post-campaign creator survey |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Prevention |
|-------------|------|------------|
| Selecting on follower count alone | Low engagement, wasted budget | Weighted selection criteria including engagement and authenticity |
| Over-scripting content | Inauthentic, low-performing content | Creative brief with key messages, not scripts |
| No tracking infrastructure | Cannot measure ROI | Unique codes + UTMs for every creator |
| Late product shipping | Rushed content, missed deadlines | Ship products minimum 2 weeks before content due |
| Skipping content review | Brand safety risk, off-message content | Mandatory review with documented criteria |
| No paid amplification | Under-leveraging best content | Budget allocated for amplifying top performers |
| Ignoring post-campaign relationship | One-time partnerships, no compounding value | Systematic relationship nurturing protocol |
| Single metric ROI assessment | Incomplete picture of campaign value | Multi-metric framework (EMV + revenue + content + reach) |

---

*Influencer campaigns succeed through preparation, genuine partnerships, and rigorous measurement. Shortcuts at any phase compromise the entire campaign. Execute every phase completely, treat creators as partners, and let data guide your decisions.*
