# Pitch Deck — Template

## Purpose

This template structures a fundraising pitch deck for venture capital or institutional investor presentations. The deck should be 10-15 slides, designed to tell a compelling story in 20-30 minutes, with enough substance to survive detailed investor scrutiny. The goal of the deck is not to close the deal — it is to earn the next meeting. Sequoia Capital's framework remains the gold standard: Problem, Solution, Why Now, Market, Product, Traction, Team, Business Model, Competition, Financials, The Ask.

---

## Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Title Slide

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Company name | [Name] |
| Tagline | [One sentence describing what you do] |
| Logo | [Company logo] |
| Presenter(s) | [Name(s) and title(s)] |
| Date | [Month Year] |
| Confidentiality | "Confidential — Do Not Distribute" |

---

### Slide 2: Problem

**Objective:** Make the investor feel the pain of the problem you solve.

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|---------|
| Problem statement | [One sentence: What problem exists?] |
| Who has the problem | [Target customer with specificity] |
| Current pain | [How do they suffer today? Quantify the cost of the problem] |
| Scale of problem | [How many people/companies have this problem?] |
| Evidence | [Customer quotes, data points, personal experience] |

**Framework:** "Today, [target customer] struggles with [problem], which costs them [quantified impact]. The current solutions — [alternatives] — fail because [specific failure reasons]."

---

### Slide 3: Solution

**Objective:** Demonstrate your solution with clarity and simplicity.

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|---------|
| What you do | [One sentence, jargon-free] |
| How it works | [3-4 bullet points or a simple diagram] |
| Key differentiators | [What makes your approach unique?] |
| Product visual | [Screenshot, demo GIF, or product image] |

**Rule:** If a smart 12-year-old cannot understand your solution from this slide, simplify.

---

### Slide 4: Why Now

**Objective:** Explain why this company can succeed now when it could not have succeeded before.

| Element | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| Technology shift | [New technology enables this (AI, cloud, mobile, etc.)] |
| Market shift | [Regulatory change, behavior change, pandemic effect] |
| Economic shift | [Cost curves, pricing disruption, new business model viable] |
| Cultural shift | [Generational change, remote work, sustainability demand] |

**Framework:** "[X] has changed, creating an opportunity that did not exist [Y] years ago. Specifically: [1-3 concrete shifts]."

---

### Slide 5: Market Size

**Objective:** Demonstrate the market is large enough to build a venture-scale business.

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| TAM (Total Addressable Market) | [$] | [Source] |
| SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) | [$] | [Calculation methodology] |
| SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | [$] | [Realistic near-term capture] |

**Bottom-Up TAM Calculation (Preferred):**
```
# of target customers × Annual revenue per customer = TAM
[#] × [$] = $[X]B
```

**Market Growth Rate:** [X]% CAGR, growing from $[Y]B (2024) to $[Z]B (2030)

**Rule:** Bottom-up TAM is more credible than top-down. "The global AI market is $500B" tells the investor nothing about your business.

---

### Slide 6: Product / Demo

**Objective:** Show, don't tell. The product should speak for itself.

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|---------|
| Product screenshots | [3-4 key screens or interfaces] |
| User workflow | [How a customer actually uses the product] |
| Key features | [3-5 features that drive the most value] |
| Demo offer | [Offer to do a live demo in the next meeting] |

**Alternative:** If the product is highly visual, replace this slide with a 60-second recorded demo.

---

### Slide 7: Traction / Metrics

**Objective:** Prove that the market wants what you are building.

| Metric | Value | Growth Rate |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| ARR/MRR | [$] | [% MoM or YoY] |
| Customers | [#] | [Growth rate] |
| Revenue growth | [%] | [MoM or YoY] |
| Net Revenue Retention | [%] | [Trend] |
| Key engagement metric | [#] | [Trend] |
| Pipeline | [$] | [Current qualified pipeline] |

**Stage-Appropriate Traction:**

| Stage | What to Show |
|-------|-------------|
| Pre-Seed | Problem validation (interviews, waitlist, LOIs) |
| Seed | Early customers, engagement metrics, initial revenue |
| Series A | Revenue growth, retention, unit economics |
| Series B+ | Scale metrics, efficiency metrics, market leadership indicators |

---

### Slide 8: Business Model

**Objective:** Explain how the company makes money and the path to profitability.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Revenue model | [SaaS subscription, marketplace take rate, transactional, etc.] |
| Pricing | [Price points by tier; ACV by segment] |
| Unit economics | [CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period] |
| Gross margin | [Current and target] |
| Path to profitability | [When? What milestones trigger profitability?] |

---

### Slide 9: Go-to-Market Strategy

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Primary GTM motion | [Sales-led, product-led, channel, enterprise] |
| Customer acquisition channels | [Top 2-3 channels by efficiency] |
| Sales cycle | [Average time from first touch to close] |
| Expansion strategy | [How customers grow: upsell, cross-sell, seat expansion] |

---

### Slide 10: Competition

**Objective:** Demonstrate you understand the competitive landscape and have a defensible position.

**Competition Matrix:**

| Capability | You | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|-----------|-----|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| [Key capability 1] | [Strong] | [Weak] | [Strong] | [Moderate] |
| [Key capability 2] | [Strong] | [Moderate] | [Weak] | [Weak] |
| [Key capability 3] | [Strong] | [None] | [Moderate] | [None] |

**Defensibility / Moat:**
- [Network effects, data advantages, switching costs, brand, IP, regulatory, etc.]

**Rule:** Never say "we have no competition." Every company has competition, even if it is the status quo (spreadsheets, manual processes, doing nothing).

---

### Slide 11: Team

**Objective:** Demonstrate that this team is uniquely qualified to solve this problem.

| Person | Role | Relevant Background |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| [Name] | CEO/Co-founder | [Relevant experience: domain expertise, prior exits, technical depth] |
| [Name] | CTO/Co-founder | [Relevant experience] |
| [Name] | [Key hire] | [Relevant experience] |

**Key Hires Planned:**
| Role | Timeline | Impact |
|------|----------|--------|
| [Role] | [Q1 2025] | [What this hire enables] |

**Advisors / Board (if noteworthy):**
- [Name — relevant expertise or brand-name recognition]

---

### Slide 12: Financial Projections

| Metric | Current | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|--------|---------|--------|--------|--------|
| Revenue | [$] | [$] | [$] | [$] |
| Growth rate | [%] | [%] | [%] | [%] |
| Gross margin | [%] | [%] | [%] | [%] |
| Operating expense | [$] | [$] | [$] | [$] |
| Net income / (loss) | [$] | [$] | [$] | [$] |
| Headcount | [#] | [#] | [#] | [#] |

**Rule:** Projections should be ambitious but defensible. If asked "how did you arrive at $X in Year 3?", you should have a bottom-up model behind every number.

---

### Slide 13: The Ask

**Objective:** State clearly what you are raising, what you will do with it, and what milestones you will reach.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Raise amount | [$X million] |
| Use of funds | [Hiring: X%, Product: X%, GTM: X%, G&A: X%] |
| Milestones this round enables | [Reach $XM ARR, launch product Y, enter market Z] |
| Timeline to next round | [18-24 months to Series [X]] |

---

### Slide 14: Appendix (Optional)

Additional slides available for Q&A:
- Detailed financial model
- Customer case studies
- Product roadmap
- Cohort analysis
- Cap table summary
- Market research data

## Pitch Deck Design Principles

| Principle | Guidance |
|-----------|---------|
| Simplicity | One idea per slide. If you need to explain the slide, it is too complex |
| Visual | Use charts, images, and diagrams over text. Maximum 30 words per slide |
| Narrative | The deck tells a story: problem → solution → proof → team → future |
| Credibility | Every claim backed by data, customer evidence, or verifiable source |
| Consistency | Brand-consistent design (logo, colors, typography from brand guidelines) |
| Readability | Works when read without presenter (investors will share and read alone) |

---

## Usage Notes

- Adapt slide count and emphasis for stage (pre-seed may not have revenue; Series B should lead with metrics)
- Practice the pitch until you can deliver it in 20 minutes with 10 minutes for Q&A
- The deck is a conversation starter, not a comprehensive document (that is the data room)
- Update the deck continuously as metrics and strategy evolve
- Archive each version with the date for historical reference
