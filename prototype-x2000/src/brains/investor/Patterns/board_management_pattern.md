# Board Management Pattern — Effective Board Governance and Relationships

## Context

This pattern governs the ongoing management of the board of directors relationship. Effective board management is a strategic capability, not an administrative task. A well-managed board provides counsel, connections, and credibility. A poorly managed board becomes a governance burden, a source of conflict, and a constraint on execution. This pattern applies from the formation of the first formal board through IPO readiness.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Board of directors formally constituted (even if just founders)
- [ ] Board composition documented (seats, rights, observer seats)
- [ ] Corporate bylaws adopted and current
- [ ] Board meeting cadence established (quarterly minimum for VC-backed companies)
- [ ] D&O insurance in place
- [ ] Board portal or document management system established

---

## Phase 1: Board Composition Design

### Board Structure by Stage

| Stage | Typical Board Size | Composition | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|-------|
| Pre-Seed/Seed | 1-3 | 1-2 founders + 0-1 investor/advisor | Minimal governance overhead |
| Series A | 3-5 | 2 founders + 1 investor + 0-2 independent | Standard initial board |
| Series B | 5 | 2 common + 2 investor + 1 independent | Balanced governance |
| Series C+ | 5-7 | 2 common + 2-3 investor + 2 independent | Independent majority emerging |
| Pre-IPO | 7-9 | Management + investors + majority independent | Exchange listing requirements |

### Board Seat Governance

| Seat Type | Elected By | Represents | Typical Holder |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Common director | Common stockholders | Founders, employees | CEO, co-founder |
| Preferred director | Preferred stockholders (by series) | Investor interests | Lead investor partner |
| Independent director | Board consensus or mutual agreement | Objective governance | Industry expert, operator |
| Observer (non-voting) | Investor right (contractual) | Secondary investor visibility | Non-lead investor partner |

### Selecting Independent Directors

Independent directors add enormous value when chosen well:

**Selection Criteria:**
- Relevant domain expertise (industry, function, or operational stage)
- No financial relationship with the company or investors
- Willingness to commit real time (not just a resume line)
- Complementary skills to existing board members
- Network value (customers, partners, hires, future investors)
- Prior board experience (public or private company)

## Phase 2: Board Meeting Operations

### Meeting Cadence

| Company Stage | Cadence | Duration | Format |
|-------------|---------|----------|--------|
| Pre-Seed to Seed | Quarterly or as needed | 1-1.5 hours | Virtual acceptable |
| Series A-B | Quarterly | 2-3 hours | In-person preferred (1-2/year minimum) |
| Series C+ | Quarterly | 3-4 hours | In-person preferred |
| Pre-IPO | Monthly to quarterly | 4+ hours | In-person for key meetings |

### Board Meeting Agenda Template

```
1. Call to Order and Approval of Minutes (5 min)

2. CEO Report (15-20 min)
   - Key highlights and lowlights since last meeting
   - Strategic priorities update
   - Organizational update (key hires, departures)

3. Financial Review — CFO (15-20 min)
   - P&L review vs. plan and prior period
   - Cash position, burn rate, runway
   - Key financial metrics and KPIs
   - Forecast update

4. Strategic Discussion Topic (30-45 min)
   - Pre-identified strategic topic requiring board input
   - E.g., market expansion, M&A opportunity, pricing change, fundraising strategy
   - Structured as decision or advice-seeking, not information dump

5. Functional Deep-Dive (15-20 min, rotating)
   - Product / Engineering / Sales / Marketing (rotate quarterly)
   - Present one function in depth, inviting functional leader

6. Board Action Items (10 min)
   - Formal votes required (option grants, budget approval, etc.)
   - Consent items

7. Executive Session (15-20 min)
   - Board meets without management present
   - Provides space for candid discussion about CEO and team performance

8. Closed Session / Adjournment
```

### Board Deck Standards

**The 48-Hour Rule:** Board materials must be distributed at least 48 hours before the meeting. Board members who receive materials during the meeting cannot provide thoughtful counsel.

**Board Deck Structure:**
| Section | Pages | Content |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Executive summary | 1-2 | Key metrics dashboard, highlights, lowlights |
| Financial overview | 3-5 | P&L, cash flow, burn, runway, budget variance |
| KPI dashboard | 1-2 | Key operating metrics with trend lines |
| Strategic update | 2-3 | Progress against strategic priorities |
| Product/roadmap | 1-2 | Key releases, customer feedback, roadmap |
| Go-to-market | 1-2 | Pipeline, sales metrics, marketing performance |
| People | 1 | Headcount, key hires, turnover, org chart |
| Strategic discussion | 3-5 | Background, options, recommendation for the strategic topic |
| Appendix | Variable | Detailed data for reference (not presented, available for questions) |

**Board Deck Anti-Patterns:**
- 50+ page decks that cannot be read in 48 hours
- No financials or vague financials
- All good news, no bad news (loses credibility)
- Presenting the deck slide-by-slide (board members have read it; discuss, don't present)
- No strategic discussion topic (board meetings become status updates)

## Phase 3: Between-Meeting Communication

### Investor Updates

Monthly or quarterly investor updates maintain trust and transparency:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Highlights | 3-5 most important wins |
| Lowlights | 1-3 challenges or misses (honesty builds trust) |
| Key metrics | ARR/MRR, growth rate, burn, runway, customers, pipeline |
| Asks | Specific requests: intros, advice, hires |
| Upcoming milestones | What you are working toward next |

**Update Best Practices:**
- Consistent format each time (investors appreciate predictability)
- Send on a regular schedule (first Tuesday of the month, every month)
- Be honest about challenges (surprises are the fastest way to destroy board trust)
- Include specific asks (investors want to help but need to know how)

### 1:1 Board Member Check-Ins

| Cadence | Format | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Monthly | 30-min call or coffee | Relationship building, early flag raising, advice seeking |
| Ad hoc | As needed | Time-sensitive issues requiring board input |

### When to Contact the Board Between Meetings

**Proactive Communication Required:**
- Material revenue miss (>20% below plan)
- Cash runway drops below 6 months unexpectedly
- Key executive departure
- Legal threat or regulatory issue
- M&A inbound interest
- Material customer loss
- Security breach or data incident

**The No-Surprise Rule:** Board members should NEVER learn material information for the first time in a board meeting. If something important happens, communicate within 48 hours.

## Phase 4: Board Effectiveness

### Evaluating Board Performance

Annual board effectiveness assessment:

| Dimension | Assessment Questions | Score (1-5) |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Strategic contribution | Does the board provide valuable strategic counsel? | [ ] |
| Operational support | Do board members actively help with intros, hires, deals? | [ ] |
| Governance rigor | Are fiduciary duties taken seriously? | [ ] |
| Meeting quality | Are meetings productive and well-structured? | [ ] |
| Candor | Is there honest dialogue, including constructive criticism? | [ ] |
| Dynamics | Do board members work well together? | [ ] |
| Time commitment | Do board members invest adequate time? | [ ] |
| Diversity of perspective | Does the board bring varied viewpoints? | [ ] |

### Managing Difficult Board Dynamics

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Board member is disengaged (misses meetings, unresponsive) | 1:1 conversation; if persistent, discuss transition off board |
| Board member is micro-managing | Reset boundaries; clarify board vs. management roles |
| Board members disagree on strategy | Facilitate structured discussion; CEO recommends, board advises |
| Board member has conflict of interest | Require recusal from relevant discussions and votes |
| Board information is leaking | Address directly; tighten information sharing; review NDA |

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Board meetings are status updates (no strategic value) | Structure agenda around one strategic discussion topic |
| Materials arrive day-of (no preparation time) | Enforce 48-hour rule; automate distribution |
| CEO surprised by board concerns | Regular 1:1s with each board member between meetings |
| Board has no independent voices | Add independent directors by Series B at latest |
| Board never meets in executive session | Schedule executive session into every meeting agenda |
| Minutes not maintained | Designate corporate secretary; approve minutes at each meeting |

---

## Post-Pattern Actions

1. After each board meeting: distribute minutes within 5 business days
2. Track action items from board meetings to completion
3. Conduct annual board effectiveness review
4. Update board composition as company stage evolves
5. Maintain board portal with all corporate governance documents
6. Log board management learnings in `Memory/README.md`
