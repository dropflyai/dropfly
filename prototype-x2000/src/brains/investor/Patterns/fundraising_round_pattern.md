# Fundraising Round Pattern — Executing Equity Financing

## Context

This pattern governs the complete lifecycle of an equity fundraising round, from strategic decision through close. It applies to priced rounds (Seed, Series A, B, C, and beyond). SAFE/convertible note raises follow a simplified version of this pattern. The pattern is designed to maximize the probability of a successful raise while preserving founder leverage and minimizing distraction from operating the business.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Business fundamentals justify fundraising (growth rate, market opportunity, capital needs)
- [ ] Financial model updated with 18-36 month projections
- [ ] Board alignment on fundraising decision, timing, and target terms
- [ ] Legal counsel engaged (preferred: startup-specialized firm)
- [ ] Key metrics documented and auditable
- [ ] Founder(s) available to dedicate 30-50% of time for 2-4 months

---

## Phase 1: Preparation (Weeks 1-4)

### 1.1 Strategic Foundation

**Fundraising narrative:** Answer these questions before approaching any investor:
- Why are you raising now? (Specific milestone or opportunity, not "we're running out of money")
- How much are you raising? (Bottom-up calculation from hiring plan and milestones)
- What will you accomplish with this capital? (Specific, measurable milestones)
- Why will you win? (Differentiation, moat, unfair advantages)
- What is the exit path? (How does the investor get a return?)

**Target raise sizing:**
```
Raise Amount = Monthly Burn Rate * Months of Runway Desired
             + Planned Investment Increases
             + Buffer (15-20%)

Rule of thumb: Raise 18-24 months of runway
```

### 1.2 Materials Preparation

| Material | Purpose | Template |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Pitch deck | Investor meetings (10-15 slides) | pitch_deck_template.md |
| Data room | Due diligence documentation | data_room_checklist_template.md |
| Financial model | Projections and scenario analysis | [Finance Brain] |
| Investor update | Recent performance summary | investor_update_template.md |
| One-pager | Pre-meeting teaser for warm intros | 1-page company summary |
| Cap table | Current and pro-forma ownership | [Cap table tool] |

### 1.3 Target Investor List

Build a prioritized list of 40-80 potential investors:

| Tier | Criteria | Target Count | Approach |
|------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Tier 1 | Perfect fit: stage, sector, thesis, check size | 10-15 | Warm intro, personalized outreach |
| Tier 2 | Good fit: most criteria match | 15-25 | Warm intro preferred, cold outreach acceptable |
| Tier 3 | Possible fit: some criteria match | 15-30 | Batch outreach, lower conversion expected |

**Quality Gate 1:**
- [ ] All materials reviewed by advisor or experienced founder
- [ ] Financial model stress-tested with realistic and conservative scenarios
- [ ] Investor list researched (recent investments, partner interests, portfolio conflicts)
- [ ] Data room prepared with all anticipated due diligence materials
- [ ] Practice pitch delivered to 3+ trusted advisors with feedback incorporated

## Phase 2: Outreach (Weeks 5-8)

### 2.1 Creating Urgency Through Process

**The fundraising paradox:** The best way to raise money is to create the impression that you do not urgently need it. Compressed timelines and parallel processes create urgency.

**Outreach Strategy:**
- Launch all Tier 1 meetings within a 2-week window (not sequentially)
- Use warm introductions wherever possible (3x higher response rate)
- Brief intro email + one-pager → First meeting request
- Track all outreach in CRM (Affinity, Streak, Notion)

### 2.2 Meeting Cadence

| Meeting Type | Duration | Objective | Typical Count |
|-------------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Intro/screening | 30 min | Mutual fit assessment | 30-50 |
| Partner meeting | 60 min | Deep dive with decision-maker | 15-25 |
| Full partnership | 60-90 min | Present to investment committee | 5-10 |
| Due diligence | Multiple | Technical, financial, customer diligence | 2-5 |
| Term sheet | N/A | Receive and evaluate offer | 1-3 |

### 2.3 Pipeline Management

Track every investor through the funnel:

```
Contacted → First Meeting → Partner Meeting → Partnership → DD → Term Sheet → Close
   50          30              15              8          4        2          1
```

**Weekly Review:**
- How many investors are at each stage?
- Which investors need follow-up materials?
- Which investors have passed? (Document reasons for pattern recognition)
- Are we generating enough top-of-funnel activity?

## Phase 3: Negotiation and Close (Weeks 9-14)

### 3.1 Term Sheet Evaluation

When term sheets arrive, evaluate using the term_sheet_analysis_template.md:

**Key Economic Terms:**
- Valuation (pre-money)
- Investment amount
- Option pool (size, pre/post)
- Liquidation preference (1x non-participating is standard)
- Anti-dilution (broad-based weighted average is standard)
- Participation rights (none preferred; capped acceptable)

**Key Control Terms:**
- Board composition (how many seats? Who gets them?)
- Protective provisions (what requires investor approval?)
- Drag-along rights
- Information rights
- Pro-rata rights

### 3.2 Negotiation Principles

- **Leverage comes from alternatives.** Multiple term sheets give you negotiating power
- **Optimize for the right partner, not the highest valuation.** A great board member at $50M pre is better than a passive investor at $60M pre
- **Most terms are negotiable.** Do not accept the first offer as final
- **Legal counsel reviews every term sheet.** Founders should not negotiate legal terms directly

### 3.3 Closing Process

| Step | Owner | Timeline |
|------|-------|----------|
| Sign term sheet | Founder + Counsel | Day 0 |
| Due diligence completion | Investor + Company | 2-4 weeks |
| Definitive documents drafted | Counsel (both sides) | 2-3 weeks (parallel with DD) |
| Document negotiation | Counsel | 1-2 weeks |
| Board approval | Board | At signing |
| Signing and initial close | All parties | Day 30-45 |
| Wire transfer | Investor | 1-3 business days post-signing |
| Post-close filings | Counsel | 30 days post-close |

**Quality Gate 3:**
- [ ] Term sheet signed with acceptable terms
- [ ] Due diligence completed without material issues
- [ ] Legal documents reviewed and negotiated
- [ ] Board approved the financing
- [ ] Wire received

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Raising too early (no traction to justify) | Define specific milestones before starting |
| Sequential outreach (takes too long, no urgency) | Launch all Tier 1 meetings in a 2-week window |
| Underprepared for due diligence | Data room ready BEFORE first meeting |
| Optimizing for valuation over partner quality | Evaluate investor value-add, not just price |
| Founder burns out from extended raise | Target 10-12 week process; have a plan B if it extends |
| Accepting bad terms under pressure | Know your walkaway terms before entering negotiation |

---

## Post-Pattern Actions

1. Send thank-you notes to all investors who engaged (even those who passed)
2. Update cap table with new round
3. Issue investor update within 30 days of close
4. Establish board meeting cadence with new board member
5. Document fundraising learnings in `Memory/README.md`
6. Begin executing the plan outlined in the pitch
