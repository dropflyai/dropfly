# Market Research Framework — Authoritative

You can't position without understanding the market.
Market research reveals where you fit and where you can win.

---

## Purpose

Market research exists to:
- understand market opportunity size
- identify underserved segments
- inform positioning and messaging
- guide feature prioritization
- reveal go-to-market considerations

Design influences conversion. Market research tells you who to convert.

## Research Queries for Market Research

### Market Sizing
```
"[Industry] market size"
"[Product category] market size"
"[Industry] TAM SAM SOM"
"[Product category] market forecast"
"how many companies use [product type]"
```

### Competitive Intelligence
```
"[Product category] market share"
"[Competitor] revenue funding"
"[Product category] competitive landscape"
"alternatives to [competitor]"
"[Competitor] pricing plans"
```

### Positioning
```
"[Product category] comparison"
"[Competitor] vs [competitor]"
"best [product category] for [use case]"
"[Product category] for [segment]"
```

### Go-to-Market
```
"[Product category] marketing channels"
"how [competitor] acquires customers"
"[Industry] buying process"
"[Product category] sales cycle"
```

## Market Sizing Methods

### Top-Down
```
Start with total market, narrow down.

Total industry spend: $X billion
× Relevant segment: Y%
× Geographic focus: Z%
= TAM estimate
```

### Bottom-Up
```
Start with unit economics, build up.

Number of target companies: X
× % that could use solution: Y%
× Average deal size: $Z
= TAM estimate
```

### Value-Based
```
Calculate value created for customers.

Number of target users: X
× Problem frequency: Y times/year
× Value of solving: $Z
= TAM estimate
```
